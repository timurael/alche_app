# Cognitive Longevity & Neuroprotection

## Overview
Targeting the preservation of cognitive function, mood regulation, and neurogenesis. Designed to support focus during the day and protect against age-related cognitive decline.

## 1. Hericium erinaceus (Lion's Mane Mushroom)
**Category:** Nootropic / Neurogenesis
**Primary Health Benefits:** Improvement of mild cognitive impairment, acute focus and mood enhancement, potential stimulation of Nerve Growth Factor (NGF).
**Active Compounds:** Hericenones and Erinacines.

### Clinical Literature & Peer-Reviewed Backing
*   **DOI: 10.3390/nu15224842** - "The Acute and Chronic Effects of Lion's Mane Mushroom Supplementation on Cognitive Function, Stress and Mood in Young Adults: A Double-Blind, Parallel Groups, Pilot Study." (Nutrients, 2023)
*   **DOI: 10.1002/ptr.2634** - "Improving effects of the mushroom Yamabushitake (Hericium erinaceus) on mild cognitive impairment: a double-blind placebo-controlled clinical trial." (Phytotherapy Research, 2009)
*   **DOI: 10.3389/fnut.2025.1405796** - "Acute effects of a standardised extract of Hericium erinaceus (Lion's Mane mushroom) on cognition and mood in healthy younger adults." (Frontiers in Nutrition, 2025)

**Contraindications:** Generally well-tolerated. Potential minor gastrointestinal discomfort. Caution in individuals with mushroom allergies or bleeding disorders.
**Optimal Scientifically Backed Dosage:** Typically 1000mg to 3000mg of fruiting body/mycelium extract daily, divided into doses. Requires specific standardization of active hericenones for acute effects.

**[Alche EFSA Compliance Note]:** Under EU Regulation 2015/2283, the *fruiting body* of Hericium erinaceus has a history of consumption and is NOT a novel food (approved for use). However, the *mycelium* IS classified as a Novel Food. Therefore, Alche potions must strictly source 100% fruiting body extracts to remain legally compliant in the European market.
