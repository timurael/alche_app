# Systemic Inflammation & Recovery

## Overview
Targeting the reduction of key inflammatory biomarkers (like hs-CRP and IL-6) caused by modern lifestyle stressors (including alcohol consumption, poor sleep, and intense exercise).

## 1. Curcumin (Turmeric Extract)
**Category:** Powerful Anti-inflammatory / Antioxidant
**Primary Health Benefits:** Down-regulation of inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6), reduction of C-reactive protein (CRP), relief from joint arthritis and systemic inflammation.
**Active Compounds:** Curcuminoids (primarily curcumin).

### Clinical Literature & Peer-Reviewed Backing
*   Systematic reviews and meta-analyses show strong statistical significance for reducing CRP levels, specifically when using formulations like "C3 complex with Bioperine" or "nano curcumin" to enhance bioavailability.
*   Randomized controlled trials demonstrate curcumin supplementation significantly lowers post-trial concentrations of pro-inflammatory IL-6 and MCP-1.
*   Curcumin modulates numerous signaling molecules, including NF-κB (a primary driver of inflammation).

**Contraindications:** Blood-thinning medications. High doses can cause GI distress.
**Optimal Scientifically Backed Dosage:** 500mg - 1500mg daily. **Crucial:** Must be paired with Piperine (black pepper extract) or formulated in a liposomal/phytosome delivery system to achieve meaningful bioavailability.

## 2. Tart Cherry Extract (Prunus cerasus)
**Category:** Antioxidant / Muscle Recovery
**Primary Health Benefits:** Reduction of uric acid, enhanced muscle recovery, lowered CRP. Contains natural melatonin for sleep cycle regulation.
**Active Compounds:** Anthocyanins.

### Clinical Literature & Peer-Reviewed Backing
*   **NCT02922920** (ClinicalTrials.gov) - A 12-week randomized-controlled trial noting a significant increase in DNA repair activity and lowered CRP levels in older adults.
*   **DOI: 10.1039/c8fo01492b** - Pilot study showing 100% tart cherry juice reduced biomarkers of inflammation (ESR, MCP-1, TNF-alpha) in subjects.

**Contraindications:** High in natural sugars (if using juice form). Extracts are preferred for low-calorie potion formulation.
**Optimal Scientifically Backed Dosage:** ~480mg of cherry powder extract equivalent to 1-2 servings of tart cherry juice, taken before bed or post-stressor.
