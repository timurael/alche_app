Subscriber Metrics Block

The very first block in the P&L — before any revenue — shows the subscriber funnel each month: new subs added, churned subs, and total active subscribers broken out by tier. This block feeds directly into subscription revenue rows below.

Three rows + three tier sub-rows. New Subs Added pulls the monthly rate from the Growth Curves sheet (itself driven by Assumptions B14–B19). Churned Subs applies the 8% monthly churn rate to the prior month's total. Total Subscribers = prior total − churn + new. The three tier rows (Core 52%, Pro 38%, Premium 10%) split the total by the tier mix from Assumptions B9–B11. At M24: ~339 total subscribers.

Churn — 8% of active subscribers cancel each month (~1 in 12). Tier mix — the split between Core (EUR 19), Pro (EUR 49), and Premium (EUR 99) plans. Feeds into — subscription revenue rows further down the P&L.
How the P&L is Structured

The P&L does not contain hardcoded numbers — every revenue and cost cell is a formula referencing either the Assumptions sheet or the Growth Curves sheet. Change either of those and the entire P&L recalculates instantly.

The sheet is organized top to bottom: Subscriber Metrics → Income (all streams) → COGS → Gross Profit → Operating Expenses → EBITDA → CapEx → Depreciation/EBIT → Tax → Net P&L → Cumulative Cash → Key Metrics → Break-Even Indicators. The column header row (row 4) shows M1 through M24, plus a TOTAL column. Freeze panes are set at B5 so months stay visible when scrolling.

Formula-based — no hardcoded values in the P&L body; all numbers trace back to Assumptions or Growth Curves. Freeze panes — locks the row/column headers so they remain visible when scrolling through 24 months.

P&L Sheet — Income (4 of 4)5 / 32
Subscriptions — Monthly & Annual (EX-01)

Three tiers (Core EUR 19, Pro EUR 49, Premium EUR 99 per month) with annual options (EUR 190 / 490 / 990 per year). Revenue recognised monthly at the equivalent monthly rate — not as a lump sum when annual payment is received. Annual mix starts at 20% of new subscribers and grows to 30% by M12.

Each tier row uses a blended ARPU formula: (1 − annual_mix) × monthly_price + annual_mix × (annual_price ÷ 12). Annual mix phases: M1–M6 = 20% (Assumptions B131), M7–M11 = 25%, M12+ = 30% (B132). Tier split: Core 52%, Pro 38%, Premium 10% (Assumptions B9–B11). True blended subscription ARPU across all tiers and mixes = EUR 38.44 — not EUR 49. The subscription total row (Subscription MRR recognised) feeds into Key Metrics and Unit Economics.

Revenue recognition — annual payments are spread across 12 months in the P&L; the cash arrives upfront but revenue is recognised monthly (German HGB). EUR 38.44 ARPU — blended actual average; not the Pro tier midpoint. Annual mix — the share of new subscribers who pay annually vs. monthly.
[INFO] Annual Cash Collected — Not P&L Revenue

This greyed-out informational row shows how much actual cash is collected upfront from annual subscribers each month — which is larger than the P&L revenue recognised. It is NOT included in Total Income. It exists to help reconcile the P&L vs Cash Flow gap.

Annual cash collected = new subs × annual_mix × weighted annual price (EUR 190 × 52% + EUR 490 × 38% + EUR 990 × 10% = EUR 384 weighted average annual price). This cash arrives in the month of signup but is spread across 12 months of P&L revenue. The difference between this row and the subscription MRR row is one component of the Cash Flow sheet's "working capital" adjustment. Investors reviewing cash should note this row — it means cash position improves faster than P&L alone suggests when annual subscriptions are growing.

[INFO] row — greyed-out; for reference only; does NOT add to Total Income. EUR 384 weighted annual price — average annual subscription fee across the tier mix. Cash timing advantage — annual subscribers pay 12 months upfront; the P&L recognises 1/12 per month.

Refund Reserve + Bad Debt Provision + Stripe Processing

Three shared COGS items: 1.5% refund/returns reserve on physical product revenue, 0.5% bad debt provision on total revenue, and 2.9% Stripe processing fee on total revenue. Together these add ~4–5% to blended COGS in early months.

Refund reserve (B40: 1.5%) applies only to retail products, smoothies, and events — not to subscriptions, B2B, or affiliate commissions. These three items are part of TOTAL COGS and reduce Gross Profit accordingly.

1.5% refund reserve — provision for product returns and exchanges on physical goods. 0.5% bad debt — provision for invoices that go unpaid (B2B) or chargebacks (P5 persona finding). 2.9% Stripe fee — payment processor fee on all transactions; unavoidable across all revenue streams.
TOTAL COGS

Sum of all 11 COGS lines. Blended rate starts at ~45–55% of revenue in early operating months and compresses toward ~32% by M24 as zero-COGS streams (CGM, B2B, gift cards, subscriptions) grow their share.

The 11 COGS lines summed: retail product cost, retail waste, retail 3PL, LED (EUR 0), smoothie ingredients, events (venue + platform), doctor verification, AI API, refund reserve, bad debt provision, and Stripe processing. The downward trend in blended COGS % is a model health signal — it should compress over time. Blended COGS % — total COGS divided by total revenue; the weighted average cost rate across all streams. ~32% by M24 — reflects the growing share of zero-COGS streams (CGM, B2B, gift cards). Feeds into — Gross Profit = Total Income − Total COGS.
P&L Sheet — Gross Profit & Payroll9 / 32
Gross Profit & Gross Margin %

Gross Profit = Total Income − Total COGS. Gross Margin % = Gross Profit ÷ Total Income. Starts at 45–55% in early operating months, improves toward ~68% by M24. An upward trend here is the primary health indicator for unit economics.

The Gross Margin % row uses an IF formula to avoid division-by-zero in M1–M3 (no revenue). It is shown as a percentage. The improvement over time reflects the revenue mix shift: as zero/near-zero COGS streams (CGM, B2B, gift cards, subscriptions) grow their share relative to physical retail and smoothies, every EUR of revenue costs less to deliver. A flat or declining gross margin after M9 would indicate the physical streams are growing faster than planned — worth investigating in Growth Curves before assuming it is a problem.

Gross Profit — revenue remaining after direct delivery costs; before fixed costs (rent, staff, marketing). Gross Margin % — the fraction of each EUR of revenue that survives to cover fixed costs. 68% at M24 means EUR 0.68 of every EUR earned flows to gross profit.
