How to Present a Pre-Revenue P&L

Do

  • Label every number as a projection
  • Show assumptions explicitly
  • Include sensitivity analysis
  • Keep projections to 12–18 months
  • Use ranges, not point estimates

Don't

  • Show projections beyond 18 months
  • Claim €0 for things that cost money
  • Hide your biggest cost items
  • Show a "hockey stick" revenue curve
  • Present a "worst case" that's still profitable

Red Flags Investors Spot

Missing or Under-budgeted COGS
A subscription business showing 95%+ gross margin with no hosting, API, or payment processing costs. Every business has COGS — if yours aren't in the model, investors assume you didn't think about them.
No Founder Salaries
Two full-time founders, €0 in salary expense. Investors will model this in regardless. Show €3,800/mo per founder plus employer costs — it signals discipline, not weakness.
Tax on Losses
Showing tax expense when EBT is negative. You don't pay corporate tax on losses. Including tax on a loss signals the founder doesn't understand basic tax mechanics.
