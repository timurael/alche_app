# THE BERLIN ADMINISTRATOR (Sub-Agent 5)
**Focus:** Foundational OPEX, Salaries, Admin, Legal, and HR in Germany.

## 1. Pre-Seed Founder Salaries (Berlin)
- **Benchmark:** For a EUR 500K pre-seed round, VCs expect founders to live frugally but sustainably. In Berlin (2024/2025), a typical pre-seed founder gross salary ranges from **EUR 45,000 to EUR 60,000** annually. 
- **The "Alche" Reality:** The funds need to stretch for 24 months. Timu and Daria should aim for the lower end of this bracket to preserve runway. 
- **Recommended Baseline for P&L:** **EUR 50,000 / year (EUR 4,166 / month gross)** per founder.

## 2. Employer Social Security Contribution (Arbeitgeberanteil)
- **Benchmark (2024/2025):** In Germany, the employer must pay roughly ~21% on top of the gross salary for social security (Pension 9.3%, Unemployment 1.3%, Health ~8.5%, Care 1.8%). Factoring in mandatory accident insurance (Berufsgenossenschaft) and minor levies (U1, U2), the true multiplier is between **1.22 and 1.23**.
- **The "Alche" Reality:** If a founder earns EUR 4,166 gross, the company pays ~EUR 5,125 total.
- **Recommended Baseline for P&L:** **22% (Multiplier of 1.22)** on top of all gross salaries.

## 3. Steuerberater (Tax Advisor), Legal, & Insurance (Monthly)
- **Steuerberater (Accounting & Payroll):** For a young GmbH with low transaction volume initially, monthly external bookkeeping and payroll runs typically cost **EUR 200 - EUR 400**. The annual statement (Jahresabschluss) adds a EUR 1,500 - EUR 2,500 lump sum at year-end, which should be amortized or planned for.
- **Legal Retainer:** While large corporations pay EUR 1,500/month retainers, a pre-seed startup should NOT be on a monthly retainer. Legal costs should be ad-hoc (e.g., setting up the SAFE/Convertible note, T&Cs, practitioner contracts) and modeled as specific one-off OPEX hits, not a monthly recurring fee.
- **Insurance:** Basic operational liability insurance (Betriebshaftpflicht) is cheap, ~$30-$50/month.
- **Recommended Baseline for P&L:** **EUR 300 - EUR 400 / month** for standard accounting/payroll overhead.

## 4. Co-Working Costs (Berlin)
- **Benchmark:** Dedicated desks at standard Berlin co-working spaces (Betahaus, Factory, WeWork) range from EUR 250 to EUR 400 per person per month. A tiny 2-person private office starts around EUR 800 - EUR 1,200/month depending on the district (Mitte/Prenzlauer Berg vs. outskirts).
- **The "Alche" Reality:** Alche does not have its physical lounge yet. They just need desks.
- **Recommended Baseline for P&L:** **EUR 300 / person / month** (EUR 600 total for 2 founders).
