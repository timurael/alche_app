05 — The Solution
alche | Pre-Seed Investment Memorandum
The Solution
Four integrated layers, each creating demand for the next -- and each generating revenue.
The KDGB framework (Know-Do-Get-Belong) is a business process, not a brand concept. Each layer has a user action, a revenue lever, and a retention mechanism.
01
KNOW
User tracks biomarkers, wearable data, biological age. Longitudinal data creates personalization engine.
Revenue: Subscription (EUR 19-99/mo)
02
DO
User follows personalized protocols -- nutrition, recovery, movement. AI-guided, physician-informed.
Revenue: Service bookings, protocol access
03
GET
User buys protocol-linked supplements, functional products. Recommended by data, not generic catalog.
Revenue: Product margins (40-65%)
04
BELONG
User joins accountability circles, shared challenges, events. Community as longevity intervention.
Revenue: Events, premium community access
KNOW → DO → GET → BELONG → KNOW (repeat, each cycle stickier)
Layer User Action Revenue Lever Retention Mechanism Key Metric
KNOW Biomarker dashboard, wearable sync Subscription (EUR 19-99/mo) Longitudinal data accumulates; 6 months > snapshot Activation rate
DO Follow personalized protocols Service bookings, LED sessions Protocol calibration improves with data Session frequency
GET Purchase protocol-linked products Product margins (40-65%) Smart replenishment from usage data AOV, repeat rate
BELONG Circles, events, shared challenges Events, premium community Social bonds create switching cost Community NPS
Each step creates demand for the next. KNOW creates motivation to DO. DO creates need to GET. GET creates belonging through shared rituals. BELONG generates better data.
Why integration is the structural advantage
Single-vertical competitors own one layer. Oura owns wearable data. AG1 owns a supplement. Restore owns recovery. Nobody connects all four. Integration is structurally hard to bolt on because it requires different organizational DNA than single-vertical excellence. A competitor would need to rebuild from the ground up -- the same thing alche is doing, but without the greenfield advantage.
DATA TRUST: Only 8% of EU consumers share health data (BEUC 2024). GDPR-native architecture, transparent data policies, user-controlled sharing. In a market where trust is the scarcest resource, privacy-first design is a competitive advantage.

Defensibility
No moat exists at pre-seed. Here is our thesis for building one across three layers over 24 months.
At pre-seed, no moat exists yet. What investors evaluate is whether the model has the structural potential to build one. We present our thesis honestly.
Moat Layer Description Timeline Evidence It Works
Social Identity Brand, content, and community build identity. People do not leave brands they identify with -- this is structural, not aspirational. Content voice + community rituals + physical space create belonging. M0-12 Taste-dependent. Hardest to copy. Interview feedback confirms "longevity with intention" resonates at identity level.
Network Effects Each new member increases value for existing members. Accountability groups, shared challenges, event networks, protocol sharing. Value grows with community size. M6-18 Time-dependent. Impossible to fast-track. Every community platform shows this pattern once critical mass is reached.
Switching Costs 12 months of biomarker data, personalized protocols, supplement subscriptions, and community connections create real friction to leave. GDPR means data is portable -- but insights, calibrated protocols, and social graphs are not. M12+ Volume-dependent. Gets more valuable over time. Oura sees this with longitudinal sleep data; users who have 6+ months of data retain at 2x the rate.
AI Resilience Assessment
AI Threat Vulnerable Layer Resilient Layer
Apple Health + ChatGPT gives free longevity advice Pure data-and-insights layer (the KNOW layer) becomes commoditized over time Physical experiences (AI cannot give you a smoothie or community event), social identity, accountability
AI-generated personalized protocols Generic protocol generation becomes a commodity Physician-reviewed protocols, community accountability, protocol calibration from longitudinal user data
AI-curated product recommendations Product discovery becomes easier Trust layer (GDPR-native, physician-backed), community-validated products, subscription convenience
Integration is the structural thesis
None of alche's competitors combine biomarker data + personalized protocols + curated products + physical experiences + community in one platform. Each does one or two layers. Integration is structurally hard to bolt on because it requires different organizational DNA than single-vertical excellence. GDPR-native data trust gives a European entrant a structural advantage US platforms cannot retrofit. When the data layer gets cheaper and AI interpretation improves, the companies that built genuine community, cultural identity, and regulatory trust are the ones still standing.
