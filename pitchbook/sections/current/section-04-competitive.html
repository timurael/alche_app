"We studied $1.4B in failures so we could build differently."
What $1.4B Taught Us
Seven companies with better funding than us made specific, identifiable, avoidable mistakes. Three survivor patterns emerged:
01 — Asset-Light

ZOE, AG1, and Levels survived by curating and connecting -- not owning the value chain. Forward built $1M hardware pods and collapsed after burning $657M.

02 — Content-Led

AG1 built a $600M business on content and community, not clinical infrastructure. ZOE grew on Tim Spector's authority. Science is necessary but not sufficient -- distribution wins.

03 — Community-First

When GLP-1 drugs arrived, companies without community had no reason for users to stay. Social fabric is the only retention moat that survives technology disruption.

The Window

12-18 months before well-funded incumbents integrate European longevity protocols.

Oura has not localized beyond hardware retail. Hims EU expansion is nascent. Levels and Function Health are US-only. Neko Health (Spotify founder) focuses on scanning, not integrated wellness. The full-stack European longevity platform is wide open.
