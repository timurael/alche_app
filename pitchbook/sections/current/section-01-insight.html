The Longevity Paradox

People who want to age well have been forced to become full-time project managers of their own health.

The majority of consumers now rank healthy aging as their top priority. Yet the average health-conscious person manages eight disconnected apps, three supplement subscriptions, and zero integrated guidance.

Longevity is no longer inaccessible. It is overwhelming. Consumers are not struggling to find options -- they are struggling to decide what is worth trusting, combining, and repeating. The problem is not lack of supply. It is lack of intelligent selection.

People became project managers of their own health. Every abandoned Oura ring, cancelled CGM subscription, and unopened wellness app represents a person who cared enough to try and was handed a dashboard instead of direction. These are not lost users. They are our addressable market.

What $1.4B in failures taught us

The graveyard of preventive health startups holds a clear lesson: people do not pay to prevent disease they cannot feel. But they will pay to feel better today. We sell how you feel today, not disease prevention tomorrow.

We experienced this ourselves. Two founders spending hundreds of euros a month on disconnected tools, wearables, and supplements -- with no integrated view of whether any of it works. The first brand to structure this behavior will define the category.
