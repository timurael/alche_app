The window

The European longevity market is forming now.
No integrated player exists. That changes fast.

The global wellness economy reached $6.8 trillion in 2024 and is projected to grow 7.6% annually to $9.8T by 2029 (GWI 2025). It's larger than sports, tourism, IT, and 4x the pharmaceutical industry. Longevity capital more than doubled from 2023 to 2024, with $8.49B flowing into the sector.

Yet in Europe's largest economy -- Germany, a $281B wellness market -- no company integrates biomarker tracking, personalised protocols, curated products, and community into a single platform. US leaders (Function Health, Levels, Oura, Restore) haven't localised. The window is 18-24 months before that changes.

Converging right now

CGM wellness market growing 18%+ CAGR. Germany's 8.7M private insurance holders can partially reimburse prevention. 68% of Germans 18-35 pay out-of-pocket for preventive health. 33M Germans already using health apps. Berlin is continental Europe's #1 startup city.

Not here yet

Oura hasn't localised beyond hardware retail. Hims EU expansion is nascent. Levels and Function Health are US-only. Neko Health (Spotify founder, $260M Series B) focuses on scanning, not integrated wellness. The full-stack European longevity platform is wide open.

Germany: highest spend, mediocre outcomes

Germany spends nearly EUR 495 billion on healthcare annually (12.8% of GDP) -- the highest in the EU. But life expectancy trails Switzerland, Sweden, France, and Japan, all of which spend less. With 7.7 hospital beds per 1,000 people (OECD average: 4.2), the system was architecturally built to treat illness, not prevent it. Consumers are filling the gap themselves.

Europe is structurally different, not just behind

If Europe were simply "the US but 3 years later," US companies would eventually arrive and win. But the market is structurally different -- and that's the moat.

Structural advantages

Insurance covers prevention. PKV (8.73M holders) reimburses EUR 200-500/yr in wellness. GKV bonus programs offer EUR 100-300/yr. US consumers pay 100% out-of-pocket.

Centuries of Kur culture. European wellness isn't a Silicon Valley import. It's built on apothecary traditions and state-supported spa systems. Alche speaks to that lineage.

GDPR as trust differentiator. Health data sensitivity is higher in Europe. A GDPR-native platform is a feature, not a compliance cost.

Lower pricing works here. EUR 19-99/mo matches European spending norms. US players entering at $199/mo will feel overpriced.

Hidden distribution channels

PKV opportunity. 8.73M full private insurance + 29M supplementary coverage. PKV requires EUR 69,300/yr income to qualify -- this IS our demographic. ~70% of tariffs cover alternative medicine.

Corporate wellness (B2B). EUR 600 per employee per year tax-free for health promotion (Sec. 3 Nr. 34 EStG). With 90-100K startup employees in Berlin alone, corporate partnerships are a meaningful channel.

Berlin specifically. 3.878M population, 800K aged 25-45, 30-40% cheaper than London, 300+ digital health startups. #1 continental European startup city.

Resilience: what if the hype cycle pops?

Alche isn't built on longevity hype -- it's built on structural demand. Even if the buzzword fades: aging populations don't reverse (29% of Germany 65+ by 2040), consumer wellness spending is recession-resilient (McKinsey), we're consolidating existing spend (not creating new behaviour), and our "imperfect longevity" positioning is explicitly anti-hype. If the optimisation culture collapses, our positioning gets stronger.
