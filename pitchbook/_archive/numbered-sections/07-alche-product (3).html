The App

Three core functions. Plans. Executes. Converts.

The app is not a health tracker. It is the single point where data, community, and commerce converge into one unified experience. Physical space gets connected to centralized digital infrastructure, and every interaction converts into recurring revenue.

Plans

Transforms intention into structured action. Goal selection, protocol templates, journey framework. MVP: template-based. V1: AI-personalized.

Executes

Turns plans into real-world behavior. LED booking, smoothie pre-orders, session reminders, habit automation. The physical space makes it tangible.

Converts

Transforms engagement into revenue. Memberships, product sales (in-store pickup or shipping), smart replenishment, event ticketing.

CGM Strategy

Phased integration. Data first, intelligence later.

Our physician partner discovered that when patients see their glucose data in real-time, behavior changes naturally -- one patient stopped midnight eating entirely. We integrate this progressively: infrastructure at MVP, display at V1, intelligence at V2. No supply chain risk at launch.

The Science of Retention

Why hybrid beats digital-only.

Digital-only health tools fail because they attempt to establish habits using weak context cues, without social accountability, during the 30-70 day window when the habit is most fragile. The science of habit formation predicts this failure before any retention data confirms it.

3.3%

Median 30-day retention for health apps. 80% drop between day 1 and day 10. Users abandon before habit formation even has a chance.

Baumel et al. 2019, JMIR (PMC6785720, n=93 apps)
66 days

Average time to reach habit automaticity. Range: 18-254 days. Most apps lose users well before this window opens. Physical cues and social accountability bridge the gap.

Lally et al. 2010, European J. of Social Psychology (UCL, n=96)

The hybrid argument: physical spaces create environmental cues that app notifications can't replicate. Community creates social accountability. A place you go to, people you see, rituals you physically do -- these function as reliable triggers that anchor habits during the fragile formation window. The space doesn't just add cost -- it adds the retention mechanism that pure digital structurally lacks.
