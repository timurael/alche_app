Why Now
Force 01
Longevity went mainstream.
Peter Attia's Outlive became a #1 bestseller. Huberman reaches 10M+ followers. Bryan Johnson made anti-aging a cultural phenomenon. Euromonitor: healthspan is the #1 global consumer trend for 2025.
Force 02
Germany's prevention gap is an open market.
EUR 474B on healthcare, 3.1% on prevention. 7.8 hospital beds per 1,000 (nearly double the OECD average). 62% of Germans say the system focuses too much on treatment. The gap is not a policy debate: it is an open market.
Force 03
The technology is ready, but nobody has integrated it.
CGM sensors, AI-generated protocols, wearables (sleep, HRV, stress), red light therapy, functional nutrition. All clinically validated. All disconnected.
Who Is Left Out
Left Extreme
Clinical biohackers who build complex stacks. Technical, intimidating, inaccessible to most.
The Missing Consumer
  • Wants fewer options, better selected
  • Wants integrated rituals, not scattered products
  • Wants authority without medical intimidation
  • Wants taste without pseudoscience
  • Has purchasing power and aesthetic sensitivity
  • High lifetime value. No ecosystem built for them.
Right Extreme
Mass-market supplement buyers with no structure. Trend-driven, no integration, no retention.
"This consumer does not want more products. They want fewer, better ones, selected within a system they trust."
Sources: Euromonitor International (2025); OECD, Health at a Glance (2023); Destatis, Gesundheitsausgabenrechnung (2023); Global Wellness Institute (2024).
