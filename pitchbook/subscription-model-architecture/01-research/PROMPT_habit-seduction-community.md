## AGENT 1 — The Habit Architect

**Model:** Sonnet 4.6
**Domain:** The science and mechanics of habit formation

### Research Mission

Extract the fundamental laws of habit formation from primary sources and map them to alche's product.

**Primary frameworks to research:**
- **The Hook Model** (Nir Eyal, *Hooked*) — Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment
- **Habit Loop** (Charles Duhigg, *The Power of Habit*) — Cue → Routine → Reward
- **Tiny Habits / Fogg Behavior Model** (BJ Fogg) — Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt
- **Atomic Habits** (James Clear) — Identity-based habits, 1% improvements, habit stacking
- **Streaks and commitment devices** — Research on Duolingo streaks, gym check-ins, daily ritual apps

**Specific questions to answer:**

1. **The Cue Problem:** What are the most powerful cues for daily health rituals? (Time-based? Context-based? Social?) Which cue types work best in the morning vs evening?

2. **The Reward Problem:** What types of rewards sustain health app engagement past Day 30? (Progress visualization? Social recognition? Data revelation? Aesthetic rewards?) Why do most health apps fail at variable reward design?

3. **The Identity Shift:** James Clear argues habits stick when they become identity ("I am a longevity person" not "I am trying to be healthy"). How do the best apps accelerate this identity shift? What product mechanics trigger it?

4. **The Streak Mechanic:** Duolingo has 50% of users maintaining streaks. What makes streaks work vs feel punitive? What is the optimal "streak recovery" mechanic (Duolingo's streak freeze, etc.)?

5. **Habit Stacking for Longevity:** What daily rituals are already in the target user's life (Berlin tech worker, 28–45, wellness-aware) that alche can stack onto? Morning routine? Evening wind-down? Post-workout?

6. **The 66-Day Rule:** Research shows habits take an average of 66 days to form (not 21). What does this mean for free trial length, subscription timing, and retention at the 30/60/90 day marks?

## AGENT 2 — The Seduction Strategist

**Model:** Sonnet 4.6
**Domain:** The psychology of desire, seduction, and product obsession

### Research Mission

Extract the laws of seduction and desire from both classical and product design frameworks. Apply them to make alche feel irresistible — not just useful.

**The distinction:** A useful app gets used. A seductive app gets *missed* when you don't use it. You want users to feel something is wrong with their day if they haven't opened alche.

**Primary frameworks to research:**
- **The Art of Seduction** (Robert Greene) — The 9 seducer types, the 24 laws of seduction, specifically: creating desire through absence, hot and cold, creating an ideal, the lure
- **Wanting** (Luke Burgis) — Mimetic desire, why we want what others want, how to make a product feel coveted
- **The Psychology of Luxury** — Why people pay 10x for Hermès vs a regular bag. Scarcity, craft, initiation, belonging to the few
- **Variable reward / dopamine loops** — Slot machine mechanics in apps (Instagram likes, Snapchat streaks), why unpredictability creates compulsion
- **Exclusivity mechanics** — Superhuman's invite-only launch, Soho House membership psychology, how scarcity creates desire
- **Aesthetic seduction** — How beauty creates desire for a product beyond its utility (Apple, Aesop, Byredo)

**Specific questions to answer:**

1. **The Anticipation Principle:** Seduction is about building anticipation, not immediate satisfaction. How do the best apps create a sense of "I can't wait to check this"? What product mechanics create anticipatory desire?

2. **Hot and Cold:** Greene's law — constant availability kills desire. Being slightly withheld creates longing. How do the best consumer apps apply this? (Release of new content, limited events, gated features) How should alche apply this without frustrating users?

3. **The Ideal Self:** Seduction works by reflecting back the person's ideal image of themselves. How does alche make users see their "longevity self" — the version of them that's vital, sharp, long-lived? What product moments create this mirror?

4. **Mimetic Desire (Wanting):** We want what others want. How do community features, leaderboards, user-generated protocols, and social proof create mimetic desire for alche? Which specific mechanics make other users' behavior contagious?

5. **The Luxury Psychology:** Why do people pay EUR 99/mo for something they could approximate for free? Research the psychological triggers that make a product feel premium vs cheap — ritual of use, packaging, language, exclusivity, craft.

6. **The Initiation Ritual:** The most seductive communities have an initiation — something that signals you've crossed a threshold. Soho House has the membership review. Superhuman had the onboarding call. What is alche's initiation? How does it make users feel special for being accepted?

## AGENT 3 — The Community Architect

**Model:** Sonnet 4.6
**Domain:** The psychology of belonging, tribal identity, and community-driven retention

### Research Mission

Extract the laws of how communities form, why people stay in them, and how community belonging becomes a retention mechanism that no competitor can copy.

**The goal:** Make leaving alche feel like leaving your people — not just cancelling a subscription.

**Primary frameworks to research:**
- **Tribes** (Seth Godin) — A tribe needs a leader, a shared interest, and a way to communicate. How does alche become a tribe, not just an app?
- **The Art of Community** (Charles Vogl) — The 7 principles of belonging: boundary, initiation, rituals, temple, stories, inner rings, common good
- **Dunbar's Number** — Why communities break above 150 people. How do the best communities (Discord servers, Soho House chapters, CrossFit boxes) stay intimate at scale?
- **Identity-based belonging** — We stay in communities that reflect who we are (or who we want to be). How do the best wellness communities (CrossFit, SoulCycle, Equinox) make members identify as "a CrossFit person" not just "someone who goes to CrossFit"?
- **Ritual as glue** — The role of shared rituals in community cohesion (weekly check-ins, #dailyalche, seasonal challenges, events). Research on how ritual frequency affects community retention.
- **The shared enemy** — Communities bond over shared opposition (fitness vs sedentary culture, longevity vs "normal aging"). How do the best wellness brands define what their community is against?

**Specific questions to answer:**

1. **The Boundary Problem:** A community with no boundary is just a group chat. What defines who is "in" the alche community? What signals membership? (App usage? Event attendance? Ritual participation? Physical space?)

2. **Inner Rings:** The most powerful communities have layers — outer members, core members, inner circle. How should alche structure this? (Free users → Core subscribers → Pro members → Premium "founding circle")

3. **The #dailyalche Ritual:** Research the mechanics of successful user-generated habit communities (#75Hard, Duolingo streaks, Strava segments). What makes a daily sharing ritual stick vs feel like a chore?

4. **Events as Community Glue:** Research the role of physical events in digital community retention. How do brands like Lululemon (ambassador program), Soho House (events calendar), and AG1 (summits) use in-person moments to deepen digital community belonging?

5. **The Shared Story:** Every strong community has a founding myth and an enemy. What is alche's story? What is the community against? (Passive aging? Supplement overwhelm? The wellness-industrial complex?) How do the best communities articulate their shared mission?

6. **Churn Prevention Through Community:** Research specifically: how much does community participation reduce churn in subscription apps? (Data from Duolingo, Peloton, ClassPass, Strava). What community features have the highest correlation with 90-day retention?
