02 — The Problem

Two camps,
neither works.

The Global Wellness Summit’s 2026 trend report identifies “backlash against over-optimization” as one of the year’s defining shifts. People are tired of treating their bodies like systems to debug.

The longevity market has bifurcated into two camps, and neither serves the person standing in the middle. On one side: clinical, quantified, cold. Hyperbaric chambers, blood panels as dinner conversation, protocols that demand your entire life restructure around them. Effective, perhaps. Sustainable, rarely. Joyful, never.

On the other side: warm, intuitive, shallow. Wellness retreats that feel wonderful for a weekend and dissolve by Tuesday. Adaptogenic lattes with no dosing information. Community without curriculum. Good vibes, no science.

“The people who need longevity science most are the ones most alienated by how it’s delivered.”

The result is a gap — a vast, underserved space where science-curious, life-loving people find nothing that speaks to them. They want the evidence. They also want the espresso. They want to understand their biomarkers. They also want to sit in a beautiful room with interesting people and not talk about biomarkers at all.

This is the space alche occupies. Not the clinical extreme. Not the shallow centre. The integration point where rigour meets ritual, where data meets delight.

all six.
