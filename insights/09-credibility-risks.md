# Insight: Credibility Risks in Current Materials

## Issues that could undermine investor trust if not addressed.

---

### Risk 1: BCG Citation Is Unverifiable

**The problem:** The positioning hypothesis claims sourcing from "GWS 2026, BCG, McKinsey, and competitive analysis." No public BCG longevity or wellness report for 2025-2026 was found during extensive research.

**Impact:** Citing phantom sources is a credibility risk that undermines the legitimate, well-sourced research. If an investor asks "which BCG report?" and there's no answer, it taints everything else.

**Fix:** Either provide the specific BCG report reference or remove the citation. The GWS and McKinsey sources are strong enough on their own.

---

### Risk 2: The 6/6 Integration Score Is Self-Assigned and Fictional

**The problem:** The hexagonal chart claims Alche scores 6/6 across Physical Space, Products, AI App, Membership, Community, and Integrated Information System. Alche has zero of these capabilities today. Scoring yourself 6/6 on capabilities that don't exist conflates ambition with reality.

**Impact:** Sophisticated investors will see through this immediately. It signals either naivety or intentional misrepresentation.

**Fix:** Show a "target state" framing. Options:
- "0/6 today → 3/6 at launch → 6/6 at Series A"
- Side-by-side: Alche *planned* capabilities vs. competitors' *actual* capabilities, with explicit execution gap acknowledgment
- Label the Alche score as "TARGET" not "CURRENT"

---

### Risk 3: Market Size Framing Is Misleading

**The problem:** Leading with "$6.8T global wellness economy" when the longevity-specific market is $23-32B (350x smaller).

**Impact:** Investors who understand TAM/SAM/SOM will discount the entire analysis.

**Fix:** Lead with $23-32B longevity market at 8-10% CAGR. Use $6.8T only as directional context: "Longevity is a growing subsector within a $6.8T wellness economy."

---

### Risk 4: No ZOE Slide

**The problem:** ZOE is the most obvious counterexample to the "nobody does science + warmth" claim. The current deck materials don't address ZOE directly.

**Impact:** Every informed investor will ask "what about ZOE?" Having no pre-loaded answer signals either ignorance or avoidance.

**Fix:** Add an explicit ZOE comparison slide with honest differentiation (scope, physical space, geography, founder dependency).

---

### Risk 5: No Neko Health in European Landscape

**The problem:** Neko Health has $325M+ in funding, $1.8B valuation, and is co-founded by a Spotify founder. It's the most prominent European longevity company. The deck doesn't address it.

**Impact:** Omitting the biggest European longevity company from a European longevity competitive analysis looks like an oversight.

**Fix:** Add Neko with clear differentiation (hardware scans vs. lifestyle platform, different model, different customer, but same investor attention pool).

---

### Risk 6: "Nobody Occupies the White Space" Is Too Strong

**The problem:** The claim that the upper-right quadrant (high science + high humanity) is "unoccupied" is overstated. ZOE occupies ~70% of this position for nutrition.

**Impact:** An all-or-nothing claim is easily falsified. One counterexample destroys the entire framing.

**Fix:** Qualify: "Nobody does this *across multiple longevity dimensions* with a physical space anchor -- especially in Europe, especially centering women." That's still differentiated, but survives scrutiny.
