# Raw Research: Alternative Mapping (JTBD Lens)

> Research date: March 2026. All web data verified against public sources.

---

## JTBD Framework Applied

For each alternative: what job does the customer hire it for? Where does it fall short? What triggers switching?

Target customer: Women 30-55 in Berlin, wellness-engaged, longevity-curious, alienated by optimization culture.

---

## TIER 1: Direct Competitors (longevity-focused platforms)

### ZOE
- **Job hired for:** "Help me understand what food does to MY body so I can eat better without guessing"
- **Strengths:** Peer-reviewed science (Nature, Lancet). Warm, inclusive brand (Ragged Edge rebrand). 556K+ app users. Tim Spector credibility. $9.99/mo entry point (ZOE 2.0). Longevity content via podcast.
- **Where it falls short:**
  - Nutrition-only scope — no sleep, movement, stress, biomarkers beyond gut
  - No physical space or in-person community
  - App complaints: food logging takes 20+ min, technical glitches, "personalization" often generic
  - Eating disorder risk flagged by dietitians — food scoring creates anxiety
  - UK ASA banned misleading ad claims (2025)
  - September 2025 app overhaul removed blood glucose and fat tests, creating user frustration
  - Cancellation complaints widespread on Trustpilot
  - Founder-dependent: Tim Spector IS the brand
- **Switching triggers:** Desire for broader longevity beyond nutrition; wanting in-person connection; frustration with food-scoring anxiety; wanting community not just content
- **Threat level:** HIGH — closest brand positioning match. But expanding beyond nutrition would require multi-year pivot. [Data]
- **2026 update:** ZOE raised total $118M, expanding in US. Published major Nature paper Dec 2025. ZOE 2.0 app at lower price. Longevity-focused content increasing but product still nutrition-only. [Data]

### Function Health
- **Job hired for:** "Give me a comprehensive picture of what's happening inside my body so I can catch problems early"
- **Strengths:** 200K+ members. $100M+ ARR. $2.5B valuation (Nov 2025 Series B, $298M). 160+ biomarker tests. Acquired Ezra for MRI. AI chat features. Mark Hyman credibility. Hired women's health medical director.
- **Where it falls short:**
  - Clinical, metric-obsessed positioning — not warm or joyful
  - No community, no physical space, no food/lifestyle integration
  - Delivers data without implementation plan (common complaint)
  - $365/yr + add-ons. US-only.
  - Brand voice is "know your numbers" — anxiety-inducing for many
- **Switching triggers:** Wanting someone to help DO something with the data; wanting community; wanting warmth instead of clinical coldness; European market
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM — massive scale but fundamentally different positioning. Could add community features but cultural DNA is metric-first. [Data]
- **2026 update:** Major growth trajectory. Chase Sapphire partnership. ChatGPT integration. Sued Superpower over testing claims. Women's health director hired. [Data]

### Noom (LONGEVITY PIVOT)
- **Job hired for:** Originally "help me lose weight through behavioral psychology." NOW pivoting to "help me live longer through microdose GLP-1 + biomarkers + behavior change"
- **Strengths:** Massive existing user base. CBT-based behavior change engine. Insurance partnerships (Highmark). $149/mo longevity program. At-home biomarker testing via Ash. AI features (Face Scan, biological age). Strong app infrastructure.
- **Where it falls short:**
  - Terrible reputation for billing/subscription practices ($62M lawsuit settlement)
  - GLP-1 delivery complaints widespread — lost meds, expired vials, unreachable support
  - Color-coding system flagged for eating disorder triggers
  - Longevity pivot is brand-new (Dec 2025) — unproven in this space
  - No physical space, no food-as-social-object, no community beyond app
  - European presence unclear/limited
- **Switching triggers:** Wanting European/local; wanting in-person community; distrust of Noom's billing practices; wanting joy not weight-management framing
- **Threat level:** HIGH — they have scale, behavioral science expertise, and are now explicitly positioning as longevity. But execution quality is poor and brand trust is damaged. [Data]
- **2026 update:** THIS IS NEW since Feb 2026 research. Noom launched "Proactive Health Microdose GLP-1Rx" Dec 2025, explicitly calling itself "the leading platform for preventive health and longevity." $149/mo. 17-biomarker at-home testing. Major competitive shift. [Data]

### Hims & Hers (LONGEVITY LAUNCH COMING)
- **Job hired for:** "Make healthcare accessible, affordable, and not embarrassing"
- **Strengths:** 2.4M subscribers. Public company. Super Bowl ad ("Rich People Live Longer"). Novo Nordisk partnership for branded GLP-1s. Acquired peptide facility. $6.5B revenue target by 2030.
- **Where it falls short:**
  - Distribution platform, not science-deep
  - Longevity vertical not launched yet (late 2026)
  - No community, no physical space, no food component
  - Stock volatility (65% crash then 40% pop)
  - "Accessible" but not "joyful" or "community-driven"
- **Switching triggers:** Wanting depth over distribution; wanting community; wanting European; wanting women-specific (not gender-neutral)
- **Threat level:** HIGH (asymmetric) — massive resources but different DNA. If peptides + longevity + women's focus converge, they could become formidable. Window is 12-18 months. [Data]
- **2026 update:** Confirmed longevity launch late 2026 with peptides, coenzymes, GLP, GIP. Peptide facility operational. Novo partnership restored after lawsuit dropped. Stock recovered significantly. [Data]

### Parsley Health
- **Job hired for:** "Give me a doctor who looks at the whole picture, not just symptoms"
- **Strengths:** Functional medicine with longevity lens. Robin Berzin credibility. 50K+ patients (80% women). Longevity Labs relaunched Nov 2025. 100+ biomarkers specifically designed for women. "Female Functional Health Score." In-network for 10M+ patients.
- **Where it falls short:**
  - Clinical/telehealth model — no physical community space
  - US-only
  - $350-550/yr for labs (expensive for ongoing)
  - Not a lifestyle brand — it's a medical practice
  - No food, no cafe, no social component
- **Switching triggers:** Wanting European; wanting lifestyle + community not just clinical; wanting affordability; wanting in-person connection
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM — strongest women's longevity positioning in US clinical space. No European plans. Different model (medical practice vs. lifestyle platform). [Data]
- **2026 update:** Launched Female Longevity Labs nationally. 100-biomarker panel specific to women. "Functional Age" and "Aging Velocity" metrics. This is the closest US player to Alche's women-first longevity thesis. [Data]

## TIER 2: Adjacent Alternatives (partial overlap)

### OURA
- **Job hired for:** "Track my sleep and recovery so I know if I'm taking care of myself"
- **Strengths:** $11B valuation. 5.5M rings sold. High-80s% retention (exceptional for wearables). $1B estimated 2025 revenue. Pivoting toward women — fastest growing segment is women in early 20s. AI model trained on women's health research (Mar 2026). Cycle tracking improvements.
- **Where it falls short:**
  - Hardware product, not lifestyle platform
  - Data-first: contributes to "orthosomnia" (sleep-score anxiety)
  - Subscription fatigue ($5.99/mo on top of $349+ ring)
  - Privacy controversy (Palantir/DoD partnerships scared women tracking cycles)
  - Battery degradation complaints
  - No community, no food, no in-person
- **Switching triggers:** Privacy concerns; subscription fatigue; sleep anxiety from scores; wanting human connection not more data; wanting guidance on what to DO with the data
- **Threat level:** LOW-MEDIUM — different model. But their pivot to women's health means they're now competing for the same attention and positioning. [Data]

### WHOOP
- **Job hired for:** "Help me train and recover like an athlete"
- **Strengths:** Performance-athlete credibility. 350K+ blood testing waitlist. Published menstrual health research in Nature Digital Medicine. Healthspan longevity metric. WHOOP 5.0 hardware. ECG + blood pressure. 75% staff expansion.
- **Where it falls short:**
  - Performance/athlete DNA — intimidating to non-athletes
  - $359/yr mandatory subscription
  - No physical space, no food, no community beyond app
  - Women growing (150% YoY) but still male-skewing brand perception
- **Switching triggers:** Not identifying as "athlete"; wanting lifestyle not performance; wanting community; European preference
- **Threat level:** MEDIUM — just launched women's health blood panel (Mar 10, 2026!). $299 for 11 female-specific biomarkers. Directly entering women's longevity space. [Data]
- **2026 update:** CRITICAL — WHOOP launched Women's Health Specialized Blood Biomarker Panel March 10, 2026. AMH, progesterone, thyroid, perimenopause markers. This is brand new and directly relevant. [Data]

### AG1 (Athletic Greens)
- **Job hired for:** "Give me one simple thing I can do every morning that covers my nutritional bases"
- **Strengths:** $600M projected revenue. Massive influencer reach. CEO Kat Cole. Redirecting $20M into UC Davis clinical research. Simple daily ritual. Strong brand recognition.
- **Where it falls short:**
  - Science heavily disputed (30-person studies, Bryan Johnson public takedown Jan 2026)
  - Single product, not a platform
  - No community, no biomarkers, no personalization
  - $100/mo for powder skeptics say is worth $30
  - 100+ FDA adverse event reports
- **Switching triggers:** Losing trust in science claims; wanting personalization; wanting community; wanting multi-dimensional longevity not just a powder
- **Threat level:** LOW — different model entirely. But proves massive demand for simple daily longevity rituals. [Data]

### Seed Health
- **Job hired for:** "I want probiotics I can actually trust, from a company that does real science"
- **Strengths:** $1B+ valuation. $200M+ revenue (profitable). Nature papers. "Seed University" for influencer education. Design-forward brand.
- **Where it falls short:**
  - Cerebral/austere brand — not joyful
  - Probiotics only, not broad longevity
  - No physical space, no community
  - Intellectual appeal, not emotional
- **Switching triggers:** Wanting warmth; wanting breadth beyond probiotics; wanting community
- **Threat level:** LOW — proves science + design works commercially. Different audience psychology. [Data]

## TIER 3: Non-Obvious Alternatives (what target customers ACTUALLY do today)

### The Huberman Stack (podcast + supplements + blood test)
- **Job hired for:** "I want to learn the science myself and build my own protocol"
- **Where it falls short:** Overwhelming (100+ episodes), male-skewing, no personalization, no community accountability, no implementation support, requires massive self-discipline
- **Switching triggers:** Information overload; wanting someone to curate; wanting community; wanting female-specific guidance

### Gym Membership + Nutritionist
- **Job hired for:** "Exercise regularly + get dietary guidance"
- **Where it falls short:** Siloed — gym doesn't talk to nutritionist. No biomarkers. No longevity framing. Expensive in combination (EUR 50-80/mo gym + EUR 80-150/session nutritionist). No community identity.
- **Switching triggers:** Wanting integration; wanting longevity-specific guidance; cost optimization

### Doing Nothing / "I'll start later"
- **Job hired for:** "Avoid overwhelm, avoid spending, avoid feeling judged"
- **Where it falls short:** Aging doesn't wait. Health anxiety accumulates. FOMO from peers who are acting.
- **Switching triggers:** Health scare (personal or someone close); aging milestone birthday; friend's recommendation; discovering approachable entry point

### ANTI Berlin
- **Job hired for:** "I want a beautiful, design-forward space for Pilates, recovery, and wellness in Berlin"
- **Strengths:** Physical space in Mitte (Brunnenstrasse). Brutalist architecture. Pilates + red light + sauna + ice bath. "Longevity Lab" branding. 460 sqm. ClassPass/Urban Sports Club presence.
- **Where it falls short:** No app, no biomarkers, no science beyond "longevity" label, no food/nutrition component, no membership community beyond class attendance. Essentially a high-end fitness studio with longevity marketing.
- **Switching triggers:** Wanting science depth; wanting digital integration; wanting nutrition/food component; wanting community beyond classes
- **Threat level:** LOW-MEDIUM as competitor, HIGH as validation. Proves Berlin demand for longevity-positioned physical spaces. [Data]

### Weight Watchers / WW (menopause pivot)
- **Job hired for:** "Help me manage my weight through life changes"
- **2026 update:** WW launched menopause program with Queen Latifah. Hims entering perimenopause/menopause care.
- **Where it falls short:** Legacy brand, stigmatized, not longevity-framed, no science depth
- **Switching triggers:** Wanting modern, non-stigmatized brand; wanting longevity not weight loss
- **Threat level:** LOW [Data]

## LANGUAGE MAP (Customer Voice Extraction)

### Pain Points with Alternatives (exact language patterns)

**On data overwhelm:**
- "At a certain point, wearable data made me feel like I was failing every day"
- "Sleep trackers are giving people insomnia" (orthosomnia — documented clinical condition)
- "Analysis paralysis rather than clarity"
- "Wellness has shifted from something we feel to something we perform correctly"

**On optimization culture:**
- "Biohacking is incredibly male"
- "Not a hack" — women reject the metaphor
- "Selling people guilt that they haven't done more already"
- "Creates a burden of unachievable expectations"
- "Quality of life — not peak performance"
- "Too much information can overload our systems"

**On longevity fixation:**
- "Longevity fixation syndrome" — obsessing over extending lifespan to the point of breakdown
- "The pursuit of perfect health can become its own form of illness"
- "The relentless drive to optimize every biological function is itself a stress response masquerading as self-care"
- "Less performance. More presence."

**On wanting something different:**
- "Steady energy, increased resilience, and a life that feels expansive"
- "Working with our bodies, not against them"
- "Connection over clinical data"
- "Meaning over measurement"
- "Imperfect, emotional, relational and sensory" (GWS 2026)
- "Inhabiting the body" vs. "hacking the body"
- "The nervous system has replaced the six-pack as the wellness status symbol"

**On ZOE specifically:**
- "So much promise, too little function"
- "Not actually personalized"
- "Food scoring created stress and perfectionism"
- "Paying for something but not really being able to utilize it"
- "One of the worst experiences trying to cancel"

**On OURA specifically:**
- "Why I'm taking off my Oura ring" (viral Reddit thread)
- "The device added stress instead of reducing it"
- Privacy fears around reproductive health tracking

**On Noom specifically:**
- "Another subscription service with no advance notice and no refunds"
- "Classic shell game — stay away"
- Color system "triggering for those with a history of disordered eating"

**On Function Health:**
- "Data without implementation — identifies problems but sends you away without a plan"
- "Recommendations are generic lists"

### What "Better" Means to Target Customer

1. **Integration** — "One place that connects everything" not 5 separate apps/services
2. **Guidance** — "Tell me what to DO, not just what my numbers are"
3. **Joy** — "Health that doesn't feel like punishment"
4. **Community** — "Other women going through the same thing"
5. **Approachability** — "I don't need to be an expert to start"
6. **Trust** — "Real science, not influencer marketing"
7. **Sustainability** — "Something I can maintain, not a 30-day challenge"
8. **Physical** — "A place I can go, not just another screen"

### Switching Triggers (what makes someone move)

1. Health scare or aging milestone
2. Friend recommendation / "I tried this and it changed everything"
3. Frustration with data that doesn't lead to action
4. Overwhelm from too many apps/subscriptions/protocols
5. Feeling alienated by male-dominated longevity culture
6. Discovering an approachable, non-judgmental entry point
7. Wanting social connection around health (post-pandemic)
8. Perimenopause/menopause symptoms creating urgency
