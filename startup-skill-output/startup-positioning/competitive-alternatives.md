# Competitive Alternatives Map

> Full JTBD-based alternatives analysis for Alche positioning. March 2026.

---

## How to Read This Document

This is NOT a traditional competitive landscape. It maps every alternative a target customer might "hire" instead of Alche — including doing nothing. Organized by the JOB the customer is hiring for, not by product category.

---

## Job 1: "Help me understand and improve my health with real science"

| Alternative | What it delivers | What it lacks | Threat to Alche |
|------------|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Function Health** | 160+ biomarker tests, AI analysis, MRI via Ezra. $365/yr. 200K+ members. $2.5B valuation. | No community. No physical space. No lifestyle. Clinical cold. US-only. Data without implementation guidance. | Medium — different positioning DNA |
| **Parsley Health** | Functional medicine. 100-biomarker women's longevity panel. Female Functional Health Score. In-network 10M+ patients. | US-only. Clinical/telehealth. No cafe, no community space, no daily touchpoint. $350-550/yr for labs. | Medium — strongest women's longevity clinical play |
| **ZOE** | Peer-reviewed nutrition science. AI food scanner. 556K+ users. $9.99/mo entry. Gut microbiome research. | Nutrition-only. No physical space. App complaints (logging, personalization). Eating disorder risks. UK-based. Founder-dependent. | HIGH — closest brand positioning match |
| **InsideTracker** | Blood biomarkers + wearable integration. MIT/Harvard advisory. | Data without implementation. Generic recommendations. Expensive for episodic use. Buggy app. | Low |

**Key insight:** Every science-first alternative delivers DATA but not DAILY PRACTICE. The customer gets numbers but no life change. This is the gap. [Data + Opinion]

|------------|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **ANTI Berlin** | Beautiful physical space. Pilates + red light + sauna. Longevity branding. Mitte location. | No app. No biomarkers. No science depth. No food/nutrition. No membership community beyond classes. | Low-Medium as competitor, HIGH as validation |

## Job 5: "I want to age well without it becoming my whole personality"

| Alternative | What it delivers | What it lacks | Threat to Alche |
|------------|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Doing nothing** | No overwhelm. No cost. No commitment. | Aging doesn't wait. Health anxiety accumulates. Missed window for prevention. | HIGH — the real competitor for most people |
| **Occasional doctor visit + supplements** | Baseline checkup. General multivitamin. | Reactive not preventive. No personalization. No community. No joy. | Medium — the default behavior |
| **Goop** | Lifestyle aspiration. Beautiful brand. | No real science. FDA warning letters. Celebrity-dependent. Expensive. | Low |

**Key insight:** The biggest competitor is inaction. The customer who would MOST benefit from Alche is the one most likely to be overwhelmed into paralysis by the existing options. The entry experience must be radically low-friction. [Opinion]

## Competitive Convergence Alert (March 2026)

**Multiple players are converging on Alche's claimed white space from different directions:**

| Player | Moving FROM | Moving TOWARD | When |
|--------|-----------|--------------|------|
| ZOE | Nutrition science | Broader longevity content | Ongoing (gradual) |
| Noom | Weight loss | Longevity platform + biomarkers | Dec 2025 (launched) |
| WHOOP | Performance tracking | Women's health + blood biomarkers | Mar 2026 (just launched) |
| Function Health | Clinical testing | Women's health director + AI guidance | 2025-2026 |
| Hims & Hers | Telehealth | Longevity vertical + peptides | Late 2026 (planned) |
| Parsley Health | Functional medicine | Women's longevity labs | Nov 2025 (launched) |
| WW | Weight management | Menopause care | 2026 |

**Implication:** The window for claiming "nobody does science + warmth + women + community" is narrowing. Not because any single player does it all, but because multiple players are each adding one piece. Within 18 months, the composite offering across 3-4 apps could approximate what Alche proposes as integrated. The integration itself and the physical space are the durable differentiators. [Opinion]

## Red Flags
- The "nobody does this" claim is weaker than it was 3 months ago. The competitive landscape is moving fast.
- Noom's longevity pivot was not in Alche's Feb 2026 research. This is a significant new threat that needs addressing.
- WHOOP's women's health blood panel launched March 10, 2026. Speed of competitive movement is increasing.

## Yellow Flags
- ZOE 2.0 at $9.99/mo is a low-barrier entry to science-backed nutrition. Alche's EUR 19/mo tier must deliver clearly more value.
- Function Health's AI chat + protocols feature starts to address the "data without direction" gap that Alche positions against.
- ANTI Berlin proves the physical-space-in-Berlin thesis but also means Alche has a local competitor for the "longevity space" positioning.
