# Target Audience -- Alche

> **Status:** Hypothesis-stage. No customer interviews conducted. Validation required.

---

## Primary Segment: "The Conscious Midlifer"

### Demographics
- **Gender:** Women (primary), gender-inclusive (secondary)
- **Age:** 30-55 (core: 35-50)
- **Location:** Berlin (Mitte, Kreuzberg, Prenzlauer Berg, Charlottenburg)
- **Income:** EUR 40K-100K+/yr (wellness-spending capacity of EUR 50-200/mo)
- **Language:** English + German (Berlin is ~15% expat)
- **Education:** University-educated, professional
- **Life stage:** Pre-menopause through menopause, career-established

### Psychographics
- **Identity:** Health-conscious but not "biohacker." Wellness-engaged but increasingly skeptical of optimization culture.
- **Values:** Community, authenticity, sustainability, self-care as self-respect (not self-punishment)
- **Motivation:** Aging well without becoming an optimization project. Science-informed but not science-obsessed.
- **Frustration:** Alienated by male-dominated longevity culture (Bryan Johnson, WHOOP culture). Exhausted by wellness "shoulds." Lacks trusted guide through information overload.
- **Spending:** Already spends on yoga, nutrition, supplements, skincare. Open to consolidating into one platform.

### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)
1. **When I** read about longevity science, **I want to** understand what actually applies to my body and my life stage, **so I can** make informed decisions without a PhD in biochemistry.
2. **When I** try a new wellness practice, **I want to** do it in community with like-minded women, **so I can** sustain motivation and not feel alone.
3. **When I** get bloodwork results, **I want to** understand what they mean and what to do next, **so I can** actually improve my health rather than just collecting data.
4. **When I** enter perimenopause/menopause, **I want to** access science-based longevity guidance specific to women, **so I can** navigate this transition proactively rather than reactively.

## Customer Journey Hypothesis

```
AWARENESS           CONSIDERATION           CONVERSION           RETENTION

Blog/social    ->   Cafe visit         ->   Free app tier   ->   Community events
Podcast        ->   Pop-up event       ->   Trial month     ->   Biomarker cycles
Word of mouth  ->   Friend referral    ->   EUR 19/mo       ->   Personalized recs
Berlin events  ->   Longevity workshop ->   EUR 49/mo       ->   Peer accountability
PR/media       ->   Product sample     ->   EUR 99/mo       ->   Identity ("I am alche")
```

**Key hypothesis:** The cafe/physical space is the awareness-to-consideration bridge. The app is the consideration-to-conversion engine. Community is the retention mechanism. If any one of these breaks, the funnel collapses.

## Competitive Alternatives (What They Do Instead)

| Alternative | Monthly Spend | What It Provides | What It Lacks |
|---|---|---|---|
| Yoga studio membership | EUR 60-100 | Movement, community, mindfulness | Science, longevity, data |
| AG1 subscription | EUR 70-90 | Convenience, "insurance" feeling | Actual science, community, personalization |
| ZOE membership | ~EUR 50-60 | Science-backed nutrition guidance | Multi-dimensional longevity, physical space, in-person community |
| Nutritionist (monthly) | EUR 80-150 | Personalized guidance | Community, longevity breadth, convenience |
| Doing nothing (status quo) | EUR 0 | No effort | Everything |

**The key substitution question:** Which of these would a target customer cancel to afford Alche? If the answer is "none -- it's incremental spend," the acquisition cost is higher. If the answer is "I'd replace my nutritionist + AG1," the value proposition is clearer. [Opinion]

## Flags

**RED:**
- No customer interviews conducted. All segments, personas, and sizing are hypothetical. Every number in this document could be wrong by 2-5x.
- Berlin expat vs. German audience split is unresolved. Language, cultural framing, and marketing channels differ fundamentally.

**YELLOW:**
- Gen Z drives 41% of U.S. wellness spend (McKinsey). The 30-55 age target may be missing the largest spending cohort. Berlin's young population (median age ~35) may skew demand younger.
- The "doing nothing" alternative is the real competitor. Inertia kills more startups than competitors do.
