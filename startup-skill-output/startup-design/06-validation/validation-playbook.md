# Validation Playbook -- Alche

> **Version:** 1.0 | March 2026
> **Purpose:** Structured experiments to validate or invalidate the 5 core bets before committing significant capital.

---

## Validation Philosophy

**Spend EUR 10-15K and 6-8 weeks to de-risk EUR 500K of investment.**

Every major startup failure in longevity (Forward $657M, DayTwo $85M, Modern Age $33M) could have been partially prevented by pre-launch validation. The cost of getting these experiments wrong is EUR 10-15K. The cost of skipping them and building on false assumptions is EUR 500K.

---

## Experiment 1: Positioning Resonance Test

**Hypothesis:** "Imperfect Longevity" positioning drives purchase intent, not just brand affinity.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | A/B/C concept testing with 3 positioning variants |
| **Variant A** | "Imperfect Longevity" -- joy-forward, anti-optimization, warm |
| **Variant B** | "Science-Informed Longevity" -- credibility-forward, evidence-based, neutral |
| **Variant C** | "Community Longevity" -- belonging-forward, social, together |
| **Sample** | 50+ women, 30-55, Berlin, wellness-engaged |
| **Stimulus** | 3 concept boards (visual + copy + pricing) |
| **Measures** | (1) Appeal rating (1-10), (2) Purchase intent (definitely/probably/maybe/no), (3) WTP (Van Westendorp), (4) Open-ended: "What would make you sign up?" |
| **Success criteria** | Winning variant scores >7/10 appeal AND >40% "definitely/probably" purchase intent at EUR 49/mo |
| **Timeline** | 3 weeks |
| **Budget** | EUR 1,500 (incentives + design) |
| **Kill criterion** | No variant exceeds 25% purchase intent at EUR 49/mo = positioning needs fundamental rework |

## Experiment 2: Willingness to Pay

**Hypothesis:** Target segment will pay EUR 49/mo for an integrated longevity membership.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter + substitution mapping |
| **Sample** | 50+ respondents from Experiment 1 |
| **Questions** | (1) At what price would this be too expensive? (2) Too cheap to be quality? (3) Getting expensive but you'd still consider? (4) A bargain? Plus: "What would you cancel to afford this?" |
| **Measures** | Optimal price point, acceptable price range, substitution patterns |
| **Success criteria** | Optimal price point falls between EUR 35-65/mo; >30% would substitute current wellness spending |
| **Timeline** | 2 weeks (can run with Experiment 1) |
| **Budget** | Included in Experiment 1 |
| **Kill criterion** | Optimal price point <EUR 25/mo = business model doesn't work at this price band |

## Experiment 3: Food-as-Social-Object

**Hypothesis:** Food (cooking, eating, sharing meals) is the universal social object that unlocks community around longevity.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | 3-5 pop-up dinner events in Berlin |
| **Format** | 15-25 women per event, functional food dinner, expert guest, facilitated conversation |
| **Measures** | (1) Attendance/sold-out rate, (2) Post-event NPS, (3) Social sharing (posts, tags), (4) Waitlist/app signup rate, (5) "Would you come to another?" rate, (6) "Would you pay EUR 49/mo for this + more?" |
| **Success criteria** | (1) Events sell out within 1 week, (2) NPS >50, (3) >30% social sharing, (4) >50% sign up for waitlist, (5) >80% "would come again" |
| **Timeline** | 6 weeks (1-2 events per week) |
| **Budget** | EUR 3,000-5,000 (venue, food, guest speaker) |
| **Kill criterion** | Events don't sell out even with heavy promotion; <30% waitlist signup; NPS <30 |

## Experiment 4: Audience Validation

**Hypothesis:** Women 30-55 in Berlin are the primary customer segment.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Screening survey + customer discovery interviews |
| **Survey** | Online, distributed via Berlin wellness communities, Instagram, Longevity Germany, events |
| **Interview target** | 30+ in-depth interviews (45-60 min each) |
| **Segments to explore** | German women 30-55, expat women 30-55, German women 25-35, men 30-50 |
| **Key questions** | (1) Current wellness spending, (2) Longevity awareness, (3) Community needs, (4) Physical space interest, (5) Berlin-specific behaviors |
| **Success criteria** | Clear primary segment emerges with >2x higher WTP and engagement intent than other segments |
| **Timeline** | 6 weeks |
| **Budget** | EUR 1,000 (incentives for interviews) |
| **Adaptation** | If data shows different primary segment (e.g., younger, or mixed gender), update all strategy documents |

---

## Experiment 5: Cafe-to-App Conversion

**Hypothesis:** A physical space converts visitors to digital subscribers at >3% rate.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Track conversion from pop-up events to app/waitlist signups |
| **Measures** | (1) Total event attendees, (2) App downloads, (3) Waitlist signups, (4) Eventual paid conversions |
| **Success criteria** | >3% conversion from event attendee to waitlist signup; eventual >10% of waitlist converts to paid |
| **Timeline** | Embedded in Experiment 3 |
| **Budget** | Included in Experiment 3 |
| **Kill criterion** | <1% conversion from event to any digital engagement = physical-to-digital bridge isn't working |

---

## Experiment 6: Heilpraktiker Regulatory Scope

**Hypothesis:** Heilpraktiker pathway covers the intended service offerings (biomarker interpretation, nutritional guidance, lifestyle protocols).

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Legal consultation with German health regulatory specialist |
| **Questions** | (1) What services can a Heilpraktiker-licensed platform offer? (2) What biomarker interpretation is permissible? (3) What disclaimers/limitations apply? (4) What would require a medical license? |
| **Success criteria** | Core service offerings are permissible under Heilpraktiker framework with clear boundaries |
| **Timeline** | 2-4 weeks |
| **Budget** | EUR 2,000-3,000 |
| **Kill criterion** | Core service offerings require full medical licensure = architecture needs fundamental redesign |

## Validation Timeline

```
Week 1-2:   Launch screening survey + begin interviews
            Commission legal review
            Design concept boards for Experiment 1

Week 3-4:   Run concept testing (Experiment 1 + 2)
            Continue interviews (Experiment 4)
            Plan pop-up events

Week 5-6:   First 2 pop-up events (Experiment 3 + 5)
            Complete interviews
            Receive legal review

Week 7-8:   Final 2-3 pop-up events
            Synthesize all data
            Go/No-Go decision on each hypothesis

Week 9:     Update strategy documents with validation data
            Finalize pitch for investors
```

**Total validation budget: EUR 8,000-12,000**
**Total validation time: 8-9 weeks**

---

## Decision Framework Post-Validation

| Result | Action |
|---|---|
| All 5 experiments pass | Proceed with confidence. Fundraise. Build. |
| 3-4 pass, 1-2 need adjustment | Proceed with documented pivots (e.g., different price point, different segment) |
| 1-2 pass, 3-4 fail | Major strategy rethink required before fundraise |
| 0-1 pass | Honest conversation about whether this startup should exist in this form |
