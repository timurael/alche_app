## CUSTOMER PROFILE

### Customer Jobs

**Functional Jobs:**
1. Understand how my body is aging and what to do about it
2. Navigate the overwhelming longevity information landscape
3. Get blood/biomarker tests and understand what they mean
4. Find evidence-based longevity practices that fit my life
5. Track my health progress over time (not just data, but meaning)

**Social Jobs:**
1. Find community of like-minded women interested in aging well
2. Share wellness experiences without judgment or competition
3. Feel part of a movement, not alone in my health journey
4. Access a "third place" that reflects my values (not gym, not spa, not clinic)

**Emotional Jobs:**
1. Feel empowered about aging, not anxious
2. Reduce the guilt/pressure of not "optimizing enough"
3. Enjoy the process of getting healthier (pleasure, not punishment)
4. Trust that I'm making good decisions based on real science
5. Feel seen -- this was designed for someone like me

## Value Proposition Statement

**For** wellness-engaged women 30-55 in Berlin
**Who** want to age well but are alienated by optimization culture
**Alche is** a longevity lifestyle platform
**That** combines multi-dimensional science with joyful, community-anchored experience
**Unlike** Function Health (cold, data-only), ZOE (nutrition-only), AG1 (unscientific), or scattered yoga/supplements
**We** integrate physical space, AI-powered personalization, biomarker testing, and real community -- delivering science with soul.

## Flags

**RED:**
- The value proposition assumes pains/gains that are researcher-constructed, not customer-validated. The entire canvas could be wrong.
- "Imperfect Longevity" as a gain creator may attract an audience that doesn't pay for optimization (because they're rejecting it). The monetization paradox: the people who hate optimizing may not value optimizing tools.

**YELLOW:**
- Pain intensity rankings are assumptions. Actual intensity ranking may differ (e.g., "menopause support" might be #1, not "information overwhelm").
- Gain creator "joy in health" is the hardest to deliver and measure. There are no KPIs for joy.
