# Lean Canvas -- Alche (Ash Maurya Framework)

> **Version:** 1.0 | March 2026 | Pre-validation

---

## 1. PROBLEM (Top 3)

1. **Science without soul:** Longevity brands with real science (Function Health, WHOOP, Blueprint) are cold, male-coded, and exclusionary -- alienating the majority of wellness-engaged women.
2. **Warmth without rigor:** Inclusive wellness brands (AG1, Goop, Noom) lack scientific depth -- consumers are spending on products that don't actually work.
3. **Data without community:** 90% wearable abandonment proves that data alone doesn't sustain health behavior. No longevity brand has solved the retention problem through community.

**Existing alternatives:**
- ZOE (~EUR 60/mo): Science + warmth, but nutrition-only
- Function Health ($365/yr): Testing platform, but cold and US-only
- AG1 (~EUR 80/mo): Easy, but scientifically questionable
- Yoga studio + nutritionist: Partial solution, fragmented, expensive
- Doing nothing: The real competitor

---

## 2. CUSTOMER SEGMENTS

**Early adopter:** Women 35-50 in Berlin, wellness-engaged, spending EUR 50+/mo on scattered wellness products/services, perimenopause/menopause age, alienated by biohacker culture, seeking science-informed but joyful approach to aging well.

**Broader market (later):** Wellness-engaged adults 28-55 in European cities who want integrated longevity guidance without optimization pressure.

---

## 3. UNIQUE VALUE PROPOSITION

**Single sentence:** Alche is the first longevity platform that combines multi-dimensional science with joyful, community-anchored experience -- starting from a physical space in Berlin, powered by an AI app, designed for women who want to age well without becoming an optimization project.

**High-level concept:** "ZOE's science + Erewhon's vibe + Peloton's community -- for longevity, in Europe"

## 9. UNFAIR ADVANTAGE

**Claimed advantages (require validation):**
1. **Community Anchor Architecture** -- physical space as marketing (revenue-generating, not cost center) + app as business engine. No competitor has this in European longevity.
2. **Daria's network** -- established figure in longevity/wellness with media/PR connections.
3. **Berlin-first mover** -- no consumer longevity lifestyle brand exists in Berlin.
4. **"Imperfect Longevity" positioning** -- rides the over-optimization backlash trend.

**Honest assessment of each:**
1. Unproven architecture. Forward Health had a similar multi-pillar ambition and died with $657M. The asset-light twist is the key differentiator.
2. Networks are real but not moats. Competitors can build similar networks.
3. First-mover in a geography is an advantage only if the geography matters. Berlin is emerging but not yet the center of European longevity.
4. Positioning can be copied. ZOE could rebrand toward "imperfect" within a quarter.

**True unfair advantage (if it works):** A physical community that creates emotional bonds and identity formation around longevity -- something that cannot be replicated digitally and takes years to build. This is the only real moat, and it's 100% unproven. [Opinion]

## Flags

**RED:**
- Monthly burn range of EUR 23.5K-42K is too wide. The strategy depends on which end you land on. Must narrow before fundraise.
- Unfair advantage is entirely hypothetical. None of the claimed advantages have been demonstrated.

**YELLOW:**
- Break-even at Month 12 requires 145 subscribers AND EUR 10K/mo space revenue. Both must hit simultaneously.
- App development cost (EUR 8-15K/mo) assumes CTO hire at below-market salary. Berlin CTO market is competitive.
