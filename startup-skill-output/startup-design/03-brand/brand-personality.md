# Brand Personality -- Alche

> **Version:** 1.0 | March 2026

---

## Personality Archetype

**Primary: The Sage** (wisdom, knowledge, truth-seeking)
**Secondary: The Caregiver** (nurturing, protection, generosity)
**Tertiary: The Explorer** (curiosity, freedom, discovery)

**Combined expression:** A wise, warm guide who makes the complex feel approachable and the serious feel joyful. Not an authority figure but a trusted companion on the longevity journey.

---

## If Alche Were a Person

**Age:** 40s -- old enough to be credible, young enough to be vital
**Vibe:** The woman at dinner who just finished reading the latest longevity paper and explains it while making everyone laugh. She's done her research but doesn't lecture. She cooks beautiful food because she loves it, not because she's performing health. She asks about your life, not your biomarkers.

**She is:**
- Curious before opinionated
- Warm before impressive
- Honest before diplomatic
- Playful before serious
- Communal before individual

**She is NOT:**
- A doctor giving instructions
- A guru dispensing wisdom
- A coach pushing you harder
- An influencer performing wellness
- A tech bro optimizing everything

## Brand Personality Traits (Big Five Model)

| Trait | Level | Expression |
|---|---|---|
| **Openness** | HIGH | Curious, creative, intellectually adventurous, science-loving |
| **Conscientiousness** | MEDIUM-HIGH | Evidence-based, reliable, but not rigid or perfectionistic |
| **Extraversion** | MEDIUM | Social, community-oriented, but values depth over breadth |
| **Agreeableness** | HIGH | Warm, inclusive, supportive, non-judgmental |
| **Neuroticism** | LOW | Calm, grounded, not anxious about health or trends |

## Personality Contrasts

### How Alche Would Handle Common Situations

| Situation | Function Health | AG1 | Alche |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bad biomarker result | "Your ApoB is elevated. Here are your numbers." | (Doesn't test) | "Your inflammation markers are higher than we'd like. That's actually useful -- here's what we can do together." |
| New longevity study | "Added to your research library." | "Science shows..." (vague) | "New study just dropped on NAD+ and sleep. We read it so you don't have to. The short version: interesting but early." |
| Member misses a month | "Your subscription is paused." | (Product, not service) | "Life happens. Your community is here when you're ready. No streaks to break." |
| Controversial wellness trend | (Ignores) | (Endorses for engagement) | "Let's talk about this honestly. Here's what the evidence says, here's what we don't know, and here's what we'd do." |

## Brand Aesthetic Personality

| Element | Character | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Color palette** | Warm earth tones + one unexpected accent | Science (neutral tones) + joy (accent) |
| **Typography** | Clean serif + organic sans | Trust (serif) + approachability (sans) |
| **Photography** | Real women, real food, real spaces -- not stock | Authenticity over aspiration |
| **Illustration** | Organic, hand-drawn quality | Human imperfection over clinical precision |
| **Space design** | Apothecary meets living room | Knowledge + comfort |
| **Texture** | Natural materials (wood, ceramic, linen) | Grounded, tactile, anti-digital |

## Personality in the Product

| Product Element | Personality Expression |
|---|---|
| Onboarding | "Tell us about you" not "Enter your data." Conversational, curious, never clinical. |
| Dashboard | "Here's how you're doing" not "Your scores." Narrative, not numbers-first. |
| Notifications | Encouraging, never nagging. "Ready for your next step?" not "You haven't logged in 3 days!" |
| Community | "What did you try this week?" not "Share your results." Process over outcomes. |
| Biomarker results | Story format: "Here's what we learned about your body" not table of numbers. |
| Events | Named with personality: "The Long Table" (dinner), "The Morning Brief" (workshop), "Lab Notes" (science talk) |

## Brand Personality Don'ts

| Don't | Because | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Don't be alarmist | Fear doesn't align with joy | "Your levels are DANGEROUSLY low!" |
| Don't be preachy | Authority kills community | "You SHOULD be doing cold exposure every morning." |
| Don't be vague | Warmth without substance is Goop territory | "Nourish your inner light with our elixir." |
| Don't be trendy | Trends pass; personality endures | "This is SO 2026." |
| Don't be exclusive | Community means everyone at the table | "Only for the most dedicated longevity enthusiasts." |
| Don't be corporate | Startups that sound like corporations feel dead | "We leverage synergies in the longevity vertical." |

## Personality Stress Test

How does the personality hold up under pressure?

| Scenario | Response |
|---|---|
| A member publicly criticizes Alche's science | "Thank you for pushing us on this. Here's the evidence we based our recommendation on. If you have counter-evidence, we genuinely want to see it. Getting this right matters more than being right." |
| A trendy supplement goes viral but lacks evidence | "We know everyone's talking about [X]. We looked at the research and we're not convinced yet. Here's what we found and what we're watching for." |
| Alche makes a mistake | "We got this wrong. Here's what happened, why it happened, and what we've changed. We're sorry." |
| A competitor launches a similar product | "Great -- more people bringing science and joy to longevity. Here's what makes us different." |

---

## Flags

**YELLOW:**
- Brand personality is the easiest thing to define and the hardest to maintain. Every hire, every piece of content, every customer interaction must embody this personality. One discordant experience breaks trust.
- The "warm science" balance is genuinely difficult. Leaning too warm = "just another wellness brand." Leaning too scientific = "just another Function Health." The personality must live exactly at the intersection.

---

*This personality guide is a compass, not a script. It should feel natural, not performed.*
