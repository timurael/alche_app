# Alche -- Intake Brief

## The Idea
- **Problem:** The longevity market splits into two camps: science-deep brands that are cold, male, and exclusionary (Bryan Johnson, Function Health, WHOOP) vs. warm/inclusive brands that lack scientific rigor (AG1, Goop, Noom). No brand combines deep, multi-dimensional longevity science with warm, joyful, inclusive positioning -- especially in Europe, especially for women.
- **Solution:** alche is a longevity lifestyle platform with a "Community Anchor Architecture": a physical cafe/apothecary in Berlin as the entry point, an AI-powered app as the business engine, lab partnerships for biomarker testing, and community as the retention mechanism. Six integration pillars: Physical Space, Products, AI App, Membership, Community, Integrated Information System.
- **Trigger:** Market research showing structural gap (GWS 2026 identified over-optimization backlash as Trend #2; gender gap in longevity -- only 6% of healthcare investment goes to women's health; 90% wearable abandonment within 12 months showing data-first approach fails).

## The Market
- **Target customer:** Women 30-55 in Berlin, wellness-engaged, longevity-curious but alienated by biohacker culture.
- **Current alternatives:** ZOE (nutrition only, UK), Function Health (clinical, cold), AG1 (warm but shallow science), Goop (lifestyle, no science), gyms, nutritionists, doing nothing.
- **Direct competitors known:** ZOE, Seed, Hims & Hers (emerging), ANTI Berlin, Aware (Berlin), Neko Health (Stockholm), MoleQlar (Germany).
- **Geography:** Berlin first, then European expansion.
- **Market size:** Global longevity market $23-32B at 8-10% CAGR. Menopause market $40B by 2030. $8.5B VC funding in longevity (2024).

## The Business
- **Revenue model:** Subscription app -- EUR 19/mo (Essentials), EUR 49/mo (Integrated), EUR 99/mo (Complete). Cafe generates revenue but primarily serves as marketing/community anchor.
- **Pricing:** EUR 19/49/99 per month.
- **Success metrics:** Break-even at Month 12, ~145 paying subscribers + EUR 10K/month space revenue.
- **Biggest unknowns:** (1) Does "Imperfect Longevity" positioning resonate at purchase level? (2) Is food the right social object for longevity community? (3) WTP at $50-100/mo in most contested price band. (4) Community hypothesis for longevity is completely unproven.
