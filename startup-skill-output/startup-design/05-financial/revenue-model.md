## Revenue Sensitivity Analysis

| Variable | Base Value | If 20% Worse | Revenue Impact (Year 1) |
|---|---|---|---|
| ARPU | EUR 50 | EUR 40 | -EUR 24K (-12.5%) |
| New subs/month | 25 | 20 | -EUR 30K (-15.6%) |
| Monthly churn | 5% | 7% | -EUR 15K (-7.8%) |
| Cafe revenue | EUR 10K/mo | EUR 8K/mo | -EUR 16K (-8.3%) |
| All variables 20% worse simultaneously | | | -EUR 72K (-37.5%) |

**Key insight:** The model is most sensitive to new subscriber acquisition rate and ARPU. Churn has moderate impact in Year 1 (compounds more in Year 2+). Cafe revenue is important but not the primary driver.

## Break-Even Analysis

| Cost Scenario | Monthly Burn | Break-Even Subs (at EUR 50 ARPU + EUR 10K cafe) | Months to Reach |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lean (EUR 23.5K/mo) | EUR 23.5K | 270 subs | 12-15 months |
| Moderate (EUR 32K/mo) | EUR 32K | 440 subs | 16-20 months |
| Full (EUR 42K/mo) | EUR 42K | 640 subs | 20-28 months |

**Honest assessment:** Break-even at 145 subscribers (from intake brief) assumed EUR 10K/mo cafe revenue covering a significant share of costs. The reality is more nuanced. At a moderate burn of EUR 32K/mo, you need 440 subscribers OR significant cafe revenue growth to break even. The original 145-subscriber break-even target works only under the leanest burn scenario. ## Flags

**RED:**
- The original break-even target of 145 subscribers is achievable only under the leanest cost scenario (EUR 23.5K/mo burn). At moderate burn (EUR 32K/mo), break-even requires ~440 subscribers -- a fundamentally different timeline and fundraising implication.
- 24-month runway at moderate burn (EUR 32K x 24 = EUR 768K) exceeds the EUR 500K raise even before revenue. Revenue must contribute meaningfully from Month 4-6 to avoid running out of cash.

**YELLOW:**
- Conservative scenario (EUR 367K cumulative revenue over 24 months) does not cover 24 months of even lean operations (EUR 23.5K x 24 = EUR 564K). The gap is EUR 197K, meaning the EUR 500K raise must cover it: EUR 500K - EUR 197K = EUR 303K of revenue-funded operations. This math is tight but feasible.
- ARPU of EUR 50 depends on 50% of subscribers choosing EUR 49/mo tier. If the mix shifts to 50% Essentials (EUR 19), ARPU drops to ~EUR 38, breaking the model.
