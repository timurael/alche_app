# Battle Card: Noom

*Updated: 2026-03-12*

---

## Who They Are

Noom is a New York-based digital health company ($624M raised, $3.7B peak valuation) founded in 2008 that began as a weight loss app using cognitive behavioral therapy. With 1,185 employees and a free tier launched in 2025, they're pivoting toward longevity and preventive health — launching "Proactive Health" (Dec 2025) combining microdosed GLP-1, biomarker testing, and a "Longevity Companion" at $149/month. Positive EBITDA and free cash flow, but brand trust is damaged by a $62M billing practices lawsuit settlement.

---

## Strengths (Honest)

- **Behavioral science foundation:** CBT-based approach is clinically validated for behavior change. [Data]
- **Scale:** Massive existing user base, strong brand recognition, 1,185 employees. [Data]
- **Financial health:** Positive EBITDA and free cash flow (Oct 2025). [Data]
- **Longevity pivot underway:** Proactive Health program with biomarkers + GLP-1 + AI. [Data]
- **B2B channel:** Highmark Health (2M members), Novo Nordisk, LillyDirect, Castlight Health. [Data]
- **AI innovation:** Face Scan (biological age in 30 seconds), Glucose Forecasting (no CGM needed). [Data]
- **Free tier:** Sept 2025 launch creates massive acquisition funnel. [Data]
- **Pricing flexibility:** $17-279/month across tiers — serves wide range. [Data]

---

## Weaknesses (Specific)

- **$62M lawsuit settlement:** Over billing practices — "one of the most common complaints." Brand trust severely damaged. [Data]
- **BBB and Trustpilot complaints:** Auto-renewal without warning, charges of $837, inability to speak to humans. [Data]
- **Coaching quality:** "Generic and superficial" — coaches may lack qualifications users expect. [Data]
- **Food database errors:** "Kung Pao chicken is NOT a GREEN FOOD!" — accuracy issues undermine trust. [Data]
- **Noom Med problems:** Nurse practitioners not showing up for appointments; medication transitions requiring full re-signup. [Data]
- **Sitejabber: 1.4 stars** (73 reviews) — worst rating among competitors. [Data]
- **Brand = "diet app":** Strong association with weight loss, not longevity. [Data]
- **Longevity offering very new:** Proactive Health launched Dec 2025 — unproven. [Data]

---

## How to Win Against Them

1. **Trust:** Noom settled a $62M lawsuit for billing practices. Alche's transparent pricing, easy cancellation, and community-based model builds trust, not litigates it.

2. **Depth vs. breadth:** Noom's longevity offering is behavioral coaching + GLP-1 + basic biomarkers. Alche offers multi-dimensional longevity with deeper lab partnerships, physical community, and science across sleep, stress, hormones, nutrition, and movement.

3. **Community vs. coaching:** Noom's coaches are criticized as generic. Alche's cafe and community provide real human connection — not a stranger texting you through an app.

4. **European native:** Noom is US-focused. Alche is built for the European market.

5. **Beyond weight:** Noom can't escape its diet-app identity. Alche was born as a longevity brand — no brand baggage to overcome.

---

## When They Win Over You (Honest)

- When the customer needs GLP-1 medication access — Alche doesn't prescribe.
- When the customer specifically needs behavioral change coaching for weight management.
- When the customer's employer offers Noom through B2B wellness benefits (free to employee).
- When the customer values a free tier to try before committing.
- When Noom's scale and AI features (Face Scan, Glucose Forecasting) attract tech-forward users.

## Key Vulnerability to Exploit

**Trust deficit.** A $62M lawsuit settlement for billing practices, Sitejabber 1.4 stars, BBB complaints, and Trustpilot reports of $837 surprise charges have created a deep trust problem. In the longevity space, trust is the core currency. Alche should position as the trustworthy alternative: "We earn your trust every day at the cafe. We don't need a $62M lawsuit to learn how to treat customers."
