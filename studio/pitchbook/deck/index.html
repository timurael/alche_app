THE PROBLEM · 02
§ 01 · The problem

The longevity market is loud, clinical, and expensive.

Europe has a €50B+ supplement market and 66% of Germans taking vitamins — but no editorial curator worth trusting.

Consumers bounce between Amazon algorithms, pharmacist defaults, and influencer chaos. The one thing that should be simple — a daily longevity protocol that actually works — is the one thing nobody has solved.

Overwhelm

Thousands of SKUs, conflicting claims, no clear starting point. 73% of consumers report decision paralysis when buying health products online.

Trust gap

Influencer wellness trust is collapsing. 93% read reviews; 85% won't buy without ratings. No source of editorial authority in European wellness.

Gatekept pricing

Personalized longevity plans cost €1,000+ through concierge services. Everyone else gets generic advice and Amazon.

No editorial authority

Europe has no Goop, no Thrive Market, no Wirecutter-for-wellness. The editorial layer that makes commerce trustworthy simply doesn't exist yet.

What's missing
A free, intelligent, editorially curated way to get from "I want to live longer and feel better" to "these three products arrive on Thursday."

THE INSIGHT · 03
§ 02 · The insight

We give intelligence away.
And monetize the decision.

Longevity shouldn't feel like a lab report.
