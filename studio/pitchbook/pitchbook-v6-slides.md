## SLIDE 2 — The Problem

**Headline:** The longevity market is loud, clinical, and expensive.

**Body:**
Europe has a €50B+ supplement market and 66% of Germans taking vitamins — but no editorial curator worth trusting. Consumers bounce between Amazon algorithms, pharmacist defaults, and influencer chaos. The one thing that should be simple — a daily longevity protocol that actually works — is the one thing nobody has solved.

**Four frictions:**
- **Overwhelm** — thousands of SKUs, conflicting claims, no clear starting point
- **Trust gap** — 73% of consumers feel overwhelmed buying health products online
- **Gatekept pricing** — personalized longevity plans cost €1,000+ through concierge services
- **No editorial authority** — Europe has no Goop, no Thrive Market, no Wirecutter-for-wellness

**What's missing:** A free, intelligent, editorially curated way to get from "I want to live longer and feel better" to "these three products arrive on Thursday."

## SLIDE 13 — The Flywheel

**Headline:** Free content compounds into owned revenue.

## SLIDE 19 — Why Now

**Five drivers:**

- **AI maturity** — LLMs can now synthesize research and personalize at scale. First generation of consumer wellness where intelligence isn't the bottleneck.
- **European white space** — AG1, Ritual, Thrive Market are US-first with minimal EU presence. No European brand combines science + curation + commerce.
- **German trust shift** — 83% of Germans trust their pharmacist (the highest-trusted health source). Alche replicates that trust infrastructure for the digital native.
- **Supplement boom + content collapse** — €50B+ European supplement market. Influencer trust collapsing. Editorial authority is the moat.
- **Post-subscription** — consumers are over SaaS fatigue. Retail + content + optional thin membership is what works (Costco: 92.9% renewal. Amazon Prime: 2.3x spend).
