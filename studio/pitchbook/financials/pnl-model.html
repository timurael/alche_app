What makes or breaks the model
Biggest swing factor: own-brand mix in Year 3. At 20% own-brand mix, gross margin stalls at 52%. At 40% (base), 60%. At 55% (optimistic), 65%. Own-brand launch timing (Q1 2027) is the EBITDA gate.

Second lever: 90-day reorder rate. Below 35% = CAC never pays back. Above 45% = compounding LTV. Content + subscribe-and-save are the levers.

Membership: Thin, intentional. 12% conversion at €7/mo = €3.2M Y3 revenue. But the real value is commitment (Costco: 73% of gross profit from 1.9% of revenue).

Key risks
Reorder rate below 35%

Base requires 45% 90-day reorder. Industry comp: Ritual 92%, AG1 high-retention DTC. Mitigation: subscribe-and-save at checkout, AI-driven protocol updates, content touchpoints at Day 14 / 30 / 60.

Own-brand launch slips past Q1 2027

Every quarter of slip costs ~€1.2M EBITDA in Y3. Mitigation: supplier conversations already underway, first SKU (Magnesium) is BVL-registered with multiple copackers in DACH.

Regulatory — ashwagandha + health claims

German BfR watchlist restricts ashwagandha. Health claims must use EU-approved verbatim language. Mitigation: curated shelf launches with no watchlist SKUs; legal review integrated into supplier onboarding.

Sunday Natural (Berlin, ~€100M rev)

Nearest direct competitor. Mitigation: we're not vertical-only — we're editorial commerce. Their model is own-brand closed-loop; ours is curated + AI + membership. Different game.

CAC inflation

Meta/Google CAC rising. Mitigation: content-led acquisition (SEO compounds), Berlin community events, podcast partnerships — all lower-CAC channels. Paid is the accelerator, not the engine.

Membership <8% conversion

Base 12%, conservative 8%. Even at 8% membership is ~€2M Y3. The commitment effect matters more than the revenue — mitigation is ensuring membership perks are visible and valuable (discounted pricing, early access, digital twin).
