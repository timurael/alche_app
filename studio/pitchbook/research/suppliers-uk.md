# Alche Supplier Due Diligence — UK Cluster

**Date:** 2026-04-24
**Scope:** 5 UK longevity / functional nutrition brands assessed for Alche's Berlin curated-retail launch
**Sister research:** Nordic cluster (see `suppliers-nordic.md`)

---

## Executive summary

Of the 5 UK brands, **Artah and Vivo Life are the strongest immediate leads** — Artah is funded for a 2025 EU launch (€2.85M Five Seasons Ventures round) and Vivo Life already has a German-language storefront (vivolife.de), a Polish return/returns facility, and DHL Germany as its last-mile carrier. **Viridian** offers the deepest catalog (250+ SKUs) and an actual International Sales Manager (Martin Clarkson) but sells exclusively through independent health stores — partnership-fit is strong. **Wild Nutrition** is a B Corp powerhouse for women's health but shows no EU trade infrastructure and has a difficult history with Continental distributors. **Innermost** has the cleanest premium-retail DNA (Harrods, Selfridges, Liberty, John Lewis) and an explicit retailer program with flexible MOQs — the easiest "yes."

**Single biggest shared risk: Ashwagandha.** Wild Nutrition, Artah, Vivo Life all lead with KSM-66 ashwagandha products. The EU HoA Working Group has recommended an Article 8 procedure against ashwagandha in food supplements; Denmark has banned it and used RASFF alerts against UK+German products. Germany (BfR Communication 39/2024) has flagged it as a potential health risk. Any ashwagandha SKU sold to Berlin faces an active regulatory cloud.

**Post-Brexit shared risk:** Only **Vivo Life** has mainland-EU infrastructure (Poland warehouse footprint, DHL Germany last-mile). The other four ship from the UK — meaning every pallet triggers UK-EU customs, duty on supplements (generally 0% under TCA with preferential origin but VAT-deferred in DE), and realistic 5–10 day lead times vs. <2 days for intra-EU.

## Post-Brexit customs analysis (all 5 brands)

| Brand | Ships from | Customs exposure | Est. lead time to Berlin |
|-------|-----------|------------------|--------------------------|
| Artah | UK (ShipBob) | UK→DE every pallet; 0% duty on food supplements under UK-EU TCA with preferential origin documentation; import VAT 19% (deferrable via IOSS for DTC / postponed accounting for B2B) | 5–10 business days |
| Wild Nutrition | UK | Same as Artah; direct shipments only | 5–10 business days |
| Innermost | UK (possibly Dublin IE node, unconfirmed) | UK→DE if shipped from London; intra-EU if Dublin node is real | 5–10 days (UK) / 3–5 days (IE) |
| Viridian | UK (with Poland distribution partner per ZoomInfo) | UK→DE direct, or via Viridian Polska Poland → DE (intra-EU, no customs) | 5–10 (UK) / 2–4 (PL) |
| **Vivo Life** | **UK + Poland returns; Germany DHL last-mile** | **Lowest friction — likely already consolidating in EU before last-mile** | **2–3 business days** |

**Key point:** Under the UK-EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement, supplements classified as food have **0% tariff duty** if goods meet preferential rules of origin (made/processed in UK). But: EORI numbers, customs declarations, and import VAT remain mandatory. All UK brands must provide:
- Commercial invoice with HS code (2106.90 typically for food supplements)
- Statement on origin (EUR.1 / invoice declaration under UK-EU TCA)
- German importer EORI (Alche's or supplier's)
- BVL product notification confirmation

**Alche action:** Get a German EORI early. Use postponed VAT accounting so import VAT doesn't hit cashflow. Budget ~€150–€300 per pallet in customs broker fees unless supplier handles DDP (Delivered Duty Paid).

## Regulatory dealbreakers

**Ashwagandha (watchlist):**
- **Wild Nutrition KSM-66 Ashwagandha Plus** — bestseller, direct exposure
- **Artah Enhanced Nootropics** — 1,000mg KSM-66 daily dose
- **Vivo Life WHOLE** meal replacement — contains ashwagandha root powder
- **Innermost Rise / Reset capsules** — verify; likely contain adaptogens

**Recommendation:** Launch Alche without ashwagandha SKUs in the opening assortment. Wait for EU Art. 8 procedure outcome (expected 2026–27). The risk of a sudden recall + RASFF alert outweighs the revenue upside. All 5 brands have plenty of low-risk SKUs — prioritise magnesium, vitamin D3, omega-3, protein, collagen, NAD+.

**Health claims to strip for EU labels:**
- "Cognitive support," "stress relief," "adrenal" — all cognitive/stress claims linked to ashwagandha, rhodiola, panax ginseng are NOT EFSA-authorised
- "Metabolic reset," "detox" — disease-implication claims prohibited
- Only EFSA Annex claims allowed (e.g., "magnesium contributes to normal muscle function")

**EU entity status:**
- **Vivo Life:** partial (Poland logistics, vivolife.eu EU storefront, DHL DE last-mile)
- **Innermost:** possible Dublin reference, unconfirmed
- **Artah, Wild Nutrition, Viridian:** no EU entity — all ship from UK

**Label translation costs (German):** Expect €0.05–€0.15 per unit for relabelling at the brand's cost, or Alche negotiates reimbursement via a first-order label credit. Standard industry practice: brand absorbs on first PO as market-entry investment.

## Gaps / concerns

1. **Ashwagandha cloud** — three of five brands have hero ashwagandha products. Alche should skip these SKUs at launch until EU Art. 8 decision lands.
2. **Only Vivo Life has real EU logistics** — the other four ship from UK. Plan for 5–10 day replenishment cycles and higher per-pallet customs overhead.
3. **Wild Nutrition's distributor history** — burned by a Scandinavian distributor who stockpiled; will be cautious. Lead with transparency + small pilot.
4. **Innermost founder opacity** — unlike Rhian / Henrietta / Cheryl / Josh, Innermost's founder isn't a public figure. Harder personal-angle outreach; compensate via clean trade-program onboarding.
5. **Viridian selectivity** — they sell "exclusively through independent health stores" as brand principle. Alche fits that definition (curated, not mass), but expect a screening conversation about channel protection. Don't pitch as mass-retail — pitch as curated editorial.
6. **No confirmed BVL notifications** for any of the 5 brands' full SKU ranges. Assume Alche (or supplier under DDP terms) must file per-SKU notifications before first sale in DE. Budget €150–€400 per SKU notification.
7. **No confirmed exclusivity agreements** for any brand with a single EU retailer surfaced in research. Clean slate on that axis for all 5.
