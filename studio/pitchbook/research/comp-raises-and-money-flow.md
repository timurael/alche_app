## 7. Single Biggest Insight on European Wellness Capital Flow in 2026

> **European consumer health VC has just become a real category in 2024–2025 — and it's concentrated in a dozen hands that already talk to each other.** For the first time in EU history, there is a €100m fund (naturalX) built _exclusively_ for consumer health at the Series A stage, plus a €107m impact fund (Eka Fund II) thesis-framed around "reactive → preventative healthcare," plus a £200m second Fund from Btomorrow, plus Five Seasons writing 4+ checks into the exact Alche category in 18 months. Total dry powder aimed at this intersection has gone from ~€50m pre-2023 to **~€450m+ in 2024-2026**. The comps (Bioniq at $150m Herbalife exit, Huel at €1B Danone, Sunday Natural at €800m CVC) have given big-PE and strategics a shot to practice buying European wellness — which means Series A/B money now has an exit thesis it didn't have in 2022. **The window for Alche to enter the cap table of a longevity-brand defining VC (Five Seasons, Redrice, naturalX, Eka, dmg) is open _right now_ and will compress by end of 2026 as those funds fill their 8-12 slots per vintage.**

The second-order insight: **DACH-specific longevity/wellness capital is thinner than UK capital but more thesis-aligned.** naturalX + Cherry + HV Capital + Atlantic Labs + IBB + Rheingau = the DACH stack. Alche being Berlin-based is a material advantage here vs. a London-based competitor pitching the same thesis — naturalX and Cherry both want a local Berlin longevity brand to back.
