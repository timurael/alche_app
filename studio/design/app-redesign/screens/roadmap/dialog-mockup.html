SCREEN 20 — ROADMAP · THE ARC

A longevity roadmap is a story, not a Kanban board.

The current Roadmap wears architectural costume: a blueprint grid, a "VER 5.0" badge, "LAT 34.05" coordinates (actually Los Angeles — the member is in Berlin), "GRID REF A-142" that indexes nothing. The audit called this editorial typography confused for editorial meaning. The dialog-first cut strips every pixel of fiction and writes the thing the screen was always trying to be: chapters of your longevity. Each phase becomes a titled chapter with a real date range and an authored summary in Alche's voice. A "you are here" badge anchors the member. Nothing decorative earns a line.

Below the read sits an honest mono strip with three real facts: started date, current week, time to next chapter. The timeline itself is a hairline rule with three node states — filled (done), ringed (active), dashed (locked). One escape hatch. No Kanban.
