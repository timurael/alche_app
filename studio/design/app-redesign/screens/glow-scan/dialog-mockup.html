SCREEN · GLOWSCAN

The camera should be quiet. The read comes after.

A 60-second mock skin scan does not need a sci-fi prop. Alche watches — observationally, never clinically — and speaks a single appearance-based line before the capture. All numeric readouts, fake camera metadata, and fictional mode tabs are cut. One action: capture.

  • CUT
    Rotating dashed circle, scan-line animation, breathing red "LIVE ANALYSIS" dot, "S: 1/200 ISO 120" fake camera metadata, floating "HYDRATION 64%" and "COLLAGEN INDEX 0.8" numeric callouts during scan, the three fictional mode tabs (Texture / Structural / Thermal), the grid overlay, "SCANNING EPIDERMIS..." progress bar. None of them carried real data or a real capability.
  • KEPT
    The viewfinder as a concept — reduced to four corner brackets and a faint dashed silhouette hint. Dark surface (the camera is the canvas). Capture as the single primary action. Front camera only.
  • ADDED
    A two-line italic read above the viewfinder — "Hold it steady. / I'll look for 30 seconds." Observational voice, never clinical. One unified "Sample · Mock Scan" tag top-right (replacing the scattered indicators). A quiet mono meta row with real-only data (camera state, light level). A previous-echo line at the bottom that previews what results will sound like: "Your skin looked well-hydrated…" — appearance-based, not numeric.
Trade-off: users who expected a bright sci-fi scanner get a slower, quieter interface. But the mock can no longer over-promise — the only numbers on this screen are the scan duration and the time-since-last.
