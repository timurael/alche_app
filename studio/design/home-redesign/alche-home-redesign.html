I · Diagnosis

The current home confesses
its own confusion.

Twelve zones on one screen. A poetic hero, a cycle band, a bio card, a ritual CTA, a 2×2 grid, a hairline divider — and then seven functional cards stacked below. The divider is a tell: editorial stuff above, functional stuff below, we didn't know where to put the rest. Five problems, specifically.

Five issues
01
Primary action

No clear "what do I do right now?"

The ritual card wants to be the hero. The 2×2 grid wants to be the hero. The functional stack wants to exist. Three primary actions cancel each other out; the user scrolls and scrolls.

02
Inclusivity

The cycle band assumes menstruation.

Alche's own founder is AMAB and queer. Defaulting every member to a 28-day estrogen cycle excludes a meaningful share of the target audience — and quietly signals whose body the app was designed around.

03
Metric vs. trigger

Bio Age is vanity, not action.

A large italic number at the top of the home is aesthetically strong and behaviourally weak. It's a result, not a decision. On a home screen, every element should either tell the user what to do or get out of the way.

04
Hierarchy

The divider is a confession.

A hairline separating "hero stuff" from "the rest" is the UI admitting it couldn't find a shared rhythm. A good home has one conceptual frame — not two stapled together.

05
Voice

"Approaching Luminous." is empty poetry.

It looks like copy. It reads like filler. A line that occupies the most valuable pixel real estate on screen must either carry information or be cut — not loiter beautifully.

II · Direction A

Ritual-first.

The whole home is today's ritual moment. One question on waking — what should I do right now? — answered in the first fold, uncompromisingly.

A · Ritual-first

The ritual is the room.

The hero isn't a tile on the home screen — it is the home screen. Smoke gradient takes ~60% of the frame. One verb: Begin. Everything else is one tap deeper.

Added · Removed · Reprioritized
Added

Time-of-day overline (08:42 · Morning Window). Body-inclusive "why-now" diagnostic that works for any physiology.

Removed

2×2 quick grid. Bio-age card. Default cycle band. "Approaching Luminous" filler poetry.

Trade-off

At-a-glance dashboard is gone. Credits, bookings, protocol status live one tap deeper.

III · Direction B

Signal-first.

You open Alche and you see where your body is right now. Actions emerge from signals, not from a grid of random choices. The editorial-longevity take on a Whoop home.

B · Signal-first

Your body, read aloud in italics.

Two typographic lead signals, six tight supporting rows with CSS sparklines, and a body-mode switcher that makes cycle opt-in. Kinfolk magazine spread, not dashboard chaos.

Added · Removed · Reprioritized
Added

Typographic lead signals (Readiness · Bio Age) side-by-side. Six-row signal column with sparklines. Body Mode strip — Menstrual / Androgen / Off.

Removed

"Approaching Luminous" hero. 2×2 quick grid. Full-width smoke ritual card. The hero/functional divider.

Trade-off

First-time members see numbers before narrative. More Bloomberg than Kinfolk on day one — until the italics earn their meaning.

IV · Direction C

Dialog-first.

Alche speaks. The home is a single conversational card — a read, an opinion, one action. Dashboard → dialog. This is what longevity apps look like in 2027, not 2022.

C · Dialog-first

A letter from your companion.

Three italic lines stating what today is. One primary action. A redirect instead of a dismiss. A tiny "Browse instead" escape hatch for members who aren't in the mood to be read.

Added · Removed · Reprioritized
Added

Single italic three-line Alche read. Space Mono data strip. Continuity echo from yesterday. "Browse instead" escape hatch.

Removed

Editorial hero. Cycle band. Bio-age card. Ritual card. 2×2 grid. Functional stack. The hero/functional divider.

Trade-off

Users who open wanting to do arrive one tap late. Alche's opinion comes first, always. The AI persona cannot be switched off.

V · Comparison

Not "which is best."
Which is right.

Each direction optimizes for a different posture. Read the matrix through the lens of the member Alche is actually trying to build for — and the version of the app that earns a second open tomorrow.

VI · Recommendation

Ship A first. Evolve toward C.

Direction A is the cheapest
path to a right home.

The ritual card DNA already exists in the codebase. Ritual-first uses it as the room instead of a tile. It answers "what do I do now?" cleanly, restores AMAB/queer inclusivity by moving cycle to a preference, and removes the editorial/functional divider without losing the Kinfolk voice. It's the shortest flight from here to a home that confesses nothing.

Direction C is the true north for a Longevity companion in 2027 — but it needs a real opinion-generator behind it. Ship that after the read engine is trained on member data, not before. Direction B stays as a pattern library for Profile and the future Biomarkers / Digital Twin screens — signal-density is a language Alche will need, just not at the front door.

  • Step 01 Lock Direction A as the home target. Timu reviews; "A go" commits.
  • Step 02 Tighten A's secondary rows: which 1–2 utilities live below the hero (next booking vs. credits vs. protocol progress).
  • Step 03 Port A into HomeView.swift — keep the smoke gradient, retire the 2×2 grid, move cycle to Profile settings under "Body tracking."
  • Step 04 Ship to TestFlight, measure: did members open a ritual within the first session? That's the only metric that counts.
