Your Excel Template, Decoded

The investor sent you an empty spreadsheet with categories designed for a product company. Here is exactly how alche's business maps to each row. The left column is what the Excel says. The right column is what you put there.

Products (Rows 4-12)

4 product lines × (units + euros each)
The template expects physical products. alche sells both physical products AND services. Map them as 4 "product lines":
  • Line 1 Curated Longevity Products (supplements, functional foods, tools)
  • Line 2 LED Therapy Sessions (units = sessions, euros = session revenue)
  • Line 3 Blood Panel Facilitations (units = panels, euros = panel revenue)
  • Line 1 Curated Longevity Potions (units = batches/jars, euros = price)
  • Line 2 LED Therapy (units = sessions, euros = price)
  • Line 3 CGM Bundle (units = shipped kits, euros = price)
  • Line 4 Events & Workshops (units = total attendees, euros = ticket revenue)
  • Line 5 Restaurant Partnerships (units = trackable bookings, euros = commission)
  • Line 6 Doctor Directory SaaS (units = listed doctors, euros = monthly fee)

Subscriptions (Rows 14-17)

3 subscription tiers: A, B, Others
This is alche's core business. The 3 rows map perfectly to 3 pricing tiers:
  • A Core Tier — EUR 19/month (biomarker dashboard, wearable sync)
  • B Pro Tier — EUR 49/month (personalized protocols, AI insights)
  • Others Premium Tier — EUR 99/month (blood panels, 1:1 reviews, CGM)

COGS (Rows 22-35)

Up to 13 line items for direct costs
Everything that costs money ONLY when you make a sale. If revenue is zero, COGS is zero.
  • Row 23 Hosting & Infrastructure (servers, cloud)
  • Row 24 API Costs (wearable syncs, AI models)
  • Row 25 Payment Processing (Stripe ~2.9%)
  • Row 26 Product Wholesale Cost (52% of retail)
  • Row 27 Product Shipping (EUR 4/unit)
  • Row 28 Product Packaging (EUR 1.50/unit)
  • Row 29 LED Practitioner Fees (EUR 18/session)
  • Row 30 Blood Panel Lab + Practitioner (EUR 55/panel)
  • Row 31 Event Facilitator Fees (EUR 200/event)
  • Row 32 Service Consumables (EUR 4/session)
  • Row 33 Equipment Depreciation (EUR 200/mo)

Operations (Rows 41-77)

6 categories: Payroll, R&D, S&M, G&A, Management, Travel
Everything that costs money whether you sell anything or not. The "lights on" costs.
  • Payroll Founder salaries (EUR 4,750 each fully loaded) + CTO (M18+, seed hire)
  • R&D Dev agency, dev tools, QA, staging servers
  • S&M Paid ads, content tools, influencer, PR
  • G&A Rent, legal, insurance, Steuerberater, office
  • Management Software tools, advisory, misc
  • Travel Investor meetings, conferences, transport
THE FORMULA CHAIN
Total Income (Row 18) = Products + Subscriptions
Total COGS (Row 36) = Sum of all direct costs
Gross Profit (Row 38) = Total Income − Total COGS
Total Expenses (Row 78) = Payroll + R&D + S&M + G&A + Management + Travel
Total P&L (Row 85) = Gross Profit − Total Expenses
Section 02

Vocabulary Bootcamp

You said you're starting from zero. Here are the 20 terms you absolutely need to know before touching that spreadsheet. No fluff, no jargon-to-explain-jargon. Each term includes what it means for alche specifically.

Revenue
Umsatz
Money customers pay you. Period. Before you subtract anything. The very first number on a P&L. Also called "top line" because it's literally at the top.
alche: 4 streams — subscriptions (EUR 19/49/99 per month), product sales (avg EUR 35), services (LED, blood panels, events), and physical space (Phase 2, EUR 0 now).
COGS
Herstellungskosten / Wareneinsatz
Cost of Goods Sold. What it costs you to deliver the thing you sold. Sell nothing = COGS is zero. These costs move in lockstep with revenue.
alche: Hosting (EUR 350/mo), Stripe fees (2.9%), product wholesale (52% of price), practitioner fees (EUR 18-55/session), packaging, shipping.
Gross Profit
Rohgewinn / Bruttogewinn
Revenue minus COGS. This is the money left after you've paid for delivering your product. It's what you have to cover salaries, rent, and everything else.
alche: Target ~72% gross margin Year 1. For every EUR 100 in revenue, EUR 72 remains after direct costs. Subscriptions: 85-90% margin. Products: 40-55%.
OpEx
Betriebsausgaben
Operating Expenses. Everything it costs to run the business that isn't COGS. Salaries, rent, marketing, legal, software. You pay these even if you sell nothing.
alche: Biggest item = founder salaries at EUR 9,500/mo (2 x EUR 3,800 gross + 25% employer costs). Then dev agency, rent, marketing.
Burn Rate
Verbrennungsrate
How much cash you spend per month beyond what you earn. Net burn = expenses minus revenue. At pre-launch, burn = roughly your total expenses because revenue is near zero.
alche: ~EUR 15,000/mo at launch. Decreases as revenue grows. Target: EUR 0 burn by Month 12 (break-even).
Runway
Reichweite (cash)
How many months until you run out of money. Cash in bank divided by monthly burn rate. When runway hits zero, the company dies unless it raises more money.
alche: EUR 500K raise / ~EUR 15K burn = 33 months. After startup costs (EUR 247K): ~24 months. That's good. Pre-seed standard is 12-18.
MRR
Monatlich wiederkehrender Umsatz
Monthly Recurring Revenue. Only counts subscription revenue that repeats every month. Product sales and one-time services don't count. Investors love MRR because it's predictable.
alche: At 145 subs (M12): Core 75 x EUR 19 + Pro 55 x EUR 49 + Premium 15 x EUR 99 = EUR 5,605 MRR.
ARR
Jährlich wiederkehrender Umsatz
Annual Recurring Revenue. MRR times 12. This is the number used for SaaS company valuations. "We're at EUR 67K ARR" means MRR is ~EUR 5,600.
alche: At M12: EUR 5,605 MRR x 12 = EUR 67,260 ARR. At EUR 2.5M cap, that's ~37x ARR multiple (high for pre-seed but normal for pre-revenue).
CAC
Kundenakquisitionskosten
Customer Acquisition Cost. Total marketing spend divided by new customers acquired. How much it costs to get one person to pay you.
alche: Target EUR 40-80 blended. Content-first strategy keeps CAC low. At EUR 1,400/mo marketing and 25 new subs: EUR 56 CAC.
LTV
Kundenlebenszeitwert
Lifetime Value. Total money one customer will ever pay you. ARPU times how many months they stay. The higher the better. Must be much bigger than CAC.
alche: EUR 49 ARPU x 8-12 months retention = EUR 392-588 LTV. LTV:CAC ratio of 5:1 to 15:1. Investors want 3:1 minimum.
Churn
Abwanderungsrate / Kündigungsrate
The percentage of subscribers who cancel each month. The silent killer of subscription businesses. If 8% churn monthly, you lose 8 of every 100 subscribers.
alche: Model at 8% monthly churn. Industry reality: 77% of health app users leave by Day 3. alche's curation model targets stickier engagement.
ARPU
Durchschnittlicher Umsatz pro Nutzer
Average Revenue Per User. Total revenue divided by paying users. Tells you how much the average customer pays per month.
alche: Weighted target EUR 49/mo. Core (EUR 19) pulls it down, Premium (EUR 99) pulls it up. Tier mix determines actual ARPU.
Gross Margin
Bruttomarge
Gross Profit as a percentage of Revenue. Shows how many cents you keep per euro after direct costs. Higher = better business model.
alche: Target 72% blended Y1, growing to 80% Y3 as subscription share increases. SaaS benchmark: 70-85%. E-commerce: 30-50%.
Break-even
Gewinnschwelle / Break-even-Punkt
The month when revenue equals expenses. No more burning cash. Every euro of additional revenue is profit. The most important milestone for pre-seed investors.
alche: Month 12, approximately 145 paying subscribers. EUR 142K of the EUR 500K raise remains as buffer.
EBITDA
same in German
Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization. Your operating profit from core business, ignoring financing and accounting adjustments. The "true" operating performance.
alche: Negative until near break-even. EBITDA = Gross Profit - OpEx. Turns positive when subscription growth outpaces expense growth.
Wandeldarlehen
Convertible Loan
A loan that converts into company shares at the next funding round. You borrow EUR 500K now; when you raise a Series Seed, that EUR 500K becomes equity at a discount.
alche: EUR 500K at EUR 2.5M valuation cap, 20% discount. Interest ~3%/year = EUR 1,250/mo accrued (shows on P&L but no cash leaves).
Depreciation
Abschreibung (AfA)
Spreading a big purchase over time on the P&L. Buy a EUR 12,000 device, use it 5 years = EUR 200/month expense. No cash leaves your bank each month; it's an accounting entry.
alche: Equipment EUR 200/mo + IT EUR 85/mo = EUR 285/mo pre-seed. Phase 2 adds space buildout depreciation.
GmbH
Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung
German limited liability company. Standard startup structure. Requires EUR 25,000 registered capital (EUR 12,500 paid at formation). Taxed at ~30% combined rate on profits.
alche: Berlin-registered GmbH. Combined tax: 15% Körperschaftsteuer + 0.825% Soli + ~14.35% Gewerbesteuer = ~30%. Zero tax on losses (they carry forward).
Unit Economics
Stückökonomie
The revenue and cost of ONE customer. If one customer pays EUR 49/mo, costs EUR 3/mo to serve, and EUR 56 to acquire, is that customer profitable? Unit economics answers this.
alche: Pro sub: EUR 49 revenue - EUR 3 COGS = EUR 46 contribution. Pays back EUR 56 CAC in 1.2 months. Then ~10 months of pure contribution.
Freemium Conversion
Freemium-Konversion
The percentage of free users who upgrade to paid. Typical for health apps: 2-5%. If you have 2,000 free users and 100 paying, that's 5% conversion.
alche: Model at 3-5% conversion. 500 free users x 5% = 25 paid subs. Content-first strategy builds free base, then converts through depth of value.

Section 05

How to Fill It

Step-by-step instructions for getting these numbers into the investor's Excel file.

Click "Copy All Numbers for Excel"

This copies the entire 24-month P&L table to your clipboard as tab-separated values (TSV) — the format Excel expects for paste operations.

Open the investor's Budget P&L.xlsx

Go to the "Budget" sheet. Click on cell B1 (the first month column). This is where Month 1 data begins.

Paste (Ctrl+V / Cmd+V)

The numbers will fill into the correct cells. Excel maps tabs to columns and newlines to rows automatically.

Verify the row labels match

Check that "COGS" numbers land in the COGS section (rows 22-36), "Operations" in rows 41-77, etc. If rows are offset, adjust by pasting section-by-section — use the "Copy Section" buttons in the table.

Add row labels for COGS and Operations sub-items

The investor's template has blank sub-rows. Type in the label names from the "Template Map" section above (e.g., "Hosting & Infrastructure" in the COGS section, "Timu (CEO)" in Payroll, etc.).

Add the "Total 2 years" column

Column Z in the Excel has "Total 2 years." For each row, enter a SUM formula: =SUM(B[row]:Y[row]). The totals are also shown in the rightmost column of this table.

Review and send

Double-check the Gross Profit row (should be positive by M8-M10) and Total P&L row (should approach zero by M12). Save and send to the investor.
