What Investors Will Ask (and Your Answers)

"Where's the break-even?"
EBITDA break-even around M11-13. Cumulative cash break-even (where cash exceeds EUR 500K starting point) around M23-24. The business is profitable operationally well before M24.
"Is EUR 500K enough?"
Yes. Minimum cash never goes below ~EUR 237-260K (around M12, the deepest trough before revenue ramp catches up). Even in the bear case (0.6x revenue), cash stays positive. That said, the Cash Flow sheet shows ~EUR 218K less actual cash than the P&L suggests due to working capital tied up in inventory. Actual M24 cash is closer to EUR 377K.
"Why so much retail vs subscriptions?"
Physical space drives foot traffic. Walk-in customers buy products. Subscriptions are the retention engine (keep people coming back, collect data, personalize recommendations). Retail is the revenue engine. Think Aesop with a membership layer, not Netflix with a store.
"What about the doctor thing?"
Commission-based referral fees are illegal in Germany (StGB 299a/299b). We switched to a SaaS model at EUR 99/mo per clinic, following Doctolib precedent (EUR 139-475/mo). Smaller revenue but legally bulletproof.
"Why no CGM?"
ZOE dropped CGM hardware in Sept 2025. Supersapiens shut down Feb 2024. Hardware-as-a-service is dead industry-wide. We do software-only BYOD (bring your own device) via Dexcom API and Apple Health. 80-90% margins on software interpretation vs thin/negative margins on hardware logistics. The greyed-out EUR 0 row in the model shows this was deliberate.
"What's your biggest risk?"
Retail volume at M24: 4,600 units/month with only 339 subscribers. That means ~92% of product sales come from non-subscribers (walk-in, e-commerce, B2B). Mitigant: curated Berlin wellness space with high foot traffic + planned e-commerce expansion + potential B2B wholesale channel.
"How is this model different from typical pre-seed decks?"
  • 123 assumptions, every one sourced to research documents or expert audits
  • 7 expert persona audits (angel investor, tax advisor, operations manager, food safety inspector, growth CFO, compliance lawyer, HR manager) -- all findings incorporated
  • German-specific costs modeled: Gewerbesteuer, Nebenkosten, GEMA, HACCP, DSB (data protection officer), Rundfunkbeitrag, KSK levy, Verpackungsgesetz. Most pre-seed models skip all of these.
  • Cash Flow sheet showing the EUR 218K working capital gap that P&L hides
  • Tax provision follows actual German GmbH rules (~30% combined rate, only when cumulative profits turn positive)
  • Illegal revenue models caught and fixed (doctor commission → SaaS)

If They Push on Valuation Multiples

Subscription businesses: 8-15x ARR. Product commerce: 2-4x revenue. We're economically a product commerce business (only ~5% subscription revenue). But the subscription layer is the data moat and retention mechanism that makes the product commerce defensible. Frame it as: "product commerce margins with subscription economics on retention."
