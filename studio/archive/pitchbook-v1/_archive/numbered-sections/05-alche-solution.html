What Alche is
in one breath
Alche is a longevity lifestyle platform that integrates biomarker tracking, personalized protocols, curated products, and community into one membership -- with a flagship Berlin space as the physical anchor.

Part 2 Goes on the slide
The five-layer
data ecosystem
Each layer generates data that informs the next. The architecture isn't theoretical -- it's the structural reason Alche can personalize at scale while single-vertical competitors can't. An investor needs to see this as an integrated system, not a list of features.
01
Biomarker Layer
Blood panels, CGM glucose data, wearable metrics (sleep, HRV, activity). Longitudinal tracking -- 6 months of data is 6x more valuable than one snapshot. This is the diagnostic foundation that every protocol builds on.
02
Protocol Layer
Personalized plans generated from biomarker data + goals + lifestyle context. AI-guided, physician-informed. Not generic -- calibrated to the individual's biology and refined over time as new data comes in.
03
Product Layer
Curated supplements, functional potions, LED therapy sessions. Not manufactured by Alche -- curated and sold through the platform. Protocol-linked: the app recommends products based on the user's data, not a generic catalogue.
04
Community Layer
Accountability circles, shared challenges, event networks, protocol sharing. Community is not a feature bolted on -- it's a longevity intervention in itself (50% greater chance of longevity with strong social connections). The retention moat that AI can't replicate.
05
Experience Layer
The Berlin flagship space. LED skin rituals, functional smoothies, wellness events, researcher talks. Revenue-generating (not a cost center) and the physical anchor that creates habit loops pure digital cannot. Every in-store interaction feeds data back into the system.

Why this matters for the pitch

Single-vertical competitors own one layer. Oura owns the biomarker layer (wearables). InsideTracker owns blood panels. Restore owns recovery services. AG1 owns a supplement. Nobody connects all five. The integration is the product, and the integration is what makes personalization actually work -- because you can't personalize a protocol without data, and you can't sustain a protocol without community and physical touchpoints.

Part 4 Appendix / backup
We lead with science.
Five evidence pillars.
This is not a wellness brand that vibes its way to credibility. Every core intervention in the Alche platform is grounded in peer-reviewed research. The investor doesn't need all of this on the slide -- but they need to know it exists, and you need to be able to pull any of it in Q&A. Organized by evidence strength.
Pillar 1: Biological age is measurable -- and reversible
Strong Evidence
3.23-year reversal
An 8-week lifestyle program (diet, sleep, exercise, relaxation + supplemental probiotics/phytonutrients) produced a statistically significant decrease in epigenetic age. First RCT to demonstrate reversal through lifestyle intervention alone.
Fitzgerald et al. 2021, Aging (RCT, n=43, males 50-72, p=0.018)
Strong Evidence
4.60-year reversal in women
Extension study to women: 5 of 6 participants showed bio age reductions of 1.22 to 11.01 years. Validates the approach across genders -- broadening the addressable market beyond the male-dominated biohacker demographic.
Fitzgerald et al. 2023, Aging (case series, n=6, p=.039)

Why this matters for Alche

Epigenetic clocks are the most accurate measure of biological age, validated across almost all tissues and organs. Three generations of clocks exist (Horvath/Hannum for chronological age, PhenoAge/GrimAge for clinical biomarkers + mortality, DunedinPACE for pace of aging). Standard blood panels can identify longevity profiles decades before outcomes manifest (Murata et al. 2024, GeroScience, 35-year follow-up). This is the scientific foundation for biomarker-based bio age tracking as a core app feature.

Pillar 2: One-size-fits-all nutrition fails
Gold-Standard RCT
ZOE METHOD Trial
18-week app-based personalized nutrition using food scores, glucose/TG responses, microbiome, and health history: significant improvements in triglycerides, body weight, waist, HbA1c, and microbiome diversity vs USDA guidelines.
Berry et al. 2024, Nature Medicine (RCT, n=347, ages 41-70)
Landmark Study
PREDICT 1 -- 1,103 participants
World's largest nutrition study including identical twins. Large inter-individual variations in response to identical meals. Genetics explained less variation than expected -- gut microbiome and lifestyle more important.
Berry et al. 2020, Nature Medicine
Additional validation: Stanford POINTS Study (Gardner et al. 2023, JAMA Internal Medicine) showed personalized dietary scoring outperformed one-size-fits-all recommendations by 15-23% across all measured endpoints. The EU PREVENTOMICS Horizon 2020 project confirmed personalized nutrition works across diverse European dietary cultures. Directly validates Alche's app-delivered personalized model.
Pillar 3: Red light therapy -- our physical differentiator
Meta-Analysis Supported
Photobiomodulation
Photobiomodulation (PBM) at 630-670nm promotes collagen synthesis, reduces fine lines, and improves skin texture. Dose-dependent and non-invasive. Multiple reviews confirm efficacy for skin rejuvenation when applied at correct parameters.
Photobiomodulation literature, multiple systematic reviews
LED therapy is the physical-space anchor service. It's clinically validated, non-invasive, visually distinctive (the "Glow Scan" branding), and generates high-margin recurring bookings through the app. It's the experience that makes the Berlin space more than a cafe.
Pillar 4: Evidence varies widely. Honesty builds trust.
Strong Evidence
Omega-3
8-10% reduced cardiovascular mortality in meta-analyses. Well-established anti-inflammatory effects. Strongest supplement evidence base in longevity.
Strong Evidence
Vitamin D
Deficiency linked to increased mortality. Supplementation beneficial when deficient. Biomarker-guided dosing (blood test confirmation) is optimal.
Strong Evidence
Magnesium
Involved in 300+ enzymatic reactions. 50%+ of population deficient. Sleep quality, muscle function, metabolic health benefits well-documented.
Moderate Evidence
Creatine & NMN
Creatine: emerging evidence for cognitive function and neuroprotection beyond muscle. NMN/NAD+: promising preclinical data, human trials ongoing. Evidence growing but not yet conclusive.
Mixed Evidence
Resveratrol
Initial excitement from Sinclair's research. Human trials have been disappointing. Bioavailability challenges. Being transparent about limitations builds consumer trust -- this honesty is the competitive advantage.

The pitch angle

Alche focuses on interventions with the strongest evidence first (sauna, omega-3, personalized nutrition, community, exercise) and is transparent about mixed evidence (resveratrol, NMN). In a market flooded with AG1-style hype, honest communication builds trust with science-literate consumers. This isn't a weakness in the pitch -- it's a differentiator. Investors who've done diligence on AG1's 100+ FDA adverse event reports will recognize the value of credibility.

The investor takeaway on community

Community is Alche's moat -- not a feature, a core intervention. It simultaneously improves health outcomes (the product works better) and improves retention (the business works better). This is the only moat in the longevity space that doesn't get commoditized by AI, cheaper hardware, or bigger competitors entering the market. When the data layer gets cheaper, when AI interpretation gets better, when wearables get more accurate -- the companies that built genuine community and cultural identity are the ones still standing.

KNOW - DO - GET - BELONG
the product flywheel
This is the engine of the product once the app exists. Each step creates demand for the next. The user never reaches a dead end -- every action leads deeper into the ecosystem. The flywheel explains why engagement compounds rather than decays.
KNOW
Track your body. Biomarkers, wearable data, biological age.
-->
DO
Follow your protocol. LED, nutrition, recovery, movement.
-->
GET
Buy what you need. Supplements, potions, functional products.
-->
BELONG
Join your people. Circles, events, shared challenges.
-->
KNOW
Better data. Repeat.
Why the loop compounds
KNOW creates motivation to DO. DO creates need to GET. GET creates belonging through shared rituals. BELONG generates better data (community accountability, longer tracking, more consistent engagement) which loops back to KNOW. Each full rotation makes the next one stickier.
Revenue at every node
KNOW: subscription fees for biomarker dashboards and wearable sync. DO: service bookings (LED, recovery). GET: product margins (supplements, potions, curated retail). BELONG: event ticketing, premium community access. No dead-end in the revenue architecture.

Part 7 Investor Q&A prep
What we're actually saying
and the honest caveats
Every solution slide has implicit claims. Here are ours, stated explicitly, with the honest counterarguments an investor will have. Don't dodge these -- lean into them.
What We're ClaimingThe Honest CaveatHow to Handle It
"Integration is the product." Integration startups have a graveyard. Forward ($657M, collapsed), Habit ($60M, dead), Gyroscope ($1M, niche). Full-stack is expensive. We're not building the stack -- we're curating it. Lab partnerships for testing. Heilpraktiker for regulatory bridge. Curated products, not manufactured. The physical space generates revenue, it's not a cost center.
"The app is the operating system." You have no app. You have no CTO. The app is a hypothesis. Correct. That's what the capital is for. MVP scope is deliberately minimal: membership, LED booking, digital menu, events, basic shop, in-store mode. Not trying to boil the ocean on day one.
"Community is the moat." Community takes years to build and is fragile. WeWork called itself a community company too. WeWork was a real estate company that used "community" as a valuation multiplier. Our community is the product -- it's a longevity intervention, not a marketing layer. WHOOP's data (2.3x retention for team members) and Blue Zones research both validate community as structurally different from co-working vibes.
"We lead with science." You're not scientists. You don't have a lab. Your physician partner's role isn't even formalized yet. We translate science, we don't produce it. The physician partnership is being formalized as medical advisor. Our competitive advantage is honest science communication -- telling people what works and what doesn't -- not original research.
"Five layers of data." At launch you'll have maybe one and a half layers. Blood panels and basic app data. The full stack is a multi-year build. Yes. The five-layer architecture is the vision, not the MVP. We launch with biomarker dashboard + LED booking + community events. Each funding round unlocks the next layer. The investor is buying into the architecture, with proof of concept in Berlin.

The pitch framing

"We're not claiming the full platform exists today. We're showing you the architecture that compounds over time and asking you to fund the proof of concept. Every layer we add makes the others more valuable. That's the thesis. Berlin is where we test it."
