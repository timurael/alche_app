Build the audience
before the product.

Alche launches as an editorial voice, not a product. We build trust and community through content before the app exists. By launch day, the hardest part of any startup -- getting people to care -- is already done.

The thesis

Most startups spend money to find customers. We find customers for free, then spend money to build what they already want. Our content-first strategy trades founder labor for capital: every video, newsletter, and community event builds an audience that converts directly into our waitlist, then our founding members, then our recurring revenue. The content flywheel runs on EUR 0 and generates the three things pre-seed investors care about most: proof of demand, proof of voice, and proof of community.

Two flywheels, sequenced
Flywheel 1 -- Running now

The Content Engine

Create radical-honesty science content → attract health-curious audience → they engage and share → we learn what they need → create sharper content. Each rotation builds trust and grows the audience that becomes the customer base. No product needed. No funding needed.

Flywheel 2 -- Activated by funding

The Product Engine

Know your body (data) → Do the protocols (action) → Get what you need (products) → Belong with others (community) → better data. Each layer creates demand for the next. Funding builds the product for an audience that already exists and already trusts us.

Channel strategy

Depth over breadth. Two primary channels, two amplifiers. English-first -- the global longevity community speaks English, Berlin is 15-20% expat, and English content has 10x the addressable audience of German.

Primary: Short-Form Video
TikTok + Instagram Reels — @alche.space

Radical honesty science content. We read peer-reviewed studies and give the real take -- industry-funded vs. independent results, what works and what doesn't. 3-5 posts per week. Pattern-breaking format: the first content piece is a collagen deep dive across 23 studies where we show that only industry-funded research shows positive results. This IS the brand voice.

Secondary: Newsletter
Weekly email — Substack or Beehiiv

One deep dive per week. Written version of the video thesis. Captures emails -- the only owned asset. Social algorithms change; email lists compound. Free tier builds audience, paid tier follows. This is where the most engaged followers live and where conversion happens.

Amplifier: YouTube
Monthly "Alche Reviewed" long-form

10-20 minute deep research episodes. "Does collagen actually work? We read 23 studies." These are evergreen pillars that rank in search for years -- the SEO moat that no short-form competitor builds. One per month, compounds indefinitely.

Amplifier: Berlin Events
Quarterly "Alche Salon" — EUR 200-500/event

Community events at existing Berlin venues. Science talks over wine. One evening generates a month of content (film everything). Doubles as customer discovery -- face-to-face with target users. Physician partner presents for medical credibility. Biohackers Berlin meetup (2,213 members) is the warm audience.

Unit Economics & Conviction

Why this works.

The economics of content-first acquisition, the virality mechanics, and the case for why this audience converts.

Customer acquisition: EUR 0 to start

Content-first means our acquisition cost in months 1-9 is effectively zero -- we trade labor for capital. Every follower, subscriber, and waitlist signup is earned through content, not bought through ads. When funding arrives, we amplify what already works: boost the video that got 50K views organically, sponsor the event format that filled 80 seats. We never spend on untested channels.

Post-funding allocation: 15-20% of raise (EUR 50-100K) to acquisition. But exclusively on amplifying proven organic winners. The content engine identifies what converts before we spend a euro on paid distribution.

Organic virality mechanics

Shareable format

Honest, surprising science takes make the sharer look informed. "Industry-funded collagen studies show results, independent ones don't" gets screenshotted and sent to friends. The content does the distribution.

Identity signal

Following @alche.space becomes a badge: "I care about aging well, but I'm not weird about it." The anti-optimization-bro identity. People share signals that flatter them.

Ritual participation

#dailyalche creates a low-barrier mechanic: post your one longevity thing today. Community members discover each other through the tag. The community grows itself.

The one channel that changes everything

TikTok. One viral science video generates 100K+ views and 2-5K followers overnight -- compressing six months of organic growth into one hit. The algorithm rewards exactly what we're built for: pattern-breaking, controversial-but-honest, science-backed short content. Every successful content-first company has one disproportionate acquisition channel. For Alche, it's short-form video where our voice is the product and the algorithm is the distribution.

Why this audience converts

By the time we launch the product, our audience has three characteristics that make conversion near-certain:

Trust is pre-built. They've consumed 6-9 months of honest, science-first content. They've seen us admit when something doesn't work. They trust the voice. Trust is the highest-friction component of any health purchase -- and we've already cleared it.

Need is validated. Every comment, DM, and event conversation is a data point. We know what they're struggling with, what they'd pay for, and what language they use. The product is shaped by real demand, not assumptions.

Community exists. They already know each other from #dailyalche, from events, from newsletter replies. The BELONG layer -- the retention moat -- is already alive before the app ships. They're not just buying a product. They're joining their people.

The investor takeaway

We are not waiting for funding to start. The content flywheel is in motion. We're building an audience that trusts us before we ask them to buy from us. Your investment doesn't start the engine -- it builds the product that this audience is already asking for. By the time we launch, the question isn't "will anyone use this?" It's "how fast can we onboard the people already waiting?"
