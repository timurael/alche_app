Misleading Risk 1 — Severity: Critical

Misleading Risk 2 — Severity: High
"Break-even at ~145 subscribers + EUR 10K/month space" — This was calculated BEFORE the decisions made on 23 Feb 2026. With CGM hardware revenue removed (D3), restaurant revenue removed for M1-M12 (D2), doctor SaaS reduced from EUR 150 to EUR 99/mo (D1), and smoothie bar added (D4), break-even needs complete recalculation. The physical space lease cost (missing from P&L) will further shift the break-even point. Do NOT present the 145-subscriber figure to investors until the model is updated.
Misleading Risk 3 — Severity: High
"20 longevity doctors in Berlin by M12" (D1) — R3 estimated 30-60 longevity-focused practices in Berlin total. Onboarding 20 means capturing 33-67% of the addressable market in Year 1. This is exceptionally aggressive for 2 non-technical founders with 1 part-time Partner Manager starting at M6. Consider modeling a conservative scenario: 8-12 doctors by M12 (EUR 792-1,188/mo vs. EUR 1,980/mo). Present the 20-doctor figure as an "optimistic scenario" with the 10-doctor figure as the "base case."
Misleading Risk 4 — Severity: Medium
"Smoothie bar EUR 9,022/mo base case" (D4) — This assumes 45 drinks/day at an average price derived from the R2 PDF. In a niche longevity space (not a high-foot-traffic coffee shop), 20-30 drinks/day is more realistic for the first 6-12 months. Model at 60% of the PDF's base case = EUR 5,413/mo as the realistic scenario. Present EUR 9,022/mo as the upside case once the space has established regular foot traffic.
Misleading Risk 5 — Severity: Medium
"Software-Only CGM" (D3) — If CGM features are baked into Pro/Premium subscription tiers without a price increase, subscription revenue stays the same but feature development cost increases (CGM data pipeline, visualization, insights engine). If subscription price increases to reflect CGM value, willingness-to-pay must be validated. Currently, the model removes hardware cost but does not account for the incremental software development cost of CGM features. An investor may ask: "What is the incremental cost of building CGM software features?"
Misleading Risk 6 — Severity: Medium
"ZOE dropped CGM" — Until the research on WHY ZOE dropped CGM is complete, this is an open investor risk. If ZOE dropped CGM because the feature had low engagement or high support costs (not just inventory issues), alche's software-only approach may face the same challenges. The counter-narrative must be ready before any investor meeting. Research agent has been tasked with this investigation.
