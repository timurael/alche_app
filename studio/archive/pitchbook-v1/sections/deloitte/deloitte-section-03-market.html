The German Health Paradox
Germany spends EUR 495B on healthcare annually (12.3% of GDP) -- the highest in the EU. Only 4.8% goes to prevention. With 7.7 hospital beds per 1,000 people (OECD average: 4.2), the system was architecturally built to treat illness, not prevent it. Consumers are filling the gap themselves.
