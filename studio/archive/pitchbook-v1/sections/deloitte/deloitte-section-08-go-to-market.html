12 months to product-market fit, structured in four phases with defined milestones at each gate.
Phase Timeline Key Activities Budget
Foundation Q1 (M1-3) Launch @alche.space. 3-5 short-form videos/week. Launch newsletter (Week 2). Goal: 300 subs. 10 informal customer discovery conversations from social DMs and meetup contacts. EUR 0
Traction Proof Q2 (M4-6) First Alche Salon event (30-40 people). Open waitlist landing page. Goal: 500 signups. Begin GmbH formation. Apply to GruendungsBONUS Plus (EUR 50K non-dilutive Berlin grant). EUR 200-500

Beta Launch Q4 (M10-12) MVP beta to first 50-100 users from waitlist. Founding member pricing: EUR 19/mo, locked. Content evolves to product teasers, beta stories, "building in public." Physical space search begins. Post-funding
Two Flywheels, Sequenced
Flywheel 1 -- Running Now (Pre-Funding)
Create radical-honesty science content → attract health-curious audience → they engage and share → we learn what they need → create sharper content. No product needed. No funding needed.
Flywheel 2 -- Activated by Funding
Know your body (data) → Do the protocols (action) → Get what you need (products) → Belong with others (community). Funding builds the product for an audience that already exists and trusts us.
Why content-first reduces risk for investors
By the time funding closes, the content engine has been running for 6+ months. The investor sees real audience data (followers, newsletter subs, waitlist signups, event attendance) before writing a check. If the content fails to build an audience, the investment thesis fails before capital is deployed. This is a feature, not a risk.
