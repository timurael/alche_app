Three converging forces create the opening
1. Demand is proven: 84% of consumers now rank healthy aging as a top priority (McKinsey 2024). 2. Prevention is underfunded: Germany spends only 4.8% of health expenditure on prevention despite EUR 495B total annual spend. 3. No integrator exists in Europe: 30+ companies analyzed; zero offer integrated biomarkers + protocols + products + community. The window is 12-18 months before well-funded US players localize to DACH.
LESSON FROM $1.4B IN FAILURES: Arivale (shut down 2019) proved consumers do not pay for disease prevention. Implication: alche sells how you feel today, not prevention of future disease. (Source: Arivale post-mortem, Lee Hood interview)

The market demands integration, not more point solutions -- and the structural barriers to building one are why it has not happened yet.
Barrier to Integration Evidence Implication for alche
Organizational DNA mismatch Companies optimized for one vertical (wearables, supplements, telehealth) cannot bolt on adjacent verticals without restructuring Greenfield advantage
Data trust deficit in EU Only 8% of EU consumers share health data with third parties (BEUC 2024) GDPR-native design
US-centric market approach Oura, Hims, Function Health are US-designed and US-priced. No localized European longevity platform exists. European positioning
Capital misallocation $1.4B+ burned on hardware-first approaches (Forward Health: $657M), clinical-first approaches (DayTwo: 2,089 citations, still failed) Asset-light model

Investment implication
Alche does not create new demand. It consolidates existing spend (EUR 126-295/mo fragmented) into a single platform (EUR 19-99/mo). The consumer saves money. alche captures a portion of spend that was previously distributed across 8+ uncoordinated providers. This is a rebundling play, not a market creation play.
