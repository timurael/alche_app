## The Seven Structural Traps

### Trap #1: Closed Loop Trap
**What It Is:** Trying to own the full stack — biomarker testing, interpretation, food/products, hardware, fulfillment — all at once.

**Who Failed:**
- **Forward Health** — $657M burned
  - Tried to own: physical clinics, testing, interpretation, food, products, hardware (body scanners)
  - Result: Too many operational complexities, couldn't scale, shut down

**Why It Fails:**
- Each vertical requires different expertise (lab ops, hardware manufacturing, food production, software)
- Capital intensity compounds (need funding for ALL verticals simultaneously)
- One weak link breaks the chain (e.g., hardware delays kill entire product launch)

**alche Fix:**
- **Partner for biomarkers:** Integrate Oura, Apple Health, WHOOP, Dexcom — we're the software layer
- **Curate products:** Affiliate model → partnership model. Never own inventory pre-Seed.
- **Partner for fulfillment:** Third-party logistics, not owned warehouse

**Location in Research:** agents_prompt.md "Graveyard Rule #1"

### Trap #2: Front-Loading Curse
**What It Is:** Complex onboarding (blood tests, genetic sequencing, extensive questionnaires) creates initial friction. Users churn post-onboarding.

**Who Failed:**
- **Arivale** — ~$50M burned
  - Led with comprehensive testing (blood, microbiome, genetics)
  - Required in-person lab visit + 2-week wait for results
  - Users engaged during onboarding, then churned after receiving data
  - Founder quote: "I do not believe at this point that there is a meaningful market for a program that's going to help people do something in the future."

**Why It Fails:**
- High initial investment (time, money, complexity) creates sunk-cost fatigue
- "Future disease prevention" lacks immediate reward
- Post-onboarding, no compelling reason to stay engaged

**alche Fix:**
- **Simple app at launch:** No required tests. Start with wearable sync (Oura/Apple Health).
- **Optional complexity:** Blood panels and CGM at Premium tier, NOT gatekeeper
- **Sell the present:** Energy today, sleep tonight, mood this week — NOT "prevent disease in 20 years"

**Location in Research:** agents_prompt.md "Graveyard Rule #2"

### Trap #3: Preventive Health Paradox
**What It Is:** Framing health benefits as future disease prevention. Humans are terrible at optimizing for distant future outcomes.

**Who Failed:**
- **Arivale** (same as above)
  - Positioned as "preventive genomics"
  - Value prop: "Optimize your health to avoid disease decades from now"
  - Result: No immediate dopamine hit, no behavior change reinforcement

**Why It Fails:**
- Behavioral psychology: immediate rewards drive habit formation
- "Prevent future disease" = abstract, low urgency
- Wellness apps need daily engagement → need daily reward

**alche Fix:**
- **Sell the present:**
  - Energy: "Wake up less groggy tomorrow"
  - Sleep: "Fall asleep 10 minutes faster tonight"
  - Mood: "Feel less anxious this week"
- **Ritual framing:** #dailyalche habit, not "health optimization project"
- **Community validation:** Social proof (user-generated content) = immediate identity reward

**Location in Research:** agents_prompt.md "Graveyard Rule #3"

### Trap #4: Two-Business Death Spiral
**What It Is:** Building software + physical operations + hardware simultaneously. Resource split = both suffer.

**Who Failed:**
- **Forward Health** — $657M (see Trap #1)
  - Physical clinics + body scanner hardware + software platform
- **Noom** (initially) — tried clinical weight-loss program + consumer app
  - Eventually pivoted to software-only, but identity crisis damaged brand

**Why It Fails:**
- Software = iterative, fast releases, digital talent
- Physical = real estate, ops teams, supply chain, slow iteration
- Hardware = manufacturing, supply chain risk, long lead times, obsolescence
- Managing all three = strategic confusion, capital exhaustion

**alche Fix:**
- **Pre-Seed (EUR 500K):** Digital-first, app-focused. Zero physical buildout.
- **Seed (EUR 3M):** Berlin Alche Salon space launches as ONE revenue module with separate P&L
  - NOT a parallel business
  - Purpose: Content studio + event space + walk-in acquisition
  - Standalone economics: EUR -2K to EUR +700/month (near breakeven)
- **Never own hardware:** We're the software interpretation layer, not the device manufacturer

### Trap #5: Identity Crisis Kill Zone
**What It Is:** Unclear positioning between "wellness" and "medical," or "entertainment" and "health." Signal loss to users = strategy confusion.

**Who Failed:**
- **Noom** — $3.7B peak valuation → mass layoffs
  - Started as behavior change app (wellness)
  - Pivoted to clinical weight-loss (medical, insurance reimbursement)
  - Then pivoted back to consumer wellness
  - Result: Brand confusion, user trust erosion

**Why It Fails:**
- "Medical" = regulatory burden, reimbursement complexity, clinical trials, slower iteration
- "Wellness" = consumer-paid, brand-driven, community, fast iteration
- Switching between the two = users don't know what you are

**alche Fix:**
- **One identity: Wellness lifestyle, not medical.**
  - Category: Longevity Lifestyle Platform
  - NOT DiGA (German digital health app certification)
  - NOT clinical, NOT pharma, NOT telehealth
- **Physician advisor (via Daria) is NOT investor, NOT co-founder**
  - Medical credibility, not medical positioning
- **No pivots:** Consumer B2C from Day 1 → Seed → Series A

**Location in Research:** agents_prompt.md "Graveyard Rule #5"

### Trap #6: Reimbursement Wall
**What It Is:** Chasing insurance reimbursement (in Germany: DiGA certification). Expensive, slow, locks revenue to insurance-dependent model.

**Who Failed:**
- **DiGA Chasers** (multiple German digital health startups)
  - Spent 12-18 months + EUR 200K+ on DiGA certification
  - Locked into insurance reimbursement rates (EUR 20-40/month, negotiated annually)
  - Regulatory burden kills iteration speed

**Why It Fails:**
- Certification cost: EUR 200K+ (consumes 40% of EUR 500K raise)
- Time to certification: 12-18 months (kills competitive window)
- Reimbursement rates: Low, non-negotiable, annual renegotiation
- Regulatory constraints: Can't iterate freely (every feature change requires re-approval)

**alche Fix:**
- **Consumer B2C, no DiGA certification, no insurance dependency**
  - Users pay directly (EUR 19/49/99 per month)
  - Pricing flexibility (can test tiers, experiment with features)
  - Fast iteration (no regulatory approval per release)
- **27% of Germans willing to pay OOP for health apps** (SpringerMedizin) = sufficient TAM

**Location in Research:** agents_prompt.md "Graveyard Rule #6"

### Trap #7: Hardware Multiplier
**What It Is:** Building proprietary hardware. Supply chain risk, capital intensity, obsolescence, DRM backlash.

**Who Failed:**
- **Juicero** — $120M raised
  - Proprietary $400 juicer with DRM-locked juice bags
  - Bags could be squeezed by hand (no machine needed)
  - Viral backlash: "You built a $400 machine to squeeze a bag?"
  - Result: Complete shutdown, hardware recalled

**Why It Fails:**
- Hardware COGS: High upfront (tooling, manufacturing setup, inventory)
- Supply chain risk: Chip shortages, shipping delays, quality control
- Obsolescence: New model every 18-24 months (Apple cycle), existing customers feel abandoned
- Capital intensity: 5-10x more expensive than software-only

**alche Fix:**
- **Zero proprietary hardware**
  - We're the software interpretation layer
  - Partner with Oura, WHOOP, Dexcom, Apple Health
  - Users bring their own devices (BYOD model)
- **If hardware is needed (e.g., CGM):** Partner with Dexcom, not build proprietary sensor

## The Survivor Pattern (What Works)

**Why It Works:**
- Asset-light: No hardware, no physical locations, partner for testing
