## The Investor Profile

A DACH-based angel or micro-VC partner who:
- Has deployed EUR 2M+ across 15+ health-tech and consumer wellness deals
- Reviews 300+ pitch books per year
- Has consulting background (Deloitte, McKinsey, BCG, Roland Berger)
- Evaluates through the Pyramid Principle: conclusion first, evidence second
- Has watched Forward Health burn $657M and die
- Has invested in at least two "lifestyle brands" that ran out of capital before PMF

**Source**: Empathy Agent persona construction, informed by fundraiser brief (`_FUNDRAISER_BRIEF.md`)

## The 6-Filter Mental Model

Investors apply these filters sequentially. Failure at any filter = rejection.

| Filter | Time | Question | What They Look For |
|--------|------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | 5 sec | Business or brand exercise? | Executive summary, financial structure visible early |
| 2 | 30 sec | Do they know their numbers? | Unit economics, break-even, assumptions explicit |
| 3 | 2 min | Is the problem real? | Bottom-up market sizing, competitive white space |
| 4 | 5 min | Can this team execute? | Relevant experience, honest gap assessment, specific plans |
| 5 | 10 min | Do the economics survive stress-testing? | Sensitivity analysis, scenario modeling, conservative base case |
| 6 | Post-read | Path to my return? | Next-round economics, cap table implications |

**Source**: Empathy Agent research, synthesized from Deloitte/McKinsey pitch book analysis

## Decision Triggers

### What Makes Them Write a Check
1. Conservative financial model that survives their own stress-test
2. Evidence (even tiny) that someone has paid for something
3. Unit economics with visible, defensible assumptions
4. Team that acknowledges gaps honestly with a specific plan to fill them
5. Break-even math that feels achievable (145 subscribers is compelling)
6. Structural insight about why competitors failed (graveyard analysis)
7. Transparent scenario modeling (EUR 500K case AND EUR 400K case)

### What Makes Them Say "Let Me Think About It" (Which Means No)
1. Beautiful design with vague business logic
2. Research depth substituting for market validation
3. Aspirational language ("my Alche era") in competitive moat sections
4. Financial projections without visible assumptions
5. Over-indexing on brand when no one has experienced the brand
6. Physical space dependency in a pre-seed model claiming digital-first
7. LTV:CAC ranges so wide (5-15x) they communicate uncertainty

**Source**: Empathy Agent persona analysis + `_INVESTOR_CRITIQUE.md` investor feedback

## First 30 Seconds: What They Scan For

In order of detection speed:
1. **No executive summary** = "made by designers, not operators"
2. **Cover says "The Art of Curated Longevity"** = brand exercise filter triggered
3. **Glassmorphic cards everywhere** = "consumer brand, not investment memorandum"
4. **No prominent page numbers** = unprofessional document structure
5. **Prose paragraphs where tables should be** = editorial, not analytical

**Source**: Empathy Agent research on consulting document standards

---

## What "Business-Heavy" Actually Means

Not just "more numbers." It means:

1. **Every page leads with an insight, not a label.** The heading IS the conclusion.
2. **Assumptions are always visible.** Gray italic rows in every financial table.
3. **Data is in tables, not cards.** Five glass KPI cards become one five-row table.
4. **The document is navigable.** TOC on page 2, section numbering, prominent page numbers.
5. **Financial information appears early.** Executive summary and financial overview within first 5 pages.
6. **Charts tell a story with annotations.** Title states the conclusion, axes labeled, source line present.

**Source**: Empathy Agent Deloitte/McKinsey visual language research
