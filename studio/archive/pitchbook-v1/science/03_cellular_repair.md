# Cellular Repair & Autophagy

## Overview
Targeting the cellular "clean-up" mechanism (autophagy), which degrades and recycles damaged cellular components. This is the cornerstone of healthspan extension and age-reversal protocols.

## 1. Spermidine
**Category:** Autophagy Inducer / Polyamine
**Primary Health Benefits:** Induces autophagy, exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, enhances mitochondrial function, and helps stabilize DNA. Broad correlation with improved healthspan and lifespan.
**Active Compounds:** Spermidine (often extracted from wheat germ).

### Clinical Literature & Peer-Reviewed Backing
*   **DOI: 10.1038/s43587-022-00322-9** - "Mechanisms of spermidine-induced autophagy and geroprotection" (Nature Aging, 2022).
*   **DOI: 10.1002/jimd.12841** - "Spermidine Recovers the Autophagy Defects Underlying the Pathophysiology of Cell Trafficking Disorders."
*   Epidemiological studies (15-year tracking of 829 participants) demonstrate a positive correlation between nutritional spermidine intake and reduced overall mortality associated with cardiovascular diseases. 
*   Currently undergoing clinical trials in the US (combined with exercise) to assess its potential in increasing the healthspan of adults aged 65-80.

**Contraindications:** Celiac disease or gluten sensitivity if sourced from wheat germ (must use alternative extracts or synthetics for gluten-free formulations).
**Optimal Scientifically Backed Dosage:** Typically 1mg to 3mg per day of supplemental spermidine, although clinical trials are evaluating dose-escalation protocols.

**[Alche EFSA Compliance Note]:** Spermidine-rich wheat germ extract (Triticum aestivum) has been explicitly authorized as a Novel Food in the EU (Commission Implementing Regulation EU 2020/443). The regulations cap the maximum daily intake at 6mg. Alche formulations must stay within this 6mg upper limit entirely.
