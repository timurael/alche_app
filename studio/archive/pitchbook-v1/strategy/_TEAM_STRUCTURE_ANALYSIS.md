## CURRENT STATE DIAGNOSIS

Before proposing alternatives, here is what both files get right and wrong.

### What the existing `section-11-team.html` gets RIGHT

1. **Honest CTO gap framing.** The headline "Two founders. One gap. A plan to close it." is the strongest single line in either team file. It pre-empts the investor's first question instead of waiting for it.
2. **Callout box for the CTO gap.** Separating this into a distinct visual element gives it weight without making it the entire section.
3. **"Why this team for this company" closing block.** The "both founders are the product's first users" line is the right emotional closer for Section 11.
4. **Governance table is clean.** Label-value pairs. No clutter.

### What both files get WRONG

1. **"Four People, No Gaps" is a lie.** The older `alche-team-section.html` uses this headline. An investor who reads it and then sees the TBD placeholder for AmirFarzam's compensation will question the founders' honesty — or their self-awareness. Either one kills the deal.
2. **Technical credentials are buried.** Timur's bio leads with "Quantitative thinker who builds brands" — which reads as a marketing person. His MSc Data Science and Daria's BSc Computer Engineering are afterthoughts. At a company that needs to convince investors it can build technology, this sequencing is backwards.
3. **AmirFarzam's presentation is unresolved.** A yellow "COMPENSATION STRUCTURE NEEDED" placeholder in an investor-facing document signals the team has not figured out its own internal agreements. This is more damaging than omitting him entirely.
4. **The 10-year friendship is thrown away.** "Not a networking match" is defensive framing. It anticipates a criticism that has not been raised and sounds like an excuse.
5. **No decision framework.** With no CEO title and a 49/51 equity split, the governance section leaves the investor wondering: who breaks deadlocks? Who is the Geschaftsfuhrer? The blank space here becomes an objection.
6. **Credential ordering is resume-style, not investor-style.** Experience lines are listed chronologically (most recent first). Investors do not read resumes — they scan for pattern recognition: "Has this person done something relevant to what they are building now?"
7. **Daria's autoimmune journey is underweighted.** A four-year personal health journey is the single strongest founder-market fit signal in the entire team. It is buried in a subordinate clause: "4-year autoimmune journey turned domain expertise." This should be a headline detail, not a parenthetical.

## APPROACH A: "THE HONEST GAP"

### Thesis
Lead with radical transparency. Make the CTO gap the intellectual centerpiece of the team section, not an admission of weakness. European angels reward honesty — especially when combined with a plan.

### Perspective: European Angel Investor
This is the approach I fund. At pre-seed, I know the team is incomplete. What I want to see is whether the founders know what they are missing and have a credible plan to fill it. A team slide that pretends to have no gaps tells me the founders are either delusional or dishonest. A team slide that says "here is the gap, here is why it exists, here is how funding closes it" tells me these founders think clearly under pressure.

### Perspective: Pitch Narrative Architect
This approach creates "Lean Forward Moment #5" — the moment where a weakness becomes evidence of discipline. The emotional beat is: *surprise* ("they are admitting the gap?") followed by *respect* ("they have a plan for it"). The risk is that the gap becomes the thing the investor remembers most. Mitigation: the gap framing must be the middle of the section, not the opening or closing. The section must open with founder strength and close with founder conviction.

#### 6. AmirFarzam Handling

**Do not name him in the pitch book.** Reference him as "an identified technical lead" within the CTO gap callout. Rationale:

- *Investor perspective*: Naming a team member without a resolved compensation structure invites the question "what is his deal?" — and the founders cannot answer it cleanly. This creates uncertainty in the section that is supposed to build confidence.
- *Narrative perspective*: The CTO gap framing is stronger when it is clean. "We have identified a technical lead and scoped the MVP with them" is sufficient. The investor does not need a name — they need to know the gap has a plan.
- *Practical*: AmirFarzam can be introduced in the meeting, with a clear compensation structure by then. The pitch book opens the door; the meeting closes the deal.

If the compensation structure IS resolved before distribution, AmirFarzam can appear as a one-line entry under "Extended Team" with title, key credential (Jung von Matt HAVEL, Star Digital), and commitment level. But never with a yellow placeholder.

This reframes the friendship as a risk-mitigation asset. The investor's concern is not "are they friends?" — it is "will they survive their first real disagreement?" The reframe answers the real concern.

## APPROACH B: "CAPABILITY COVERAGE MAP"

### Thesis
Do not lead with the gap. Lead with what the team covers. Present the four domains (Data & Growth, Operations & Architecture, Engineering, Medical) as a coverage map, with each domain filled by a named person. The CTO gap is a footnote — a single row in the coverage map marked "Funding-dependent" — not a headline.

### Perspective: European Angel Investor
This works for brand-oriented or consumer-focused angels who invest in market intuition more than technical execution. The coverage map gives me confidence that the founders have thought about organizational design. But if I am a technical investor, I will still ask about the CTO — and the fact that the pitch book downplayed it will make me trust the team less. The coverage map approach is a gamble on the investor's profile.

### Perspective: Pitch Narrative Architect
The coverage map is visually clean and answers the "can they execute?" question through structure rather than biography. It de-emphasizes individual CVs in favor of organizational capability. The risk is that it feels corporate — like a consulting deck, not a founder story. The emotional arc of Section 11 is supposed to be *confidence*, and confidence comes from people, not org charts.

The coverage map handles this structurally. The "Engineering" row shows `[First Funded Hire]` with status `Funding-dependent`. No further explanation is needed on page 1.

The founding team's combined technical background (MSc Data Science + BSc Computer Engineering) ensures we can evaluate candidates, define architecture requirements, and manage the build until full-time technical leadership is in place.

This is lower temperature than Approach A. It does not make the gap a centerpiece — it explains it in two sentences and moves on.

#### 5. Title Framing (No CEO)

If compensation IS resolved: AmirFarzam could replace `[First Funded Hire]` in the Engineering row with his name and title, and the status would change from "Funding-dependent" to "Part-time" or "Contract" — whatever the resolved structure is. The coverage map format makes this a clean swap.

No elaboration. The coverage map already signals organizational maturity — adding an emotional "decade of trust" narrative would clash with the tone. Save it for the meeting.

## APPROACH C: "FOUNDER-MARKET FIT"

### Thesis
Lead with WHY these two people are the only ones who should build THIS company. The emotional core is Daria's four-year autoimmune journey and both founders' status as the product's first users. The CTO gap is reframed as a hiring principle: "We will not add a technical co-founder who has not seen the thesis validated." The section reads as a founder story, not an org chart.

### Perspective: European Angel Investor
This is the riskiest approach for European angels because it leads with emotion, and DACH investors are structurally skeptical of emotional pitches. HOWEVER — if the underlying credentials are strong enough (and they are: data science + computer engineering + NASDAQ experience + backend development), the emotional lead creates differentiation. Most pre-seed team slides are boring. This one would not be. The risk: if the investor is purely analytical, the emotional opening will feel like it is hiding something. Mitigation: the credentials must appear within the first 50 words of each bio, even in narrative form.

### Perspective: Pitch Narrative Architect
This is my preferred approach. Section 11 sits between The Moat (Section 10, which is analytical and restrained) and The Ask (Section 12, which is transactional). The emotional register needs to shift here — from proof to people. The reader has spent 20 pages absorbing data, frameworks, and analysis. They need to FEEL something about the founders before they are asked for money. Daria's autoimmune story and both founders' personal spending on longevity tools are the emotional anchors. The CTO gap reframe ("hiring discipline, not oversight") is the "lean forward" moment.

---

#### 1. Structure (2.5 pages)

**Page 1 — The Founders' Story (full page)**

## RECOMMENDATION

**Use Approach C ("Founder-Market Fit") as the primary structure, with two elements borrowed from Approach A.**

### Why Approach C

1. **Section 11 sits at page 20-22 of a 27-page book.** By this point, the investor has absorbed 20 pages of data, frameworks, competitive analysis, and financial models. They are analytically saturated. The pitch narrative architect's insight is correct: the reader needs an emotional register shift. They need to feel something about the founders before they are asked for money on the next page.

2. **Daria's autoimmune story is the single strongest founder-market fit signal in the entire pitch book.** No competitor has a co-founder who spent four years as a patient navigating the exact problem the product solves. Burying this in a subordinate clause is malpractice. Approach C puts it where it belongs — at the center.

3. **The "CTO Principle" reframe is the most persuasive version of the gap framing.** It ties the decision to the $1.4B graveyard analysis the investor has already read (Section 04). It does not just admit the gap — it argues that the gap is evidence of discipline. This is "Lean Forward Moment #5."

4. **Both founders' technical credentials are strong enough to survive an emotional opening.** MSc Data Science and BSc Computer Engineering are real degrees from real institutions. NASDAQ-listed company experience and backend production systems are real work. The analytical counterweight is there. The emotional opening does not hide weak credentials — it frames strong ones.

5. **The 10-year friendship becomes an asset in this approach.** In Approaches A and B, the friendship is a governance line item that raises more questions than it answers. In Approach C, it is the founding story. The investor understands why these two people are building this together. The question "what if they fight?" is pre-empted by the narrative: they have already navigated a decade of life together.

### What to borrow from Approach A

1. **The Decision Framework row in the governance table.** This is non-negotiable regardless of approach. With no CEO title and a 49/51 split, the investor needs to see deadlock resolution in writing. Use the exact framing from Approach A: "Timur owns brand, data, and growth decisions. Daria owns architecture, operations, and vision decisions. Product decisions are shared. Deadlocks are resolved by majority equity holder."

2. **The credential ordering principle (technical first).** Even in narrative form, the first credential mentioned for each founder must be the technical one. "Timur holds an MSc in Data Science" before any mention of brand or marketing. "Daria trained as a computer engineer" before any mention of operations. This is insurance against the "two marketing people" objection.

### What NOT to do

1. **Do not use "Four People, No Gaps."** Under any approach. It is demonstrably false and the Fundraiser brief already flagged it as a language red flag.

2. **Do not name AmirFarzam unless his compensation structure is resolved before distribution.** A named team member with unresolved terms is worse than an unnamed technical lead with a clear plan. The pitch book opens doors — the meeting introduces people.

3. **Do not show the 49/51 equity split without the Decision Framework.** The split without explanation creates ambiguity. The split WITH a decision framework creates clarity. Both approaches are available — only one is acceptable.

4. **Do not include Dr. Jarmoukli's full scope of responsibilities.** "Protocol design validation, medical content review, biomarker interpretation, EU regulatory compliance" reads like a job description. Replace with: "His patients — entrepreneurs investing in proactive health out of pocket — are alche's target demographic." That single sentence does more work than four responsibility labels.

5. **Do not use "No visa risk."** It is defensive and raises a concern the investor may not have had. "Long-term Berlin residents" is sufficient.
