# Positioning -- Alche (April Dunford Framework)

> **Version:** 1.0 | March 2026 | Pre-validation

---

## Five Components of Positioning

### 1. Competitive Alternatives

What would customers do if Alche didn't exist?

| Alternative | What They Get | What They Miss |
|---|---|---|
| ZOE (EUR 60/mo) | Science-backed nutrition, warm brand | Multi-dimensional longevity, physical space, community, women-first |
| Function Health ($365/yr) | 200+ biomarkers, data depth | Warmth, guidance, community, women-focus, European access |
| AG1 (EUR 80/mo) | Easy, convenient, feels good | Real science, personalization, community |
| Yoga studio + nutritionist (EUR 150-250/mo) | Movement + nutrition, some community | Longevity science, integration, biomarkers, digital convenience |
| ANTI Berlin (per-session) | Physical longevity experience in Berlin | Digital platform, personalization, biomarkers, ongoing guidance |
| Scattered approach (5+ apps/services) | Covers some bases | Integration, simplification, coherent longevity strategy |
| Nothing | No cost, no effort | Everything |

### 2. Unique Attributes

What does Alche have that the alternatives lack?

| Attribute | Alche | Nearest Competitor | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Multi-dimensional longevity scope | 6 pillars (sleep, nutrition, movement, stress, biomarkers, social) | ZOE: 1 dimension (nutrition) | WIDE |
| Physical space + digital platform | Cafe + app | Function: digital only | WIDE |
| Community architecture (in-person + digital) | Events + cohorts + forum | Peloton: digital community (not longevity) | WIDE |
| Women-first design | Core positioning | ZOE: general audience | MODERATE |
| "Imperfect Longevity" brand positioning | Core identity | No competitor claims this | WIDE |
| Berlin/European presence | HQ and first market | No consumer longevity lifestyle brand in Europe | WIDE |
| Asset-light architecture | Partner model | Forward (dead): owned everything | STRUCTURAL |

### 3. Value (What Do These Attributes Enable?)

| Attribute | Value to Customer | Measurable? |
|---|---|---|
| Multi-dimensional | One place for complete longevity, not 5+ fragmented services | YES (# of services replaced) |
| Physical + digital | Real human connection + digital convenience | PARTIALLY (event attendance + app usage) |
| Community | Sustainable motivation, reduced loneliness, peer learning | PARTIALLY (retention, NPS) |
| Women-first | Content/protocols designed for female physiology | YES (satisfaction scores) |
| "Imperfect Longevity" | Reduced wellness anxiety, enjoyment | HARD (sentiment, qualitative) |
| European/Berlin | Accessible, local, culturally relevant | YES (no geographic barrier) |

### 4. Target Customer Characteristics

Who cares most about these attributes?

**The customer who values Alche most:**
- Female, 35-50
- Berlin-based
- Already spending EUR 50+/mo on wellness (yoga, supplements, nutrition)
- Perimenopause/menopause aware
- Has tried and been disappointed by at least one optimization tool
- Values community and in-person connection
- Wants science but not at the cost of joy
- Income: EUR 50K+ (can afford EUR 49-99/mo without financial strain)

**The customer who does NOT value Alche:**
- Pure optimizers who want maximum data (-> Function Health)
- Budget-conscious who want the cheapest option (-> AG1, free content)
- Men in the Bryan Johnson mold (-> Blueprint, WHOOP)
- People satisfied with their current fragmented approach
- Skeptics of wellness as a category

### 5. Market Category

**Category:** Longevity lifestyle platform

**Category creation vs. existing:**
This is a category-creation play. "Longevity lifestyle platform" does not exist as a recognized category. Closest neighbors:
- "Health & wellness app" (too broad, commoditized)
- "Longevity testing platform" (too clinical -- that's Function)
- "Wellness membership" (too vague)

**Risk of category creation:** Investors and customers must be educated on what the category is. This adds friction to every conversation. The upside: category creators capture disproportionate value if the category takes hold. The downside: most category-creation attempts fail.

## Positioning Statement

**Alche is a [longevity lifestyle platform] for [wellness-engaged women in Berlin] who [want to age well but are alienated by optimization culture]. Unlike [Function Health, ZOE, or scattered wellness services], Alche [combines multi-dimensional longevity science with a physical community space, warm inclusive brand, and AI-powered personalization -- delivering science with soul, not science as punishment].**

## Positioning vs. Competitors (Battle Cards)

### vs. ZOE
- "ZOE does nutrition brilliantly. We do all of longevity -- sleep, stress, biomarkers, movement, nutrition, and social connection. And we meet you in person, not just in an app."
- ZOE advantage: 70+ papers, $500M+ validation
- Alche advantage: Breadth, physical space, community, women-first

### vs. Function Health
- "Function shows you 200 numbers. We help you understand what they mean, do something about them, and surround you with community to keep going."
- Function advantage: Scale (200K+ members), price ($1/day), data depth
- Alche advantage: Warmth, guidance, community, women-first, European

### vs. AG1
- "AG1 gives you one scoop and hopes for the best. We test, personalize, guide, and connect you with community -- with science you can actually verify."
- AG1 advantage: Simplicity, brand recognition, distribution
- Alche advantage: Real science, personalization, accountability

### vs. Doing Nothing
- "You already spend EUR 100-200/mo on scattered wellness. Alche replaces 3-5 of those with one integrated platform that actually tracks whether it's working."
- Nothing advantage: No cost, no effort
- Alche advantage: Results, community, accountability

## Positioning Risks

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| "Imperfect Longevity" attracts browsers, not buyers | HIGH | Test with pricing-paired concept boards |
| Category creation fails (market doesn't understand what Alche is) | HIGH | Use familiar analogies ("ZOE for all of longevity") |
| Women-first positioning limits TAM | MEDIUM | "Designed for women, welcome to all" |
| Positioning is easily copied | MEDIUM | Community and physical space are harder to copy than messaging |
| Over-optimization backlash is a temporary trend | MEDIUM | Build positioning on "science + joy" foundation, not just "anti-optimization" |

---

## Flags

**RED:**
- "Imperfect Longevity" has never been tested with actual customers. It could resonate strongly or fall completely flat. This is the #1 validation priority.

**YELLOW:**
- Category creation is risky. Every investor meeting will start with "so what IS this?" -- have a 10-second answer ready.
- Positioning against ZOE is important but requires acknowledging their science advantage (70+ papers vs. 0).
