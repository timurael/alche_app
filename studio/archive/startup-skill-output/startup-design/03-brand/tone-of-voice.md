## Voice Character

Alche sounds like **your smartest friend who happens to know a lot about longevity science -- the one who explains complex things clearly over a really good meal, never makes you feel stupid, and always makes you laugh.**

Not a doctor. Not a guru. Not a coach. A friend with a brain and a heart.

## Tone Principles

### 1. Warm, not soft
Say things with care. Don't say things without substance.

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| "The research on sleep and longevity is clear -- and also kind of liberating." | "Sleep is so important, babe!" |
| "Your cortisol levels suggest your body is under stress. Here's what you can do." | "Everything will be fine, just breathe." |

### 2. Smart, not superior
Share knowledge as a gift, not a flex.

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| "NAD+ declines roughly 50% between ages 40 and 60. That's a big deal -- here's why." | "As the literature demonstrates, the age-related decline in NAD+ biosynthesis..." |
| "This supplement has three clinical trials behind it. That's more than most." | "Only evidence-based practitioners would understand the significance of..." |

### 3. Honest, not harsh
Tell the truth with empathy.

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| "Your inflammation markers are higher than ideal. That's useful information -- it means we know where to focus." | "Your CRP levels are dangerously elevated." |
| "We don't carry this product because the evidence isn't strong enough yet." | "This product is snake oil and you've been scammed." |

### 4. Encouraging, not demanding
Inspire action without shame.

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| "Even 15 minutes of movement changes your inflammatory markers. Start there." | "You need to exercise 150 minutes per week. No excuses." |
| "Missed your supplements this week? That's fine. Your body is resilient." | "Consistency is KEY. Don't break the streak!" |

### 5. Communal, not lonely
Speak as "we" more than "you should."

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| "We're learning about this together." | "You need to take control of your health." |
| "Our community tried this and here's what they found." | "According to the latest studies, individuals should..." |

## Language Rules

### Words We Use
- longevity, healthspan, joyspan
- nourish, support, build
- evidence, research, study
- community, together, gather
- explore, discover, learn
- progress, journey, practice
- imperfect, real, honest

### Words We Avoid
- optimize, hack, stack, protocol (biohacker language)
- transform, revolutionize, disrupt (startup hype)
- anti-aging (negative framing -- we are pro-aging-well)
- perfect, ideal, optimal (perfection language)
- detox, cleanse, purify (pseudoscience adjacent)
- should, must, need to (shame language)
- patient, treatment, diagnosis (clinical language)

### Phrasings

| Instead of | We say |
|---|---|
| "Anti-aging supplement" | "Longevity support" |
| "Optimize your health" | "Support your body" |
| "Biohack your way to..." | "Simple changes that matter" |
| "The science proves..." | "The research suggests..." |
| "You should be doing..." | "Here's something worth trying..." |
| "Our patented formula..." | "Backed by [specific study]..." |
| "Join our tribe" | "Come to the table" |

## Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| App notifications | Brief, warm, non-pressuring | "Your latest results are in. Good news and useful news inside." |
| Blog/newsletter | Curious, educational, conversational | "We spent a week reading every study on NAD+. Here's what actually matters." |
| Instagram | Visual, warm, community-forward | Image of cafe table, friends laughing. Caption: "This is what longevity looks like." |
| Cafe menu | Playful, informative | "Matcha + lion's mane. Because your brain deserves nice things." |
| Investor communication | Clear, data-driven, honest | "We validated X. We haven't validated Y yet. Here's how we'll test it." |
| Community forum | Peer-like, inclusive, supportive | "Has anyone tried this? Here's what the research says, and here's what our members report." |
| Crisis/bad news | Direct, empathetic, transparent | "We got this wrong. Here's what happened and what we're doing about it." |

## Voice Test: Would Alche Say This?

| Statement | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Unlock your full potential with our longevity stack" | NO | Optimization language, "stack" is biohacker |
| "The research on omega-3s and brain health is fascinating -- and practical" | YES | Smart, warm, actionable |
| "You're aging wrong" | ABSOLUTELY NOT | Shame, fear |
| "Your body is remarkable. Let's give it what it needs." | YES | Warm, empowering, science-adjacent |
| "Studies show this compound extends lifespan in mice by 30%" | CAREFUL | Honest but might overpromise. Add: "Mouse studies don't always translate to humans, but here's why researchers are excited." |
| "Join our community of women who refuse to age quietly" | YES (strong) | Empowering, community, defiant without being aggressive |

## Flags

**YELLOW:**
- Tone of voice is easy to define, hard to maintain at scale. Every piece of content, every app notification, every cafe menu item must pass the voice test. This requires a style guide that evolves with the team.
- The "avoid optimization language" rule will be tested when communicating biomarker results. Results inherently invite optimization framing. Finding non-optimization language for data communication is a genuine design challenge.
