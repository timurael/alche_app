# Kill Criteria -- Alche

> **Version:** 1.0 | March 2026
> **Purpose:** Pre-defined conditions under which the current strategy should be abandoned or fundamentally reworked. Established BEFORE emotional investment makes objective evaluation impossible.

---

## Philosophy

Kill criteria exist because founders fall in love with their ideas. These are the pre-commitments made with a clear head -- so that when the data comes back disappointing, there's a rational framework for the decision, not just hope.

**Rule: If a kill criterion is triggered, the default action is to stop and reassess. Not pivot within 24 hours. Not "try one more thing." Stop, reassess, and make a deliberate decision.**

---

## Pre-Launch Kill Criteria (Validation Phase)

| # | Criterion | Trigger | Default Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| K1 | Positioning doesn't convert | No concept variant exceeds 25% purchase intent at EUR 49/mo (n=50+) | Pause fundraise. Rework positioning. Retest. |
| K2 | WTP is structurally low | Van Westendorp optimal price <EUR 25/mo | Rethink business model. EUR 25/mo x 145 subs = EUR 43.5K/yr sub revenue. Not viable with cafe costs. |
| K3 | Events don't draw | <12 attendees per event after active promotion (3+ events) | Food-as-social-object hypothesis fails. Explore alternative community formats. |
| K4 | No cafe-to-digital conversion | <5% of event attendees sign up for anything digital (app/waitlist) | Physical-to-digital bridge doesn't work. Consider digital-first model. |
| K5 | Wrong audience | Interviews reveal no clear segment with both pain + WTP | Broad rethink of target customer and positioning. |
| K6 | Heilpraktiker doesn't cover core services | Legal review says key services require full medical license | Architecture redesign. May need clinical partner or different regulatory pathway. |
| K7 | CTO unfindable | No qualified CTO candidate after 3 months of active search within budget | Consider technical co-founder path, equity-only, or outsourced development. |

## Post-Launch Kill Criteria (Months 1-12)

| # | Criterion | Trigger | Default Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| K8 | No product-market fit by Month 6 | <30 paying subscribers after 6 months of operation | Serious pivot discussion. Current approach is not working. |
| K9 | Catastrophic churn | Monthly churn >15% for 3 consecutive months | Retention mechanism is fundamentally broken. Pause growth, fix retention or pivot. |
| K10 | Cafe is a pure cost center | Cafe revenue <EUR 3K/mo AND cafe-to-app conversion <1% for 3 months | Cafe is not justifying its cost as either revenue or marketing. Close, sublease, or redesign. |
| K11 | ARPU collapse | ARPU <EUR 30 after 100+ subscribers | Tier structure/value prop isn't working. Most customers choosing cheapest tier. |
| K12 | Community doesn't form | <10 members attend any community event for 3 consecutive months | Community hypothesis fails. Pivot to product-led (app-only) model. |
| K13 | Cash runway <6 months without clear fundraise path | Cash on hand covers <6 months of burn AND no active investor conversations | Emergency mode. Cut costs radically or begin wind-down conversation. |

## Scenario-Based Kill Decisions

### Scenario: "Positioning resonates but WTP is low"
- Concept testing: High appeal (8+/10), Purchase intent: >40%
- Van Westendorp: Optimal price EUR 20-30/mo
- **Action:** Not a kill. Adjust pricing tiers downward. Explore freemium with premium upsell. Cafe revenue becomes more important.

### Scenario: "WTP is fine but community doesn't form"
- Pricing study: EUR 49/mo works for 30%+
- Pop-up events: Low attendance, low engagement
- **Action:** Not a kill. Pivot community model: digital-first (Discord, in-app), influencer-led, or cohort-based rather than event-based. Cafe becomes retail + app-conversion, not community hub.

### Scenario: "Everything works but only for a tiny segment"
- All metrics positive, but subscriber growth plateaus at 80-100
- Berlin SOM is smaller than estimated
- **Action:** Not a kill. Evaluate: (1) Expand to adjacent segments (younger, men, other Berlin districts), (2) Expand geographically earlier (Munich, Hamburg), (3) Accept lifestyle-business scale.

### Scenario: "A competitor launches the exact same thing"
- Well-funded player (EUR 5M+) launches community-anchored longevity platform in Berlin
- **Action:** Not an automatic kill. Evaluate: (1) Can you differentiate on community depth? (2) Can you move faster? (3) Can you partner instead of compete? (4) If they have 10x capital AND same positioning, honest conversation about odds.

### Scenario: "ZOE announces European expansion + multi-dimensional longevity"
- ZOE says: "We're now covering all of longevity, and we're opening in Berlin"
- **Action:** Serious reassessment. ZOE has 70+ papers, 120K members, $500M+ valuation. If they enter the exact position, Alche's differentiation narrows to physical space + community only. Evaluate whether that's enough.

## The Hardest Kill Criterion

### K14: The founders disagree on whether to continue
- If one founder wants to kill and the other wants to continue after a kill criterion is triggered, the default is: **honor the data, not the emotions.** If the data says stop, stop. A disagreement at this stage, unresolved, will destroy the company more certainly than a bad market will.

---

## When Kill Criteria DON'T Apply

Kill criteria are NOT triggered by:
- One bad week or one bad event
- A single negative customer interview
- A press article criticizing the concept
- A competitor's funding announcement (unless they're in the exact position)
- Founder stress or fatigue (those are real but not data)

Kill criteria ARE triggered by:
- Sustained underperformance against pre-defined thresholds
- Quantitative data from properly designed experiments
- Multiple concurrent red flags
