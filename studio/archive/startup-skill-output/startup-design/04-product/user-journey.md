## Journey Map: "Eva" (Primary Persona -- Perimenopause Navigator, 42)

### Stage 1: Awareness (Week 0)

```
TRIGGER: Eva reads a BeautyMatter article about the over-optimization backlash.
She thinks: "Finally, someone gets it. I'm so tired of being told to optimize everything."

TOUCHPOINTS:
- Instagram post shared by a friend: photo of Alche cafe, functional food, women laughing
- Caption resonates: "Longevity is better imperfect than never."
- Eva follows @alche.life

FEELING: Curious. Relieved. "This looks like it's for me."
METRIC: Instagram follow, website visit
```

### Stage 2: Discovery (Weeks 1-4)

```
TOUCHPOINTS:
- Eva sees event listing: "The Long Table: Longevity Dinner at Alche"
- Event description: "20 women, 3 courses of functional food, 1 longevity scientist, 0 judgment."
- EUR 35 dinner ticket. She buys it.

FEELING: Intrigued. Slightly nervous (new people). Excited.
METRIC: Event ticket purchase
```

### Stage 3: Experience (Week 4 -- The Dinner)

```
TOUCHPOINTS:
- Eva walks into the cafe. It's warm -- wood, ceramics, plants. Not clinical. Not gym-like.
- She's greeted by name (RSVP list).
- Menu cards explain each ingredient: "Lion's mane: supports cognitive function (see: Li et al., 2023)"
- The guest expert talks about women's longevity -- Eva feels seen.
- Table conversation: women her age sharing similar experiences with perimenopause.
- She leaves with a card: "Download the Alche app. Your first month is free."

FEELING: "I found my people." Emotional. Motivated.
METRIC: App download, free trial activation
KEY MOMENT: The emotional bond formed at the dinner is the conversion catalyst.
```

### Stage 4: Exploration (Weeks 5-8 -- Free Trial)

```
TOUCHPOINTS:
- App onboarding: "Tell us about you" (conversational, not clinical)
- Eva enters: age, life stage (perimenopause), current wellness practices, goals
- AI generates first recommendations: "Based on what you've told us, here are 3 things to focus on this month"
- Eva browses content library: "What actually happens during perimenopause (the science, plainly)"
- She joins the community forum: "New here -- just had the most amazing dinner at Alche"
- She gets a reply: "Welcome! I've been a member for 2 months. The sleep content changed my life."

FEELING: Engaged. Learning. Feeling like she's making progress without pressure.
METRIC: Day 7 retention, content views, forum posts, app sessions/week
```

### Stage 5: Conversion (Week 8 -- Free Trial Ending)

```
TOUCHPOINTS:
- In-app message: "Your trial is ending. Here's what you've done so far:" (summary of engagement)
- Eva sees: 12 articles read, 3 community posts, 1 event attended, 2 AI recommendations followed
- Tier selection: "Which plan fits your life?"
  - Essentials (EUR 19/mo): App + community + content
  - Integrated (EUR 49/mo): + Personalized protocols + biomarker integration + monthly events
  - Complete (EUR 99/mo): + Quarterly biomarker testing + 1:1 sessions
- Eva chooses Integrated (EUR 49/mo) -- she wants the biomarker integration and monthly events.

FEELING: "This is worth it. I'd spend more on yoga + supplements separately."
METRIC: Conversion to paid, tier selection, payment method entry
KEY MOMENT: The value-price comparison against current spending (yoga + supplements + nutritionist = EUR 200+/mo)
```

### Stage 6: Engagement (Months 2-6)

```
MONTHLY RHYTHM:
Week 1: New AI recommendations based on previous month's data
Week 2: Community event at cafe (workshop or dinner)
Week 3: Check biomarker progress (if testing done)
Week 4: Content deep-dive + community discussion

TOUCHPOINTS:
- Eva does her first biomarker test (via lab partner). Results arrive in app.
- App contextualizes: "Your Vitamin D is low -- common in Berlin winters. Here's what to do."
- She follows the recommendation. 3 months later, retests. Vitamin D improved.
- She shares in the forum: "It actually works. My Vitamin D went from 22 to 48."
- Other members congratulate her. She feels part of something.

FEELING: Progress. Belonging. Trust.
METRIC: Monthly retention, biomarker retest rate, community engagement, NPS
KEY MOMENT: First measurable health improvement attributed to Alche -> trust deepens.
```

### Stage 7: Advocacy (Months 6+)

```
TOUCHPOINTS:
- Eva invites a friend to a dinner event.
- She shares a before/after biomarker screenshot (anonymized) on Instagram.
- She writes a testimonial for the community page.
- She upgrades to Complete (EUR 99/mo) because she wants quarterly testing + coaching.

FEELING: Identity formation. "I am an Alche member." Pride. Belonging.
METRIC: Referrals generated, tier upgrades, LTV, social sharing
KEY MOMENT: When Eva's identity incorporates Alche -> she's no longer a customer, she's a member.
```

## Journey Friction Points

| Stage | Friction | Risk Level | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness | "What IS this? A cafe? An app? A club?" | HIGH | Clear positioning in 10 seconds. "Longevity platform anchored by a Berlin cafe." |
| Discovery | "EUR 35 for a dinner with strangers?" | MEDIUM | Strong event descriptions, social proof, friend-bring-friend |
| Experience | "Will I be the only one who doesn't know about longevity?" | MEDIUM | Event design explicitly for beginners. "No expertise required." |
| Exploration | "The app feels basic compared to OURA/ZOE" | HIGH | Lead with content + community (strengths), not data features (weakness in v1) |
| Conversion | "EUR 49/mo for what exactly?" | HIGH | Clear value comparison vs. current spending. Show what's included. |
| Engagement | "I'm not seeing results fast enough" | MEDIUM | Manage expectations. "Longevity is measured in months, not days." |
| Advocacy | "I don't want to be 'that wellness person'" | MEDIUM | Make sharing feel natural, not performative. Community, not evangelism. |

## Alternative Journey: "Mia" (Secondary -- Optimization-Fatigued, 34)

**Key difference:** Mia discovers via social media or word-of-mouth (not event-first). She downloads the app, browses content, and the "Imperfect Longevity" framing draws her in. She attends a cafe event because the app told her about it. Her journey is digital-first, physical-second -- the reverse of Eva's.

**Implication:** Both journeys must work. The GTM prioritizes physical-first (Eva's path) but the app must also convert digital-first users (Mia's path).

---

## Flags

**RED:**
- Stage 4-5 (Exploration -> Conversion) is where most health apps lose users. Day 30 retention of 3% (industry avg) means 97% of free trial users leave. The community and event bridge must be strong enough to hold users through this valley.

**YELLOW:**
- The journey assumes the cafe experience is good enough to create emotional bonds. If the cafe is mediocre, the entire funnel breaks at Stage 3.
- "Eva's" journey takes 8 weeks from awareness to paid. In that time, she has 8 opportunities to drop off. Each handoff (social -> event -> cafe -> app -> community -> conversion) is a potential leak.
