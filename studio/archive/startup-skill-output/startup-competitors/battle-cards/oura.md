# Battle Card: OURA

*Updated: 2026-03-12*

---

## Who They Are

OURA is a Finnish health tech company ($11B valuation, $1B+ revenue in 2025) that makes the dominant smart ring for sleep, activity, and recovery tracking. With 5.5M rings sold, 80%+ market share, and retention in the high 80s%, OURA is evolving from a sleep tracker into a comprehensive health platform — adding metabolic health, women's cycle tracking, and partnership with Dexcom for glucose monitoring. Their fastest-growing segment is women in their early twenties.

---

## Strengths (Honest)

- **Market dominance:** 80%+ of smart ring market. [Data]
- **Revenue scale:** $1B in 2025, projecting $1.5B+ in 2026. [Data]
- **Retention:** High 80s% at 12 months — exceptional for consumer tech. [Data]
- **Hardware moat:** Proprietary ring creates switching costs (data locked in ecosystem). [Data]
- **Women's health expansion:** Pregnancy, cycle, hormonal insights — targeting female demographic. [Data]
- **Partnerships:** Team USA/LA28 Olympics, US Military, Dexcom Stelo, 4,000+ retail stores. [Data]
- **24/7 passive data:** Continuous tracking without user effort. [Data]
- **Resilience Score and Metabolic Health:** Expanding beyond sleep into comprehensive health. [Data]

---

## Weaknesses (Specific)

- **Subscription resentment:** $5.99/month mandatory fee on top of $349-499 hardware. "Money-grab for data that should go to my phone." [Data]
- **Refund nightmares:** 6+ weeks for refunds, multiple contacts required, EU directive violations reported. [Data]
- **Battery degradation:** Reports of 24-hour battery life after 8 months; ring dying after 3 years with only $100 off replacement. [Data]
- **Workout tracking unreliable:** "During HIIT, ring says I spent most time at rest." [Data]
- **Customer service bot-only:** Difficult to reach real humans. [Data]
- **Overheating incidents:** Burn marks and blisters reported (isolated). [Data]
- **Data discrepancies:** Significant differences between OURA and Apple Watch readings reported. [Data]
- **Growing competition:** Samsung Galaxy Ring (subscription-free), Ultrahuman, Amazfit. [Data]
- **Privacy concerns:** Military partnership + period tracking created alarm among women. [Data]
- **No guidance layer:** Data tracking without actionable coaching or community. [Data]

## How to Win Against Them

1. **Insight vs. data:** OURA gives you three scores every morning. Alche gives you understanding: what to DO about your sleep score, how it connects to your biomarkers, what your community has found helpful. "We don't just track your health — we help you improve it."

2. **Community vs. isolation:** OURA is a solo experience. You look at your phone alone. Alche provides a community of women on the same journey, a cafe where you can discuss your health, and coaching that connects the dots.

3. **Multi-dimensional vs. wearable-only:** OURA tracks sleep and activity. Alche integrates lab testing, nutrition, hormonal health, stress management, and community — the dimensions OURA can't measure from your finger.

4. **No hardware lock-in:** OURA's subscription model on top of expensive hardware creates resentment. Alche's subscription model stands on its own value — no hardware required.

5. **Complementary positioning:** "Love your OURA ring? Great — Alche is what you do with the data. We're the coaching, community, and lab testing layer that makes your ring data actionable."

---

## When They Win Over You (Honest)

- When the customer primarily wants passive 24/7 biometric tracking — OURA is genuinely best-in-class for sleep and recovery.
- When the customer wants a hardware-based solution they can wear and forget.
- When the customer cares about heart rate variability, sleep stages, and daily readiness scores specifically.
- When the customer is already in the OURA ecosystem and locked into their data history.
