# Battle Card: Hims & Hers

*Updated: 2026-03-12*

---

## Who They Are

Hims & Hers is a US-based public telehealth company ($2.35B FY2025 revenue, $128M net income) with 2.5M+ subscribers. Originally men's health (ED, hair loss), they've expanded to women's health, weight loss (GLP-1), and are planning a longevity specialty launch in 2026. They acquired a peptide manufacturing facility (Feb 2025) and ZAVA for European expansion (June 2025). The Hers brand alone is on pace for $1B+ revenue in 2026. They are the most well-capitalized competitor planning to enter Alche's exact category.

---

## Strengths (Honest)

- **Scale that dwarfs everyone:** $2.35B revenue, $2.7-2.9B guidance for 2026, 2.5M subscribers. Nobody else is close. [Data]
- **Profitable and public:** $128M net income, $318M EBITDA — self-funding growth. [Data]
- **85% subscriber retention:** Industry-leading retention rate. [Data]
- **Hers brand momentum:** Growing triple-digit YoY, $1B+ pace for 2026. [Data]
- **Vertically integrated longevity:** Owns peptide manufacturing, lab testing, telehealth — building end-to-end. [Data]
- **European infrastructure:** ZAVA acquisition (1.3M active customers) gives immediate EU/UK access. [Data]
- **Marketing machine:** $182M/quarter in marketing spend that returns profitably. 1,200+ influencer creators. [Data]
- **Brand normalization expertise:** They're experts at taking taboo health topics (ED, weight loss) and making them approachable — could do the same for longevity.

---

## Weaknesses (Specific)

- **Longevity offering doesn't exist yet:** Planned for 2026, no details on pricing, features, or differentiation. [Data]
- **Brand baggage:** Still strongly associated with ED pills and hair loss — "longevity" is a stretch for the brand. [Opinion]
- **No community or physical presence:** Pure telehealth model. No physical spaces, no community features, no local connection. [Data]
- **Medication-centric model:** Longevity launch based on peptides, GLP drugs, coenzymes — pharmaceutical approach, not lifestyle. [Data]
- **Marketing-dependent:** 48% of revenue spent on marketing. If customer acquisition costs rise, margins compress quickly. [Data]
- **Regulatory risk:** GLP-1 supply chain, peptide manufacturing, state-by-state telehealth regulations create vulnerability. [Assumption]
- **No science/research credibility:** No published studies, no scientific advisory board equivalent to ZOE or Alche's positioning. [Data gap]

---

## How to Win Against Them

1. **Community vs. telehealth:** Hims is a screen. Alche is a cafe, a community, a space. "You can't get longevity from a 15-minute telehealth call."

2. **Lifestyle vs. pharmaceuticals:** Hims' longevity = peptides and drugs. Alche's longevity = nutrition, movement, sleep, community, biomarkers, joy. "We believe the best longevity protocol is a great life, not more medication."

3. **Authenticity vs. commercialization:** Hims spent $6M promoting a single ad. Alche builds trust one relationship at a time. "We're not a marketing machine selling you longevity — we're a community living it."

4. **Women-first vs. men-adapted:** Hims started as men's health and is adding women. Alche centers women 30-55 from the start. "We didn't pivot to women — we were built for women."

5. **European native vs. American import:** Hims is acquiring European companies (ZAVA). Alche IS European. Different values, different healthcare culture, different community standards.

---

## When They Win Over You (Honest)

- When the customer wants medication-based longevity interventions (peptides, GLP-1, hormone therapy) — Alche doesn't prescribe.
- When the customer values brand familiarity and a proven platform with 2.5M subscribers.
- When scale and convenience matter more than community and depth.
- When the customer is in the US and wants a one-stop telehealth platform.
- When the Hers brand achieves genuine longevity positioning — their marketing capabilities are formidable.

---

## Customer Objections & Responses

**"Hims & Hers is already huge and launching longevity — how can Alche compete?"**
"Size isn't a moat. Forward Health raised $650M and collapsed. Modern Age raised $33M and failed. The question isn't who's bigger — it's who builds genuine community and continuous value. Hims sells prescriptions; Alche builds a lifestyle."

**"But Hims is already in Europe through ZAVA."**
"ZAVA is a prescription delivery service, not a longevity community. Having a pharmacy in your country isn't the same as having a cafe in your neighborhood."

**"Their Hers brand is growing 100%+ YoY."**
"Growing fast at marketing isn't the same as building something meaningful. Ask yourself: does Hers have a community? A place you'd go? A relationship with you beyond the next prescription?"

---

## Key Vulnerability to Exploit

**No physical presence, no community, no lifestyle.** Hims is a transactional telehealth platform scaling into longevity via medication. They have no community infrastructure, no physical spaces, no relationship depth. Every failed longevity startup (Forward, Modern Age, Ever/Body) teaches that technology alone doesn't retain customers in health — human connection does. This is exactly what Hims cannot build quickly.

---

## Churn Signals (Why Their Customers Leave)

- GLP-1 customers may leave if drug access/pricing changes [Assumption]
- Telehealth fatigue — "15-minute video call doesn't feel like healthcare" [Opinion]
- Brand perception mismatch — "I don't want my longevity platform from an ED pill company" [Assumption]
- ZAVA integration friction in Europe (different regulations, different UX expectations) [Assumption]
- When medications stop/change, no ongoing community or lifestyle reason to stay [Assumption]

**Note:** Hims has 85% retention, so churn signals are harder to identify. Most churn data relates to GLP-1 specific programs.
