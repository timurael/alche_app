## Key Themes from Reddit & Online Forums

### Theme 1: "What Do You Actually Use?"

The longevity community is fragmented across many tools. Common stacks mentioned:
- **Wearables:** OURA ring, WHOOP, Apple Watch, Garmin
- **Supplements:** NMN, resveratrol, spermidine, Vitamin D, Omega-3, creatine
- **Pharmaceuticals:** Metformin (off-label), rapamycin (off-label)
- **Testing:** Function Health, InsideTracker, Marek Health
- **Cold/heat:** Cold plunges, sauna protocols (Huberman-influenced)
- **CGMs:** Levels, Dexcom Stelo (declining enthusiasm — "too inconsistent to be informative")
- **Biological age tests:** TruAge, GlycanAge, MoleQlar

**Key insight:** No single platform integrates all of these. Users cobble together 4-7 tools. This is the integration opportunity.

### Theme 2: "Is X Worth It?"

Heavy price-sensitivity discussions:
- AG1: "Is AG1 worth $99/month?" — consistently debated, split opinion
- Function Health: "Is $365/year worth it vs. asking your doctor?" — most say yes for breadth
- OURA: "Is the subscription worth it?" — growing resentment
- Seed: "Is Seed better than $15 probiotics from Amazon?" — many say no difference
- ZOE: "After the initial test, is the subscription worth keeping?" — most say no

**Key insight:** The "is it worth it?" question dominates. Value must be continuously demonstrated, not just at onboarding.

### Theme 4: What People Wish Existed

Recurring requests from forum discussions:
- **"An app that connects all my health data in one place"** — wearable + labs + supplements
- **"A longevity community that isn't bro-science"** — women and non-biohackers feel excluded
- **"Affordable testing in Europe"** — Function Health US-only, Neko too expensive
- **"Someone to actually explain my results"** — not AI, not a data dashboard
- **"A less extreme version of Bryan Johnson's protocol"** — rigorous but realistic
- **"Something for women going through perimenopause"** — longevity specifically for hormonal transitions
- **"Community that isn't just online"** — desire for physical meetups, shared experiences

**Key insight:** These wish-list items map almost perfectly to Alche's positioning.

### Theme 5: Longevity as Identity vs. Longevity as Lifestyle

Clear cultural split in communities:
- **Identity camp:** Bryan Johnson devotees, protocol-followers, n=1 experimenters, "quantified self" adherents. Male-dominated, tech-adjacent, Silicon Valley-coded.
- **Lifestyle camp:** People who want to age well, feel good, have energy. More female, less dogmatic, more open to community and imperfect approaches. Often dismissed by identity camp as "not serious."

**Key insight:** Alche's "Imperfect Longevity" positioning directly serves the lifestyle camp — which is larger but underserved.

### Theme 6: Trust & Credibility Signals

What builds trust in this community:
- Published peer-reviewed research (not company-sponsored)
- Transparent ingredient lists and dosages
- Real user before/after biomarker data
- Founder/team credentials in science or medicine
- Honest about limitations and what doesn't work
- Long-term user testimonials (not influencer partnerships)

What destroys trust:
- Proprietary blends that hide dosages (AG1)
- Company-sponsored-only research
- Influencer-heavy marketing without substance
- Aggressive subscription tactics
- AI-only customer service
- Celebrity endorsements without scientific backing

### Theme 7: European Longevity Discussion
