**Status:** PROPOSAL — awaiting founder decision

---

## 1. Current State Assessment

The deck currently pitches Alche as a **subscription-first digital platform** with tiers at **EUR 0 / 19 / 49 / 99**, projecting subscription revenue growing from **52% to 68%** of total by Year 3 and chasing SaaS-adjacent multiples of **8-15x ARR**. This model requires converting 4.5% of free users to paid digital subscriptions in a market where **60-75% of German consumers switch brands for a better price** and the median health/wellness app conversion rate is far lower. The research overwhelmingly shows that free app + product commerce outperforms subscription-gated models in this category: AG1 does **$600M** on product revenue with free content, Care/of raised **$84M** on a subscription-first model and is now dead. The deck needs to be rebuilt around retail-first economics.

## 2. Option A: Pure Retail Commerce

**Model:** No membership. No subscription. 100% of revenue from product sales (own-brand + curated third-party). App is free. Science content is free. AI concierge is free. Everything is free except the products you buy.

### Revenue Composition

| Stream | Year 1 | Year 3 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Curated third-party products | 60% | 35% |
| Own-brand products | 25% | 55% |
| Physical space (services + events) | 15% | 10% |

### Margin Structure

| Product Type | Gross Margin |
|-------------|-------------|
| Own-brand supplements (DTC) | **60-80%** |
| Curated third-party (wholesale) | **20-50%** |
| Curated third-party (dropship) | **15-40%** |
| Blended (base case) | **55%** |

### How It Works

Free app delivers wellness protocols, tracking, community, and AI-powered product recommendations. Every recommendation links to a purchase. Subscribe-and-save creates recurring product revenue without a separate membership SKU. Curated products build trust and breadth in Year 1; own-brand captures margin as the brand matures.

### Comps

- **The Ordinary:** $700M in revenue from transparent, affordable product retail. No subscription. Education IS the sales engine.
- **AG1:** $600M/year from a single DTC product. Free podcasts and content spend ($2.2M/mo) drive all acquisition.
- **Goop:** ~$100M. Free editorial content funds a product commerce engine. Content is "not a media business model."

### Trade-offs

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Zero friction to purchase. No membership gatekeeping. | No recurring committed revenue stream outside subscribe-and-save |
| Simplest model to execute. Ship products, sell products. | Investor story is harder: **1-2x revenue multiple** for pure e-commerce |
| Every user is a potential buyer from Day 1 | LTV depends entirely on reorder behavior |
| Lowest CAC: no need to sell a subscription concept | No sunk cost psychology driving engagement |

## 3. Option B: Thin Membership + Retail (Costco Model)

**Model:** EUR 5-9/mo membership unlocks better pricing, early access, and community features. Non-members can still browse and buy at full price. Membership is the profit center. Products are the revenue engine.

### Revenue Composition

| Stream | Year 1 | Year 3 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Curated third-party products | 45% | 30% |
| Own-brand products | 20% | 40% |
| Membership (EUR 5-9/mo) | 15% | 15% |
| Physical space (services + events) | 15% | 10% |
| Premium tier (digital twin, CGM, doctor) | 5% | 5% |

### Margin Structure

| Stream | Gross Margin |
|--------|-------------|
| Membership | **~95%** (pure software/access) |
| Own-brand products | **60-80%** |
| Curated third-party | **20-50%** |
| Blended gross margin | **55-60%** |

### How It Works

Free tier: full access to content, community browsing, product discovery, basic tracking. Membership (EUR 5-9/mo): unlocks **10-20% subscribe-and-save discounts**, priority access to drops, community features, and the full wellness dashboard. The membership fee is nearly pure profit and funds operations. Products sell at competitive prices because membership creates the margin buffer. Optional premium tier (EUR 19-29/mo) adds digital twin, CGM integration, and doctor reviews for the biohacker segment.

### Comps

- **Costco:** Membership is **1.9% of revenue** but **73% of gross profit**. Renewal rate: **92.9%**. The fee is small, the commitment is total.
- **Thrive Market:** **$5-12/mo** membership, **$700M+** revenue. Members spend **$437/yr** in product revenue.
- **Amazon Prime:** Members spend **2.3x more** ($1,400/yr vs $600/yr). Membership changes buying behavior through sunk cost psychology.

### Trade-offs

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Membership = predictable, high-margin revenue from Day 1 | Adds a conversion step before product purchases at best pricing |
| Sunk cost psychology: members **spend 2.3x more** (Prime data) | German price sensitivity: 60-75% switch for a better deal. Will they pay EUR 7/mo to save 15%? Needs validation. |
| Investor story is stronger: recurring revenue + commerce = **3-5x multiple** | Two things to sell: the membership AND the products |
| Costco's 92.9% renewal rate proves the model at scale | Requires critical mass of product catalog to justify membership value |
| Small fee = disproportionate commitment (behavioral economics verified) | Non-members browsing at full price may bounce to competitors |

## 4. Option C: Freemium App + Retail + Premium Tier

**Model:** Free app for everyone. Retail revenue from products (own-brand + curated). Premium tier (EUR 19-29/mo) for power users who want digital twin, CGM integration, blood panel credits, and doctor reviews. Three independent revenue streams.

### Revenue Composition

| Stream | Year 1 | Year 3 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Curated third-party products | 45% | 30% |
| Own-brand products | 20% | 35% |
| Premium tier (EUR 19-29/mo) | 10% | 15% |
| Physical space (services + events) | 15% | 10% |
| Subscribe-and-save recurring product revenue | 10% | 10% |

### Margin Structure

| Stream | Gross Margin |
|--------|-------------|
| Premium tier | **85-90%** (software) |
| Own-brand products | **60-80%** |
| Curated third-party | **20-50%** |
| Blended gross margin | **50-55%** |

### How It Works

The free app delivers the full content experience, basic wellness tracking, AI concierge, community, and product discovery. No paywall on the core experience. Products are available to everyone at the same price. Subscribe-and-save offers 10-15% discounts on recurring orders for any user. The premium tier (EUR 19-29/mo) is a separate, clearly differentiated offering for users who want quantified-self features: digital twin modeling, CGM data integration, biological age scoring, blood panel credits, and periodic doctor reviews.

### Comps

- **Sephora:** Free app drives **80% of North American e-commerce sales**. Loyalty program (free) creates engagement. Premium services (paid) layer on top.
- **Noom:** Freemium health app + paid coaching tier. Proved willingness to pay for personalized health guidance.
- **Xiaohongshu:** Free content, community, and commerce. **$4.8B revenue**, **$31B valuation**. 300M+ MAU. "Xiaohongshu for longevity in Europe" is the investor pitch.

### Trade-offs

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| No friction for product purchases. No membership barrier. | Premium tier conversion will be small: **2-5%** of active users at best |
| Premium tier preserves optionality for the SaaS-adjacent investor narrative | Three revenue streams = three things to optimize. Complexity tax on a pre-seed team. |
| Digital twin / CGM features are genuinely premium and defensible | Premium tier won't move the needle on revenue until V2 (Month 10+), per the roadmap |
| Free app maximizes top-of-funnel for product sales | Blended margin is lower because premium tier revenue (highest margin) is smallest bucket |
| Clean separation: products for everyone, data tools for biohackers | Risk of being "good at three things, great at nothing" early on |

## 5. My Recommendation

**Option B: Thin Membership + Retail**, with Option C's premium tier layered on top as a Phase 2 upsell.

Here is why:

**The behavioral economics are proven.** Costco's membership is 1.9% of revenue but 73% of gross profit. Amazon Prime members spend **2.3x more**. Thrive Market generates **$437/year** per member on a $5-12/mo membership. These are not projections -- they are verified patterns across multiple categories and decades.

**A EUR 7/mo membership solves three problems at once:**
1. **Predictable revenue from Day 1.** Even 200 members at EUR 7/mo = EUR 16,800/yr in near-pure-profit recurring revenue. That is not the engine, but it funds operations while the product flywheel spins up.
2. **Changes buying behavior.** The sunk cost effect is strongest for experience goods (wellness = exactly this). Members who pay a small fee feel compelled to use their "account" -- which means more product purchases, higher reorder rates, and better LTV.
3. **Investor narrative.** Commerce + membership + AI commands a **20-86% premium** over traditional retail multiples. You are not pitching 1-2x e-commerce. You are pitching **3-5x** recurring revenue + commerce, with a credible path to SaaS-adjacent multiples as the premium tier scales.

**The German risk is manageable.** Yes, 60-75% switch for better pricing. But the membership IS better pricing. EUR 7/mo to save 15% on subscribe-and-save means the membership pays for itself on a single **EUR 47/mo product order**. At an AOV of EUR 70, the value proposition is immediately clear.

**Why not pure retail (Option A)?** Because you lose the behavioral lock-in and the investor narrative. You are a pre-seed company. You need every advantage.

**Why not triple-stream (Option C) from Day 1?** Because the premium tier features (digital twin, CGM, doctor reviews) are not shipping until Month 10+. Selling a premium tier you cannot deliver yet is a liability. Add it in Phase 2 when the product is ready.

## 6. Pricing Architecture (Recommended Model)

### Membership

| Tier | Price | What You Get |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **Free** | EUR 0 | Full content library, community browsing, product discovery, basic wellness tracking, AI concierge |
| **Member** | **EUR 7/mo** (EUR 69/yr annual) | **15% subscribe-and-save discount**, full wellness dashboard, wearable sync, protocols, priority access to drops, community features, member-only events |

### Premium Tier (Phase 2, Month 10+)

| Tier | Price | What You Get |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **Premium** | **EUR 29/mo** | Everything in Member + digital twin, biological age score, CGM integration, blood panel credits (1/quarter), doctor review (1/quarter) |

### Product Pricing

| Category | Price Range | Margin |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Own-brand supplements (capsules, powders) | **EUR 25-35** | 60-80% |
| Curated third-party supplements | **EUR 18-55** | 20-50% |
| Bundles (2-3 products, curated protocol) | **EUR 55-80** | 40-60% blended |
| Starter kit (own-brand + curated + welcome protocol) | **EUR 89** | 50-60% blended |

### Subscribe-and-Save

| Discount | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **5%** off | Any user, any recurring order |
| **15%** off | Members, any recurring order |
| **20%** off | Members, 3+ product subscription bundle |

### Bundles

| Bundle | Contents | Price | Saves |
|--------|---------|-------|-------|
| Starter Protocol | 1 own-brand + 1 curated + digital protocol card | EUR 49 | ~15% vs individual |
| Complete Stack | 3 products (personalized by AI) + protocol | EUR 79 | ~20% vs individual |
| Quarterly Box | 4 products + seasonal protocol, auto-shipped | EUR 99/quarter | ~25% vs individual, member-only |
