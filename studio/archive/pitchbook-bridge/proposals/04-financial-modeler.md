## 1. Current State Assessment

The deck's financial slides (19-21) are built on a **SaaS-first model**. Here's what they claim:

| Current Deck Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Revenue engine | Subscription tiers (€0/19/49/99) |
| Subscription share of revenue | 52% (Y1) → 68% (Y3) |
| Weighted ARPU | €49/mo |
| Break-even | M12, 145 paying subscribers |
| LTV:CAC (conservative) | 5.1x |
| LTV:CAC (realistic) | 14.7x |
| Blended gross margin | 72% → 80% |
| Target multiple | SaaS-adjacent (8-15x ARR) |
| Raise | €500K = 24-month runway |

**The problem:** With a retail-first pivot, **every single number above changes.** Margins drop from 85-90% (software) to 30-70% (physical product). Revenue comes from transactions, not recurring seats. Break-even at 145 subscribers is meaningless — you now need hundreds of active purchasing customers. The entire unit economics story must be rebuilt from scratch.

**The opportunity:** The research validates that this model works — AG1, Ritual, Goop all prove DTC wellness commerce at 60-80% own-brand margins. The numbers are different but they are **fundable numbers**, especially with the content-to-commerce flywheel story. You just need them to be correct and defensible.

### 2.4 LTV Calculation

**Formula:** LTV = AOV x Orders/Year x Avg. Customer Lifespan (years) x Gross Margin

| Scenario | AOV | Orders/Yr | Lifespan | Gross Margin | LTV | Confidence |

| **Optimistic** | €90 | 9 | 2.5 yrs | 62% | **€1,255** | LOW |

> Key retention data backing these estimates:
> - 50.3% of repeat purchases happen within 30 days (VERIFIED)
> - 76.4% within 90 days (VERIFIED)
> - Subscriber retention: 55-67% annually vs. 18-31% for one-time buyers (VERIFIED)
> - Subscribe-and-save customers are the engine — they underpin the base and optimistic cases

### 2.5 LTV:CAC Ratio

| Scenario | LTV | Blended CAC | LTV:CAC | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## 3. Option A: Conservative Model (Curated-Only)

**Thesis:** Launch with curated third-party products only. No own-brand investment. Lower margin, lower risk, fastest to market. Test demand, build customer base, introduce own-brand later with validated data.

### Assumptions

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Own-brand investment | €0 |
| Product margin (curated only) | 22-30% (improves with wholesale terms) |
| Blended gross margin (incl. membership + space) | 28-35% |
| AOV | €60 → €70 (grows with trust) |
| Orders/customer/year | 4 (conservative reorder) |
| CAC (blended) | €25 |
| Time to first product sale | M2 (fast — no formulation needed) |

### Projections

| Metric | M6 | M12 | M18 | M24 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Active customers** | 300 | 800 | 1,500 | 3,000 |
| Monthly orders | 100 | 267 | 500 | 1,000 |
| Product AOV | €60 | €65 | €68 | €70 |
| **Product GMV** | €6,000 | €17,333 | €34,000 | €70,000 |
| Product gross margin | 22% | 26% | 28% | 30% |
| Product gross profit | €1,320 | €4,507 | €9,520 | €21,000 |
| Membership revenue (8% x €7/mo) | €168 | €448 | €840 | €1,680 |
| Physical space gross profit | €2,750 | €4,400 | €5,500 | €6,600 |
| **Total monthly gross profit** | **€4,238** | **€9,355** | **€15,860** | **€29,280** |
| Monthly OpEx | €16,000 | €19,000 | €21,000 | €24,000 |

> **Math check (M12):** 800 customers x (4 orders/yr / 12) = 267 orders/mo. 267 x €65 = €17,333 GMV. At 26% margin = €4,507 product gross profit. Add membership (€448) + space (€4,400) = €9,355 total. OpEx €19K → net -€9,645/mo.

**Verdict:** Viable but slow. Thin margins mean break-even takes nearly the full runway. Buffer is uncomfortably small. Risk: if customer acquisition is slower than projected, you run out of cash before break-even.

## 9. My Recommendation

### Lead with Option B (Curated + 2-3 Own-Brand SKUs) in the Deck

**Three reasons:**

**1. It's the only option that survives a stress test.** Option A dies if growth is 30% slower than projected. Option C has zero budget buffer. Option B breaks even at M16-17 with €250K still in the bank — that's a 12-month buffer at minimum burn. Investors back companies that can survive their own mistakes.

**2. It tells the right margin story.** Option B shows margin improvement over time: 28% at M6 → 40% at M12 → 55% at M24. This is the "expanding margin" narrative investors love — it means the business gets better as it scales. Option A is flat at 30% forever (boring). Option C starts high but requires the investor to believe you can execute a 5-product launch as a 2-person team (they won't).

**3. It preserves optionality.** If own-brand products sell well (they will — the data says 60-80% margin is standard), you accelerate into Option C using revenue. If they don't, you haven't bet the company on it. Present Option B as the plan and Option C as "where we go with traction." Investors hear: "These founders are disciplined but ambitious."

### How to Present This in the Deck

**Slide 19 (replace current):** Lead with the new unit economics — AOV €70, blended margin 52%, LTV:CAC 4.4:1 conservative. Show the COGS breakdown for own-brand (investors love seeing you understand your cost structure).

**Slide 20 (replace current):** Show the revenue ramp — M6/M12/M18/M24 GMV trajectory. Highlight the margin expansion from 28% → 55% as own-brand share grows.

**Slide 21 (replace current):** Break-even at M16-17 with €250K buffer. Show the sensitivity analysis — "even at 30% slower growth, we break even at M21 with €175K remaining." This is the confidence slide.

### One Number to Memorize

**€42,000/month.** That's break-even revenue. At a €70 AOV and 5 orders/customer/year, you need **~1,100 active customers**. That's the number you're building toward. Every metric in the deck should trace back to this.
