## Options

- [ ] **Option A: Conservative (curated-only)** — Break-even: M22-23, active customers needed: 2,600, monthly revenue at break-even: €82K. Lowest risk, thinnest margins (30%), dangerously thin buffer (€13K). Does NOT survive 30% growth slowdown.
- [ ] **Option B: Base (curated + 2-3 own-brand SKUs)** — Break-even: M16-17, active customers needed: 1,100, monthly revenue at break-even: €42K. Margin expands 28% → 55% over time. €250K buffer at break-even. Survives stress test.
- [ ] **Option C: Aggressive (own-brand focus, 5+ SKUs)** — Break-even: M14-15, active customers needed: 850, monthly revenue at break-even: €35K. Best margins (64% at M24), fastest break-even, but €0 budget buffer. May require raising €550-600K instead of €500K.

## Sub-Decisions

### Own-Brand Investment
- [ ] **€0** (Option A — curated only, introduce own-brand later from revenue)
- [ ] **€25,000** (Option B — 2-3 SKUs, private label, EU partner)
- [ ] **€45,000** (Option C — 5+ SKUs, full own-brand line from launch)

### Inventory Model
- [ ] **Dropship only** — lowest risk, lowest margin (~15%), limited brand control
- [ ] **Wholesale + warehouse (3PL)** — better margin (~25-30%), requires inventory investment
- [ ] **Hybrid** — dropship for long-tail, wholesale for top sellers + own-brand (RECOMMENDED)

### First Own-Brand Products (if Option B or C)
- [ ] **Supplement powders** — higher AOV (€35-45), more complex formulation, bigger differentiation
- [ ] **Capsules** — simplest to manufacture, lowest MOQ, fastest to market
- [ ] **Functional blends** — unique positioning, harder to comparison-shop, best margin protection
- [ ] **Start with capsules, expand to powders at M8-10** (RECOMMENDED — de-risks the timeline)

### Fulfillment Partner
- [ ] **FLEX Logistik** — supplement-specialized, German-based, best for compliance
- [ ] **byrd** — strong pan-European network, good for expansion beyond DE
- [ ] **ShipBob** — US-headquartered, European warehouse, strongest tech integration
- [ ] **Self-fulfill initially** — lowest cost at <100 orders/mo, switch to 3PL at scale

### Financial Metric to Lead With in Deck
- [ ] **LTV:CAC ratio (4.4:1 conservative)** — investors' most-watched efficiency metric
- [ ] **Gross margin trajectory (28% → 55%)** — expanding margin = improving business quality
- [ ] **Break-even timeline (M16-17 with €250K buffer)** — capital efficiency and survivability
- [ ] **GMV trajectory (€7.5K → €96K/mo)** — pure growth story
- [ ] **Lead with LTV:CAC, support with margin trajectory** (RECOMMENDED)

### Raise Amount
- [ ] **€500K** — sufficient for Options A and B, tight for Option C
- [ ] **€550-600K** — comfortable for all options including C, adds meaningful buffer
- [ ] **Keep €500K in deck but accept up to €600K if oversubscribed**

---

## Specialist Recommendation: Option B (Curated + 2-3 Own-Brand SKUs)

**Rationale:** Option B is the only model that (a) breaks even within 18 months, (b) survives a 30% growth slowdown with cash to spare, and (c) shows investors an expanding margin trajectory — the hallmark of a commerce business getting stronger as it scales. It preserves the optionality to accelerate into Option C using early revenue if own-brand products outperform, while not betting the entire raise on product development execution before you have a single customer.
