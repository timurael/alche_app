# Alche Pitch Book — Open Decisions

> Track pivotal decisions for the revenue model shift. Mark resolved with date.

---

## DECIDED

- **App is fully free.** No subscription gate on core value. (2026-04-14)
- **Revenue comes from retail.** Curated products, own-brand, third-party. (2026-04-14)
- **Research-backed curation is the trust engine.** Every product recommendation backed by studies. (2026-04-14)

## OPEN

### 1. Premium Tier — What Gets Paywalled?
**Status:** undecided
**Options:**
- A) Digital twin extended (bio age score, predictive model, advanced insights)
- B) CGM integration + doctor review loop
- C) 1:1 protocol reviews with medical advisor
- D) Combination — tiered premium features
- E) No paywall at all — pure retail revenue

**Notes:** The premium tier is gravy, not the engine. Keep it optional. Whatever we paywall must feel like a genuine extension, not a feature gate on the free experience.

**Decide by:** before final deck revision

---

### 2. Own-Brand vs Curated Third-Party Split
**Status:** undecided
**Options:**
- A) Launch curated-only (faster, lower capital, editorial authority)
- B) Launch with 2-3 own-brand hero products + curated shelf
- C) Own-brand first, curated later

**Implications:** Own-brand = higher margin (65-75%) but needs capital + formulation + compliance. Curated = faster to market (50-70% margin on wholesale) and builds trust through editorial independence.

**Decide by:** before financial model finalized

---

### 3. Physical Space Role in New Model
**Status:** undecided
**Options:**
- A) Retail + experience node (LED sessions, smoothie bar, product shelf) — stays as planned
- B) Digital-first, physical space deferred to Phase 2
- C) Pop-up model instead of permanent lease

**Notes:** Physical space was already Phase 2 in the deck. With retail-first revenue model, it becomes a showroom + experience center. Question is timing and capital allocation.

**Decide by:** before investor conversations

---

### 4. Pricing Architecture for Products
**Status:** undecided
**Options:**
- A) Standard retail markup (research justifies premium pricing)
- B) "Costco for wellness" — membership unlocks better pricing on products
- C) Transparent margin model (show cost + alche markup)
- D) Bundle pricing (protocol-matched product kits at a discount)

**Notes:** Timu's instinct: find great deals for the customer. This suggests competitive or transparent pricing, not premium markup. Research needed on which model drives highest reorder rate.

**Decide by:** before business model slide rewrite

---

### 5. Fulfillment Model
**Status:** undecided
**Options:**
- A) Dropship from third-party brands (lowest capital, lowest margin)
- B) Wholesale + own fulfillment (higher margin, needs warehouse/3PL)
- C) Hybrid — own-brand fulfilled by us, curated products dropshipped
- D) Marketplace model (brands sell through alche, we take commission)

**Decide by:** before financial projections
