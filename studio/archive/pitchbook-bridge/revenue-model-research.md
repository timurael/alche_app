# Alche Revenue Model Research — Full Synthesis

> **Date:** 2026-04-14
> **Purpose:** Validate the pivot from subscription-first to retail-first revenue model
> **Verdict:** VALIDATED. The data is overwhelming.

---

## THE THESIS

**Old model:** Subscription-first digital platform. App tiers at €0/19/49/99. Subscription = 52→68% of revenue. Chasing SaaS multiples (8-15x ARR).

**New model:** App = free value delivery engine. Revenue from curated retail — own-brand products + vetted third-party supplements. Thin premium tier (€5-9/mo) for advanced features (digital twin, CGM, doctor review) as gravy, not the engine.

**One sentence:** Alche is an editorial commerce platform — the intelligence is free, the products pay the bills.

### 5 Patterns That Emerged

1. **Free app = sales engine, not the product.** Sephora's free app drives 80% of North American sales. AG1 spends $2.2M/mo on free content to sell $600M in products. Goop explicitly says content is "not a media business model."

2. **Transparency is the moat.** The Ordinary → $700M by disclosing ingredients. Ritual → 92% repeat by showing supply chain. 78% of consumers favor transparent brands (2024).

3. **Own-brand is where margin lives.** Thrive Market: 25% of revenue from private label at higher margins. Ritual: ~70% gross margin. Pattern: curate third-party first (builds trust) → introduce own-brand (captures margin).

4. **Subscription = retention mechanism, not paywall.** AG1's $79/mo product subscription creates predictable revenue. Care/of died because subscription was the whole identity.

5. **European white space is massive.** iHerb does 82% of revenue internationally. AG1, Ritual, Thrive Market are all US-first with minimal EU presence. No European brand combines science content + curated commerce + community.

### 5 Design Principles for the Funnel

1. **Value first, commerce second.** Deliver wellness value before asking for a purchase. Gating after value converts 2-3x better.
2. **Research IS the product.** Wirecutter drove $1B in commerce from editorial trust. The Ordinary → $460M from transparency. Science content isn't marketing — it's the sales engine.
3. **AI personalization is the margin lever.** 73% feel overwhelmed. AI guided shopping converts 4x better. Build a concierge, not a catalog.
4. **Subscription = retention, not the model.** Subscribers retain 55-67% annually vs 18-31% for one-time. But lead with genuine recommendation, then offer subscribe-and-save.
5. **The bridge must be invisible.** Research → protocol → product = one continuous thread. "Here's what your body needs, here's the science, it arrives Thursday."

### Regulatory — Must-Knows

- Supplements = legally classified as **foods**, not medicines
- BVL notification per product (not approval — just registration)
- **No license required** to sell supplements online in Germany
- All labels must be **in German** and include "Nahrungsergänzungsmittel"
- Health claims must use **verbatim EU-approved wording** only (~260 approved claims)
- Penalties: up to EUR 50,000 per LFGB violation
- Ashwagandha is on a **watchlist** — regulatory risk for products containing it
- Solid supplements (capsules, powders) = **7% VAT**; liquids = 19% VAT

### Consumer Behavior

- **83% of Germans trust their pharmacist** — #1 trust driver
- **60-75% will switch brands** for a better price
- Only **16-22%** will pay a green/ethical premium (and declining)
- **32%** of current users planning to cut supplement spending
- Curation premium is viable but must be justified by **tangible value** (quality verification, personalization, convenience, access to unavailable products)

## 7. WARNINGS & RISKS

### Care/of Died — Why
Raised $84M, acquired by Bayer at $225M, shut down June 2024. Personalization was the brand — when they went to Target, the story evaporated. **Lesson:** Personalization is a feature, not a business model. Brand authority must survive channel expansion.

### German Price Sensitivity
60-75% will switch brands for a better deal. Only 16-22% pay ethical premiums. Curation must deliver **tangible value** — not just aesthetics. Research-backed recommendations, verified quality, personalization from real health data.

### Regulatory Risk
- Ashwagandha on watchlist — products containing it carry regulatory risk
- EU health claims are strict: ~260 approved, verbatim only
- "On-hold" botanical claims are legally unstable
- All marketing copy must be reviewed for compliance

### DTC Food/Supplement Graveyard
Daily Harvest (-86% from peak), Sun Basket (-93%). Cold-chain logistics + high CAC + churn = death. **Don't be the logistics company.** Curate, don't warehouse perishables.
